# OnEMI Technology Solutions Limited (brand: Kissht)

**One-liner:** A technology-first digital NBFC (brand: Kissht) focused on unsecured personal loans and LAP for mass-market/mass-affluent borrowers; listed on NSE & BSE in May 2026 with ₹7,066 Cr AUM and 75% PAT growth.

---

## Current State

*As of Q4 FY26 (March 2026)*

OnEMI listed on NSE & BSE on May 8, 2026, pricing its ₹850 Cr fresh issue at ₹162–171/share and listing at ₹191 (+11.7% premium, 9.9x subscribed; QIBs 25.9x). FY26 was its first year as a public company. AUM grew 73% YoY to ₹7,066 Cr, PAT grew 75% to ₹281 Cr, ROA 5.05%, ROE 24.0%.

Asset quality improved materially through FY25 stress — GNPA fell from 2.89% (Mar-25) to 2.12% (Mar-26), 77 bps YoY improvement. Collection efficiency (DPD 30) at 97.15%. Stage 1 portfolio share recovered from 93.6% to 95.5%.

The business model is half on-book (50.3%) and half off-book via co-lending/DA (49.7%) — asset-light by design. On-book NBFC (Si Creva Capital) is A-/Stable rated. Borrowing cost: 14.16% on-book; 45+ lending partners.

Products: (1) Personal Loans up to ₹5L, up to 5 years — 92.7% of AUM; (2) LAP up to ₹15L, up to 10 years, 98 branches across 8 states — 7.3% and growing fast (1.8% a year ago).

Customer base: 11.76 Mn cumulative, 3.25 Mn active. Profile: 81% top-100-cities, 48% salaried, 73% under 35, median CIBIL 746. AI/ML underwriting: 7,000+ variables, 68.55 Mn registered app users.

## Timeline

- **2016**: Incorporated; registered Si Creva Capital Services as NBFC with RBI.
- **2022–2023**: Scaled off-book lending partnerships; expanded merchant PoS and e-commerce integrations.
- **2024**: Launched LAP product; onboarded Sachin Tendulkar as strategic investor & brand ambassador.
- **FY25**: Navigated digital lending sector stress (overleverage cycle); GNPA rose to 2.89% by Mar-25 but collection efficiency held.
- **FY26**: AUM +73%, PAT +75%; GNPA improved 77 bps YoY. Classified as "Middle Layer" NBFC under RBI Scale-Based Regulations.
- **May 8, 2026**: Listed on NSE & BSE. IPO: ₹850 Cr fresh issue + OFS. Listing at ₹191 vs ₹162–171 price band, 9.9x subscribed (QIBs 25.9x).

## Thesis / View

Kissht is one of the cleaner survivors of the FY25 digital lending stress cycle — the GNPA improvement (2.89% → 2.12%) and rising Stage 1 share (93.6% → 95.5%) are genuine underwriting alpha signals, not just portfolio run-off. The AI/ML stack (7,000+ variables) and disciplined collections model appear to differentiate it from peers who leaned on loose underwriting during the boom.

The business model is structurally asset-light: ~50% off-book keeps capital consumption manageable at this stage. The LAP ramp (1.8% → 7.3% in one year) adds secured collateral diversification and longer-tenure cohorts that compound ROA over time.

ROE at 24% and ROA at 5%+ are strong for a ₹7,000 Cr AUM NBFC. The concern is the top-line cost base: opex/NTI at ~60% (operating expenses ₹324 Cr vs NTI ₹548 Cr in Q4 FY26) — there's meaningful operating leverage left to capture as AUM scales, and management has guided opex/AUM to decline.

**Key risks:**
- Cost of borrowings (14.16%) is high for A-rated — meaningful benefit to unlock as liability profile matures and relationship with institutional lenders deepens post-IPO.
- PAT growth has slowed sequentially (Q4 +6.6% QoQ, PPOP -0.6% QoQ) — opex is rising faster than income on a quarterly basis; needs monitoring over 2–3 quarters.
- Digital lending sector still in regulatory watchlist territory (RBI scale-based regulation, FLDG norms, co-lending guidelines) — policy risk is a structural overhang.

---

## Contradictions

None identified in this quarter's presentation. NNPA is slightly up YoY (+4 bps to 0.29% despite GNPA improving) — possibly explained by lower PCR (86.15% vs 91.48% a year ago). Monitor whether PCR compression creates future provision catch-up.

# Swiggy Limited

**One-liner:** India's #2 food delivery and quick commerce platform (behind Eternal/Zomato), with 25.2M monthly transacting users across food delivery, Instamart (quick commerce), and Dineout (out-of-home dining).

---

## Current State

*As of Q4 FY26 (quarter ending March 31, 2026)*

Swiggy is at a pivotal moment: food delivery is profitable and accelerating; Instamart (quick commerce) is losing money but improving unit economics rapidly; Dineout is profitable and growing steadily. The company is on a glide path toward consolidated profitability but has not yet reached group-level positive Adj EBITDA.

**Q4 FY26 Platform highlights:**
- Platform MTU: **25.2M** (+27.2% YoY, +3.8% QoQ)
- Consolidated Adj Revenue: ₹6,665 Cr (+41.3% YoY, +3.6% QoQ)
- Consolidated Adj EBITDA: −₹652 Cr (improved from −₹712 Cr Q3; improvement ₹60 Cr QoQ)
- B2C Adj EBITDA margin: −3.0% of GOV (+181 bps YoY, +43 bps QoQ)
- Total B2C GOV: ₹18,131 Cr (+40.7% YoY, flat QoQ — note: Q4 seasonally weak for quick commerce)

**Food Delivery:**
- GOV: ₹9,005 Cr (+22.6% YoY) — **15-quarter high**; first time exceeding prior cycle peak growth
- MTU: 18.3M (+21% YoY, +0.2M QoQ)
- Adj EBITDA: ₹297 Cr (3.3% of GOV) — **best ever margin** (+26 bps QoQ, +41 bps YoY)
- Growth driven by volume (orders and users), not AOV inflation — management considers this more durable
- Sub-platforms: Bolt (fast delivery), One BLCK (premium), 99-Store (value), Eat Right (healthy)
- Snacc (quick food) proactively shut down during Q4 — *"not convinced on steady-state size of the category for the incremental complexity"*

**Quick Commerce (Instamart):**
- GOV: ₹7,881 Cr (+68.8% YoY); NOV: ₹5,675 Cr (+60.3% YoY, +4% QoQ)
- AOV: ₹700 (+33% YoY) — driven by non-grocery mix and larger-basket buying
- Darkstores: **1,143 stores** across **129 cities** (total area: 4.8M sq ft, +21.1% YoY; +7 net Q4)
- Contribution margin: −1.8% of GOV in Q4; improved to **−1.1% in March 2026** (month-end run rate)
- Adj EBITDA: −₹858 Cr (−10.9% margin; improved 55 bps QoQ; losses reduced ₹50 Cr QoQ)
- Strategy: **differentiation over price/value** — investing in premium/clean food (Noice brand) and category upgrades, not discounting war
- Noice (own clean-label brand): high-protein eggs, preservative-free bread, Triply cookware category expansion
- Target: CM breakeven as the unlock for profit-driven growth

**Out-of-Home (Dineout):**
- GOV: ₹1,245 Cr (+43% YoY)
- Monthly active restaurant partners: 52k (+9% QoQ)
- Adj EBITDA margin: 0.8% — **profitable** and expanding
- GIRF (Great Indian Restaurant Festival) — flagship event driving ad monetisation

**Social impact:**
- 100,000 delivery partners registered on E-Shram — accident, health, life insurance, pension access

## Thesis / View

**Swiggy is the most credible challenger to Eternal's dominance**, but structurally behind on quick commerce: 1,143 stores vs. Eternal/Blinkit's 2,243. The gap is 2x in store count and significantly larger in dark store area (4.8M sq ft vs. 17M sq ft). Swiggy's differentiation strategy in Instamart (premium/clean food vs. commodity quick delivery) is the right long-term answer but will take 4–6 quarters to show in consumer data.

**Food delivery is the cash engine.** At 3.3% Adj EBITDA margin (₹297 Cr Q4, ~₹1,188 Cr annualised), food delivery is already a profitable, compounding business. GOV growth at 22.6% YoY (15-quarter high) demonstrates Swiggy has not ceded food delivery share to Eternal/Zomato — it is growing faster than it has since COVID demand normalised.

**The profitability timeline.** The path: Food delivery (profitable ✓) → Dineout (profitable ✓) → Instamart CM breakeven (expected FY27) → Instamart EBITDA positive (FY28-29). The group consolidated Adj EBITDA breakeven likely FY28. Management explicitly avoids "short-term decisions" — quality of improvement matters more than speed.

**Instamart differentiation is the strategic bet.** By investing in Noice (clean food) and premium upgrades rather than matching Blinkit's discounting, Swiggy is betting on a segment of Indian consumers who will pay for quality. Risk: if this premium segment is too small to sustain Instamart's unit economics without broad-market volume, the strategy fails. Early signs (strong uptake on Noice) are encouraging but pre-scale.

**Snacc shutdown = discipline signal.** Proactively closing a product line ("not convinced on steady-state category size") during a period of competitive intensity signals management discipline — they're not chasing adjacent TAMs for growth optics. This is rare in Indian consumer tech.

---

## Contradictions

- **Instamart store additions**: Only +7 net stores in Q4 (to 1,143) vs. Eternal/Blinkit's +216 stores in the same quarter. This is a 30x gap in store velocity. Swiggy management has not explicitly addressed why store additions slowed so dramatically — whether it's capital allocation, profitable-store-first strategy, or real estate availability. Watch Q1 FY27 Instamart store additions.

- **Contribution margin vs. EBITDA gap.** Instamart CM improved to −1.8% of GOV in Q4 (monthly −1.1% March), but Adj EBITDA is still −10.9% of GOV (−₹858 Cr). The ~9% gap between CM and EBITDA is fixed cost overheads. As volumes scale, fixed costs should dilute — but the absolute EBITDA loss is still significant at current scale.

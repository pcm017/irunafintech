# Axis Bank Limited

**One-liner:** India's 3rd largest private sector bank by assets, with a 5.7% advances market share, digital leadership in UPI (36% payer PSP share) and credit cards (14% CIF), pursuing a "GPS" strategy (Growth, Profitability, Sustainability) toward 18% ROE.

---

## Current State

*As of Q4 FY26 (March 31, 2026)*

Axis Bank closed FY26 with a balance sheet of ₹18.87 Tn (+17% YoY), advances of ₹12.34 Tn (+19% YoY), and deposits of ₹13.36 Tn (+14% YoY). Q4 FY26 PAT was ₹7,071 Cr (+9% QoQ), and FY26 PAT ₹24,457 Cr (−7% YoY) — headline decline masked by two offsetting one-time items: a ₹2,193 Cr tax credit from Citi intangibles deferred tax and a ₹2,001 Cr voluntary standard asset provision against West Asia macro tail risk.

NIM has compressed to 3.62% (FY26: 3.69%) due to 125 bps of repo rate cut transmission; management reaffirms 3.80% through-cycle NIM target, expected 15–18 months from last rate cut. Retail book rebalancing is underway — Q4 retail disbursements +24% YoY, +19% QoQ — but the wholesale-heavy mix (33% corporate) will take 2–3 years to normalise to the 70/30 target.

Asset quality continued its clean-up trajectory: GNPA 1.23% (−17 bps QoQ), net credit cost 0.37% (−39 bps QoQ). Technical slippages are now negligible (net ₹218 Cr, 0.07% of book) and disclosure will be discontinued from Q1 FY27. The bank holds ₹15,473 Cr in cumulative non-NPA provisions — standard asset coverage of 1.26%.

AI initiative (AXIOM): Only ISO 42001-certified BFSI organisation globally. Expects "meaningful bottom-line impact" over next 18–24 months.

## Thesis / View

Axis Bank is in a credible multi-year re-rating cycle, transitioning from a balance-sheet-repair story to a sustainable compounding franchise. The FY26 NIM dip and PAT decline are cyclical (rate cut pass-through + deliberate provisioning conservatism), not structural. The underlying trajectory — fee growth, cost efficiency, asset quality clean-up, deposit momentum — is constructive.

The key risk is the wholesale overweight (33% corporate vs. 30% target): it is NIM-dilutive, leverage-constrained, and exposes the bank to macro shocks (West Asia). Management has pre-positioned a ₹2,001 Cr buffer for this and is explicit that retail/SME disbursement momentum will rebalance the mix over 2–3 years.

The 3.80% through-cycle NIM target and 18% ROE aspiration are achievable if: (1) residual TD repricing benefits materialise in FY27, (2) retail disbursement momentum sustains (Q4 evidence is encouraging), and (3) West Asia does not escalate to a demand-destruction event for India.

AXIOM (AI operating model) is the medium-term differentiator — ISO 42001 certification, enterprise-scale deployment in progress, expected "meaningful bottom-line impact" by FY28.

---

## Contradictions

- **GNPA vs. PCR:** GNPA improved to 1.23% but PCR declined from 75% (Mar-25) to 70% (Mar-26). This is explained partly by technical NPA composition and partly by write-offs outpacing provisions. Worth monitoring if GNPA inches back up.
- **NIM target:** Management has consistently guided 3.80% through-cycle NIM for 3+ years; it remains at 3.62%. The timeline is extended each time due to rate environment. Achievability requires scrutiny.

# PB Fintech Limited (POLICYBZR / 543390)

## One-liner
India's largest online insurance and credit marketplace, operating Policybazaar (insurance) and Paisabazaar (credit/lending), listed on NSE/BSE since November 2021.

---

## Current state

As of Analyst Day 2026 (May 11, 2026), PB Fintech has fully delivered on its post-IPO operating plan. The company turned profitable in FY24 and reported PAT of ₹670 Cr in FY26 — a ₹1,503 Cr swing from the -₹833 Cr loss at IPO. FY27 PAT target is ₹1,000 Cr. Revenue reached ₹6,794 Cr (48% CAGR since IPO).

The business is organized into four operating units: Policybazaar (Life + Health + Motor), Paisabazaar (credit marketplace), PB Partners (POSP/agent distribution platform), and UAE operations (Policybazaar.ae).

The primary compounding engine is renewal revenue — ₹935 Cr in FY26 at ~80% margins, growing at 43% CAGR since FY19. This near-passes-through to EBITDA and is structurally tied to the quality of prior-year new business.

## Thesis / view

PB Fintech is best understood as an insurance distribution platform whose economics compound through renewals, not as a transactional marketplace. The key insight is that ~80% of renewal revenue drops near-directly to EBITDA — so every year of high-quality new business written today becomes a high-margin annuity starting next year.

The business has three reinforcing moats: (1) **data and risk intelligence** — 30+ parameters per customer, fraud detection reducing insurer loss ratios; (2) **claims infrastructure** — 250+ cities, on-ground support, 245k health claims in FY26; (3) **co-created products** — 26% of all health plans on PB are co-designed with insurers, creating switching friction for both sides.

Paisabazaar is in a different, more complicated position. The pivot away from credit cards (peak 152k/qtr → 18k) reflects macro credit tightening in unsecured lending. The strategy now pivots to repeat lending economics, secured credit (home loans, LAP), and a nascent wealth/savings platform. Recovery is real but execution risk remains — the secured credit pivot is early and unproven at scale.

The FY30 target of ₹1 Lac Cr insurance premium implies roughly 3.3x growth from FY26's ₹29,934 Cr over 4 years (~35% CAGR). Given FY22→FY26 was 49% CAGR, this is a deliberate deceleration assumption — plausible but dependent on health insurance market growth continuing and protection (term) penetration deepening.

---

## Contradictions

- *Paisabazaar credit card trajectory*: The FY26 credit card issuance of 3.5 lacs (~18k/qtr run-rate by Q4) is far below the FY25 peak (~6 lacs annualized at peak). Management frames this as deliberate quality tightening, but it also coincides with broader industry stress in unsecured credit. Whether this recovers or is structurally impaired is unclear.
- *UAE profitability*: 54% YoY premium growth and 12x since FY22 are impressive, but profitability of UAE operations is not disclosed separately. No margin or loss ratio data provided for UAE.

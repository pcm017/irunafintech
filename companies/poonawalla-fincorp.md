# Poonawalla Fincorp Limited

**BSE:** 524000 | **NSE:** POONAWALLA | **Parent:** Cyrus Poonawalla Group | **HQ:** Pune

## One-liner

Poonawalla Fincorp is a mid-sized India NBFC (AUM ₹60,348 crore) executing a multi-product consumer and MSME lending pivot under MD & CEO Arvind Kapil, with ROA recovering sharply to 1.81% in Q4 FY26 and 35–40% AUM CAGR guided over the next two years.

## Current State

*As of Q1 FY27 (quarter ending June 30, 2026):*

**NEW (July 17, 2026 — Q1 FY27 results, inflection continues):** The Q4 FY26 "inflection point" has extended rather than stalled: **AUM ₹67,054 Cr (+62.5% YoY, +11.1% QoQ); PAT ₹308 Cr (+391.5% YoY, +20.8% QoQ); ROA improved a further 17bps QoQ to 1.98%** (from 1.81% Q4 FY26, 0.68% a year ago). New products contributed ₹3,600+ Cr disbursement (26% of total) with Gold Loan branches expanded to 460. Management reiterated 35-40% AUM CAGR guidance over the next two years. *As of Q4 FY26 (quarter ending March 31, 2026):*

Q4 FY26 is management's self-described "significant inflection point." The company has crossed a threshold — investments in technology, collections infrastructure, and six new product lines are now converting to operating leverage rather than just cost drag.

**Key Q4 FY26 achievements:**
- AUM ₹60,348 crore (+69.4% YoY), growing across all product lines
- PAT ₹255 crore (+70% QoQ, +311% YoY) — parabolic profit recovery
- ROA 1.81% — up from 0.78% a year ago; structural improvement, not one-off
- NIM restored to 9.05% — management had guided 9%+ in 3–4 quarters from Q1 FY26; achieved in 3
- GNPA 1.44% (improving sequentially for 3 quarters from 1.84% peak)
- Credit cost declining: 2.51% vs 2.62% Q3; 6MoB30+ delinquencies at new lows

**New product ramp:**
Six new products (Prime Personal Loan, Gold Loan, Consumer Durables, Commercial Vehicle, Education, Shopkeeper Loan) now represent 24% of disbursements, up from 20% in Q3. Gold loan: 400 branches operationalised across Tier 2/3 India. PL Prime: ₹468 crore/month disbursement rate; 33% processed end-to-end digitally. Consumer durables: 12,500+ retail outlets, 240 locations.

**Digital aggregator channels include:** Prefr, PaisaBazaar, Super.money, Paytm, MobiKwik, Angel One, CRED, Buddy Loan, Bajaj Markets.

**Post-quarter:** ₹2,500 crore QIP raised in April 2026 (not in Q4 balance sheet; simulated CAR 20.74% post-raise).

## Thesis / View

Poonawalla Fincorp is executing one of the cleaner NBFC pivots in the India lending market — from a legacy corporate/MSME book under prior management to a diversified consumer + MSME multi-product platform under Arvind Kapil. The Q4 FY26 results are the first quarter where the pivot economics become clearly visible in the P&L.

**Bull case:** ROA expands to 2%+ in FY27 as credit costs normalise below 2%, NIM stabilises above 9%, and operating leverage from digital channels compounds. AUM growth at 35–40% CAGR on a ₹60,000 crore base would make Poonawalla a ₹80,000–85,000 crore AUM company by FY27-end — approaching top-10 India NBFC scale.

**Bear case:** Gold loan branch network (400 branches) requires sustained opex before reaching scale profitability. Consumer durable and education loan yields are at the lower end of the product mix. If unsecured consumer credit stress re-emerges (CIBIL Bureau data showed sector-wide delinquency uptick in 2025), Poonawalla's newer cohorts may show worse-than-expected vintage behaviour.

**Key watch:**
- Credit cost trajectory: is 2.51% the floor or still declining toward management's implicit 2% target?
- New product AUM mix: tracking if 35% target of disbursements from new products is hit in FY27
- Gold loan profitability: branch break-even timeline; are Tier 2/3 branches generating positive unit economics?
- Prefr channel performance: as digital aggregator, any Prefr-Poonawalla sourcing volume data would be valuable

---

## Contradictions

- PCR declined from 54.5% to 49.0% even as GNPA improved — the company is releasing provisions faster than NPAs are moving. A macro stress event could require provision rebuild.

# Trent Limited

## One-liner

Tata Group's fast-fashion and retail arm, operating the Westside (large-format lifestyle), Zudio (value fashion) and Star (food & grocery) chains across 1,286 stores in 321 Indian cities plus a small UAE footprint.

## Current state

Q4FY26 (filed 22 April 2026): Trent delivered standalone revenue of ₹4,937 Cr (+20% YoY) with profit growing faster than revenue — EBITDA +43%, operating EBIT +43%, PAT +30% to ₹455 Cr — signalling strong operating leverage from its rapidly scaling store network (1,286 stores, +109 Zudio openings in the quarter alone). Zudio remains the primary growth engine, more than tripling Westside's store count (963 vs. 300) and pushing over 80% of new-store openings into Tier II/III cities and peripheral micro-markets rather than existing metro catchments. FY26 ROCE exceeded 29%. The Board approved an in-principle ~₹2,500 Cr capital raise to fund store upgrades, new brand/category/geography incubation, supply-chain automation, and Star's real-estate-backed rollout — signalling continued aggressive expansion rather than a pause. Management flagged consumer caution and moderating discretionary spending tied to "the unfolding geopolitical situation" (West Asia conflict), plus early signs of input-cost inflation on select raw materials.

## Thesis / view

Trent is executing a deliberate land-grab strategy in Indian value/fast fashion: Zudio's store count (963, more than 3x Westside) and its Tier II/III-first expansion (>80% of new stores) prioritise market coverage and share over same-store comp growth (like-for-like growth was low single digits in Q4 and for FY26). The fact that profit is growing faster than revenue (EBITDA +43% vs. revenue +20%) suggests this expansion is not diluting margins — RFID-driven supply-chain automation and disciplined manpower-cost management are doing real work. The ~₹2,500 Cr capital raise is the clearest forward signal: management intends to keep scaling (new brands, categories, geographies, automation, and Star's real-estate-backed store rollout) rather than harvest cash from the current base. The main watch item is management's own caution flag on consumer discretionary spending tied to geopolitical/input-cost pressure — worth checking against India's broader consumption and CPI data for corroboration.

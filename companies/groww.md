# Groww (Billionbrains Garage Ventures Limited)

**BSE:** 544603 | **NSE:** GROWW | **CIN:** L72900KA2018PLC109343  
**Registered Office:** Vaishnavi Tech Park, Bellandur, Bangalore

## One-liner

India's largest retail investment platform by user base, offering stocks, mutual funds, derivatives, commodities, and credit products to 21.6 Mn transacting users, with ₹3.0 Tn in total customer assets and industry-leading PAT margins (~45%).

## Current state

*As of Q4 FY26 (quarter ending March 2026):*

Groww is India's most profitable listed fintech by PAT margin. Q4 FY26 PAT of ₹6,864 Mn (+122% YoY) on Total Income of ₹15,355 Mn (+81% YoY) represents a PAT margin of 44.7% — extraordinary for a platform of this scale. The driver is operating leverage: cost structure is largely fixed, so revenue growing at 80%+ YoY is flowing almost entirely to the bottom line.

The platform has achieved broad-based market share gains in its core products: MF SIP share 15.7% (was 12.1% a year ago), Equity Derivatives 10.6% (was 6.8%), MTF book gaining share even as the industry contracted. Product attach rates (Stocks 72%, MF 60%) indicate deep engagement among active users.

Two subsidiaries are loss-making: Fisdom (acquired Oct 2025, expected profitable FY28) and Growwmf (the AMC, needs 5-6x AUM growth — a multi-year journey). These drag consolidated EBITDA by ₹316 Mn in Q4 vs. the core platform's ₹9,704 Mn EBITDA.

Groww has begun deploying PAT and fundraise proceeds into on-balance-sheet lending (MTF ₹28,143 Mn book, LAS/PL also scaling), marking a strategic shift from pure marketplace to balance-sheet participant. Credit business contributed 4.1% of consolidated PAT in Q4.

India equity market penetration remains in single digits — management estimates 3-4x expansion potential over the next decade. West Asia/Hormuz geopolitical volatility creates short-term derivatives tailwinds but medium-term risk of FII selling dampening sentiment.

**NEW (July 17, 2026 — Q1 FY27 headline figures, newsletter-sourced, preliminary):** Groww reported Q1 FY27 **net profit up 94% YoY to ₹735 Cr** (vs. ₹378 Cr Q1 FY26) on **revenue up 66% YoY to ₹1,501 Cr** (vs. ₹904 Cr) — continuing the PAT-margin-expansion trend seen through FY26. Total customer assets reached **₹3.6 lakh crore (+38% YoY)**. Growth was more muted on the user-acquisition side: Groww added 1,15,000 net active clients on the NSE even as the overall industry saw a **net decline of ~2,57,000 active clients** — management attributed this to lower capital-markets activity (fewer IPOs/ETFs). Equity derivatives remain ~half of revenue but a shrinking share, with MTF, commodity trading, LAS, and asset management scaling faster. *Figures are from a newsletter digest (YourStory/Filter Coffee), not yet a primary results deck/transcript ingest — treat as preliminary pending the formal Q1 FY27 filing.* See *2026 07 17 fintech bfsi digest*

## Timeline

- **2021-2024**: Expanded into stocks, derivatives, IPOs, digital gold, US stocks
- **Oct 2025**: Acquired Fisdom (wealth management / goal-based advisory)
- **FY26**: IPO / listing on BSE and NSE (BSE: 544603, NSE: GROWW); first full reporting year as a public company

## Thesis / view

Groww's moat is the combination of superior user experience (mobile-first, ultra-low-friction onboarding) and a first-mover advantage in converting India's newly digital middle class into capital market investors. With India equity penetration in single digits and management estimating 3-4x expansion potential over the next decade, the TAM runway is enormous.

The operating leverage dynamic is extraordinary and will continue for several more years: the platform's cost base is largely fixed (tech infrastructure, G&A, risk systems), while revenue scales with users, assets, and transaction volume. As long as user acquisition and engagement sustains, PAT margins should keep expanding toward 50%+.

The key risks are: (1) **regulatory** — SEBI could restrict derivatives volumes (options on small-caps, weekly expiry caps) which would directly hit ~55% of revenue; (2) **market-driven** — sustained FII selling and bear markets reduce new investor confidence, slow user acquisition, and shrink equity AUM; (3) **competitive** — Zerodha remains strong in active traders; PhonePe and Paytm Money are investing in wealth; banks have deeper relationships for HNI wealth.

The strategic move into on-balance-sheet lending (MTF, LAS, PL) is the right next step — it monetises the customer relationship more deeply and diversifies revenue away from markets-dependent trading commissions. But credit risk management at scale is a new muscle for Groww.

**Growwmf is a long-duration bet.** Running an AMC requires patience — the unit economics improve slowly as AUM scales. It will be a drag on reported profitability for at least 2-3 more years.

## Contradictions

- Management projects 3-4x capital market penetration growth over the next decade, yet simultaneously warns that sustained FII selling and geopolitical tensions can dampen sentiment and slow new user acquisition. The tension between the structural long-term opportunity and cyclical short-term sensitivity to market performance is not fully reconciled.

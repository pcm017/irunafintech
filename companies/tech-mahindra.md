# Tech Mahindra Limited

**One-liner:** Tech Mahindra is India's 5th-largest IT services company (Mahindra Group), undergoing a margin-led turnaround (Project Fortius) while pivoting toward an AI-native delivery model — stabilisation phase in FY26, targeting peer-leading growth in FY27.

---

## Current State

*As of Q4 FY26 (April 22, 2026)*

Tech Mahindra has completed its **"Stabilisation Phase" (FY26)** — marked by 10 consecutive quarters of EBIT margin expansion and a return to positive CC revenue growth in Q4 FY26 (+2.4% YoY). FY26 EBIT reached **12.6% (+290 bps YoY)**, up from 9.7% in FY25. Q4 FY26 EBIT hit 13.8% — the highest in 5 quarters. The recovery is **cost-led (Project Fortius)** more than demand-led, with flattish overall revenue growth.

FY27 is the "Reaping Returns" phase: topline growth > peer average, industry-standard margins (target: 15%), high predictability. The key test: does the demand side (deal wins, revenue CC growth) now validate the cost-discipline narrative?

The company is building toward a genuine **AI-native delivery model**: 350+ service agents, 80% workforce AI-ready, token-based pricing ("beyond the billable hour"), and Orion (agent marketplace, live on 3 hyperscalers). If successful, this represents a structural disruption to traditional IT T&M billing.

## Thesis / View

TechM's turnaround is **real but cost-led**: the margin recovery from 9.7% → 12.6% → target 15% has come from Project Fortius (headcount pyramid optimisation, G&A cuts, delivery discipline) more than from revenue acceleration. The real test is FY27: can the demand side (+2.4% Q4 CC growth) continue to accelerate? Deal wins at $3.8B LTM (+41.6%) are constructive — the pipeline is building.

The **AI-native delivery pivot** is TechM's most differentiated bet among India IT majors. Token-based pricing and vector squads (replacing T&M billing) is a genuine commercial model innovation that TCS/Infosys/Wipro have not yet attempted at scale. If this scales in 1–2 large engagements, it becomes TechM's primary competitive differentiator.

**Key risks:**
- H-1B regulatory change affects TechM more than peers (higher onshore ratio)
- GCC rise: large Indian tech companies (HDFC Bank, Bajaj Finance, Jio) building captive centres rather than outsourcing
- Revenue growth has just turned positive — one bad macro quarter could reverse it
- Communication vertical (TechM's heritage strength) faces structural pressure from software-defined networking and carrier AI capex declines

---

## Contradictions

- Management calls FY26 a "Stabilization Phase" but deal win improvement (+41.6% Q4 LTM) and revenue turning positive (+2.4% CC) suggest early "Reaping Returns" signals. The conservative framing may be deliberate sandbagging.
- Revenue per employee improved 7% from AI — but total revenue growth is flattish. This implies either workforce reduction or revenue stagnation offsetting the productivity gain. Source doesn't resolve this clearly.

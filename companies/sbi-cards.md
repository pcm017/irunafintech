# SBI Cards and Payment Services Limited

**One-liner:** India's largest pure-play credit card company and #2 credit card issuer by cards-in-force and spends, operating as an SBI Group entity (SBI holds 68.58% stake).

---

## Current State

*As of Q4 FY26 (March 31, 2026)*

SBI Cards is emerging from two years of elevated credit cost (FY24–FY25 asset quality stress) into a recovery phase. FY26 PAT reached ₹2,167 Cr (+13% YoY) and Q4 FY26 PAT ₹609 Cr (+14% YoY), driven primarily by declining credit costs rather than receivables growth. The receivables book is effectively flat YoY (+2%) because the company deliberately tightened new account sourcing (−12% YoY in new accounts) to improve portfolio quality.

The key story for FY27 is: (1) whether credit cost continues its sequential decline toward management's medium-term ROA target of 4–4.5%, and (2) whether the revolver mix stabilises or continues drifting lower, and whether installment lending can compensate.

**Key management:** MD & CEO Salila Pande; CFO Rashmi Mohanty; Chief Risk Officer Krishna Kant Bishnoi; Chief Sales & Marketing Officer Girish Budhiraja.

## Thesis / View

**Accumulated from 2 sources (presentation + transcript)**

SBI Cards is a recovery-phase BFSI play. The fundamental investment thesis rests on: (a) credit cost normalisation back toward 5–6% as FY25 vintage stress fully works through, (b) receivables growth re-acceleration as new-account quality improves and cards-in-force grow back above current 2.21 Cr, (c) EMI book build compensating for structural revolver decline, and (d) operating leverage from digital-first acquisition and AI-driven collections.

The bear case: revolver mix falls faster than EMI compensates, creating NIM compression; corporate spends normalise (removing the C/I relief); and geopolitical macro (Hormuz) creates new wave of consumer stress.

Management's medium-term ROA target of 4–4.5% would require credit cost falling to ~6% and receivables resuming mid-to-high single-digit growth — both plausible but dependent on macro stability.

**Key structural advantage:** SBI parentage gives access to ~550 Mn SBI bank customers for Banca acquisition channel (~46% of new sourcing). This is a durable moat unavailable to standalone fintechs.

## Contradictions

1. **Cost-to-income guidance vs. history:** Management guides 55–58% C/I for FY27, but pre-FY25 C/I was ~51–52%. The gap is attributed to corporate spends (pass-back model adds cost + income at thin margin). If corporate spends are now structural, C/I may not return to previous lows. *Sources: Q4 FY26 transcript.*
2. **Revolver timing:** Management says revolver has "slight downward bias" but declined to give a floor. If revolver drops from 22% to 19–20%, each 1% drop is ~25–27 bps of NIM compression assuming no EMI offset. Management's NIM stability guidance implicitly requires EMI acceleration — which has been slow (EMI mix 22% in Q4 FY26). *Sources: Q4 FY26 transcript.*
3. **ECL overlay vs. improving portfolio:** Company built ₹220 Cr management overlay while Stage 2/3 stocks were declining. CFO cited geopolitical uncertainty and ECL model refresh as rationale. If overlay is released in H1 FY27 (as environment clarifies), it would boost PAT by ~₹165 Cr net (post-tax). *Sources: Q4 FY26 transcript.*

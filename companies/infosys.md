# Infosys Limited

**BSE:** 500209 | **NSE:** INFY | **NYSE:** INFY | **Founder:** N. R. Narayana Murthy (et al.) | **HQ:** Bengaluru

## One-liner

Infosys is India's second-largest IT services company by revenue — a $20B/year technology outsourcing, consulting, and AI services firm with 328,594 employees globally — currently guiding modest FY27 CC growth of 1.5–3.5% while accelerating AI services revenue.

---

## Current State

*As of Q4 FY26 (quarter ending March 31, 2026):*

Infosys delivered FY26 CC revenue growth of 3.1% — below the historical 8–12% growth era but above the FY25 near-stagnation. Growth was absorbing headwinds from lower third-party pass-through revenue (-1%) and on-site mix normalisation (-70 bps). The underlying services growth was stronger than the headline suggests.

Q4 FY26 was a strong quarter: operating margin at 20.9% (vs 18.4% in Q3), net profit $919M (+23% QoQ), and large deal TCV of $3.2B. Financial Services, Communication, Life Sciences, and Energy/Utilities were growth drivers; Hi-Tech was the laggard (-1.5% YoY).

**FY27 guidance**: 1.5–3.5% CC revenue growth with 20–22% operating margin. The guidance range is wide, reflecting three opposing forces: (1) AI services growing strongly, (2) AI-driven productivity compression on T&M billing, and (3) continued competitive intensity including GCC expansion. Management expects **acceleration in Financial Services and Energy/Utilities** in FY27.

AI revenue as % of revenue was >5.5% in Q3 FY26 and continued growing in Q4 (not separately disclosed). Infosys's AI platform is **Topaz Fabric** (AI services) + **Cobalt** (cloud). TAM for AI services estimated at $300–400B by 2030 across six domains: AI strategy, engineering, data, process, legacy modernisation, physical AI, and trust/compliance.

**Leadership risk**: CEO Salil Parekh's term ends March 2027. Extension discussion is reportedly ongoing at board level but no announcement as of April 2026.

## Thesis / View

Infosys is navigating the most structurally complex IT cycle in a decade — simultaneously managing AI-driven revenue compression (productivity tools reducing human T&M billing) and AI-driven revenue opportunity (new AI services spend). The FY27 guidance of 1.5–3.5% CC captures this tension honestly.

The bull case is that Infosys's Topaz Fabric and large-deal momentum ($14.9B TCV, +28% YoY) allow it to grow AI services faster than compression eats into traditional billing. The bear case is that the compression is industry-wide and structural, and Infosys's guidance is achievable but uninspiring vs. global peers.

**Key watch factors:**
- Financial Services vertical acceleration in Q1/Q2 FY27 — this is the stated driver of guidance beat
- AI revenue absolute number disclosure (currently withheld) — when/if disclosed, will reset valuation debate
- Salil Parekh succession — extension or new leadership by Q3 FY27

**Vs. TCS**: TCS guides no numbers but expresses "a lot of positivity"; TCS AI revenues ($2.3B annualised) are higher in absolute terms but Infosys's AI % of revenue (>5.5%) may be proportionally similar. Infosys has the more modular AI platform story (Topaz + Cobalt); TCS has the execution track record and HyperVault as a new AI infrastructure growth engine.

---

## Contradictions

- Infosys employee count declined QoQ (337,034 → 328,594) while guiding 1.5–3.5% growth — either AI-driven productivity reduces headcount need, or growth will require re-hiring. Management guided hiring ~20,000 freshers in FY27 (similar to FY26).

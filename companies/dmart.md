# Avenue Supermarts Limited (DMart)

**NSE:** DMART | **BSE:** 540376 | **Sector:** Value retail / grocery

## One-liner

DMart (Avenue Supermarts) is India's leading value-retail/grocery chain, whose Q1 FY27 results showed re-accelerating headline revenue and profit growth (+15.1%/+12.8% YoY) but a sharp like-for-like growth deceleration (10.8% to 5.5% QoQ) that raises early questions about per-store productivity as new store additions outpace existing-store demand.

---

## Current State

*As of Q1 FY27 (quarter ending June 30, 2026):*

DMart's revenue from operations grew **+15.1% YoY to ₹18,343 Cr** (+6.6% QoQ), with **EBITDA up 16.3% YoY to ₹1,527 Cr** (margin 8.3%, recovering from a Q4 FY26 dip to 7.2%) and **PAT up 12.8% YoY to ₹936 Cr** (margin 5.1%). The more important underlying signal is a sharp **like-for-like (LFL) growth deceleration to 5.5%** (from 10.8% in Q4 FY26) — the softest same-store growth print in the trailing five quarters — alongside **revenue per retail sq ft declining to ₹8,571** (from a Q3 FY26 peak of ₹9,290). Retail business area reached **20.7 million sq ft** (+17.6% YoY, flat QoQ), meaning a meaningful share of this quarter's headline growth came from new store additions rather than existing-store demand strength. ## Thesis / View

DMart enters the wiki as a bellwether India-consumption read given its value-retail positioning across staples and discretionary categories. The Q1 FY27 LFL deceleration (10.8% to 5.5%) is the key thing to watch into Q2 FY27 — if it's a one-quarter blip (e.g., high base effect from a strong Q4 FY26), the headline growth story remains intact; if it persists, it would be a meaningful corroborating data point for the wiki's "consumption paradox" thread (premiumisation booming at the top end while EMI-burdened middle-income consumers pull back), given DMart's customer base skews toward exactly that middle-income segment.

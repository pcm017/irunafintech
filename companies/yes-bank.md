# Yes Bank

## One-liner

India's 6th-largest private sector bank by assets (₹4.69 lakh cr), undergoing a post-2020 reconstruction-to-compounding transition with ROA reaching 1.0% in Q4FY26 — its highest quarterly return since the SBI-led bailout.

## Current state

Q4FY26 (June 2026): Yes Bank delivered PAT of ₹1,068 cr (+44.7% YoY, +12.3% QoQ) — the highest quarterly profit since the 2020 reconstruction. FY26 PAT of ₹3,476 cr (+44.5% YoY). The headline numbers are flattered by ₹1,559 cr in P&L gains from Security Receipt (SR) settlements in FY26 — normalized PAT growth is still real but more modest. The structural story is more compelling: NIM expanded 20 bps YoY to 2.7% in Q4 as the PSL shortfall deposit drag reduces (₹27,931 cr in mandated low-yield placements, down 24.5% YoY); C/I ratio improved 430 bps YoY to 63.0% in Q4 with positive jaws (income +8.8% YoY, opex +1.8% YoY); and GNPA fell to 1.3% (from 15.4% peak in FY22). CASA crossed ₹1 lakh cr and CASA ratio hit 35.1% for the first time post-reconstruction. CET1 at 13.8%, LCR healthy at ~119%.

## Thesis / view

Yes Bank is in a third phase of its lifecycle: **reconstruction (FY20–FY22) → repair (FY23–FY24) → compounding (FY25–FY26+)**. The market often discounts it because of the SR-gain noise in the P&L and the PSL shortfall drag on NIM. Both are misread.

The SR gains (₹1,559 cr in FY26) will taper as the security receipt book fully settles — but the underlying operating momentum is what matters. Opex growth of 4.6% YoY against income growth of 11.7% YoY in FY26 is genuine operating leverage, not financial engineering. The C/I trajectory (71.3% FY25 → 66.7% FY26 → 63% Q4FY26 run rate) points toward low-60s within 2–3 years, which is competitive with mid-tier private peers.

The PSL shortfall deposit drag is the bigger structural story. At ₹27,931 cr (6% of assets) in mandated low-yield RIDF/PSL placements, Yes Bank is pricing its deposit franchise less than its true cost would suggest. As this burden reduces toward <5% and eventually <3%, NIM structurally expands 50–100 bps without any loan yield or mix shift needed. This is a slow-burn but high-visibility tailwind.

Key risks: (1) retail slippage trajectory — retail book is 46% of advances and slippage ratio improved to 2.8% Q4FY26 (9-quarter low) but still elevated vs Kotak/ICICI peers; (2) management continuity after reconstruction leadership era; (3) SMBC stake — strategic anchor but no governance catalyst yet; (4) SR gains will fully zero out in 1–2 years, leaving the P&L on its own.

## Contradictions

- SR gains inflate PAT: ₹1,559 cr FY26 P&L gain from SRs = ~45% of total PAT of ₹3,476 cr. Stripping this, FY26 normalized PAT ≈ ₹2,600 cr. Management presents headline PAT without consistently normalizing for SR contribution — note this when comparing to peers.

# ICICI Bank

## One-liner

India's second-largest private sector bank by assets, operating a 360-degree financial services group spanning banking, insurance, AMC, securities, and home finance.

## Current state

Q4FY26 (April 2026): ICICI Bank delivered steady PAT growth of 8.5% YoY to ₹137.02 bn for the quarter, with FY26 PAT crossing ₹501 bn (+6.2% YoY). The headline story was a collapse in credit costs to near-zero in Q4 (₹0.96 bn, 0.03% of avg advances), driven by normalizing retail unsecured delinquencies and higher corporate recoveries — FY26 credit cost landed at 38 bps vs guidance of sub-50 bps. NIM held at 4.32% despite a full quarter of December repo cut impact, as deposit repricing offset benchmark-linked loan repricing. Loan growth accelerated to 15.8% YoY — led by rural/gold loans (+25.6% YoY), business banking (+24.4% YoY), and a mortgage revival (+13.2% YoY). The West Asia conflict in Q4 created some uncertainty, but management saw no credit stress signals in the book as of April 2026. Opex growth of 12% outpaced NII growth of 8.4% — partially one-offs (Labour Code, agri PSL compliance, equity MTM), but bears watching in FY27.

## Thesis / view

ICICI Bank operates as a "franchise compounder" — consistent mid-to-high single-digit PAT growth, high capital adequacy (CET1 16.35%), and a building buffer of contingency provisions (₹131 bn). The business model's key tension is: (a) 56% of domestic loans are repo-linked, so each RBI cut compresses NII immediately; (b) opex investment in branches and technology runs at 12% growth even in a rate-cut year; (c) credit quality is currently exceptional — but it has been built on a period of tight RBI regulation on unsecured lending that may not continue indefinitely.

The West Asia crisis is a new variable. ICICI's rural book is growing fast (+25.6% YoY) and includes agri priority sector where they hold an RBI-directed extra provision. A below-normal monsoon in FY27 (IMD forecasting) would test this book. The contingency cushion absorbs some risk, but not if rural slippages spike materially.

The credit card book contracting (-5.6% YoY) is a systemic issue — industry revolvers declining, fee income softer. ICICI acknowledged this as an area for improvement in FY27.

# State Bank of India (SBI)

## One-liner

India's largest bank by assets (~₹76 lakh cr balance sheet, ~22% system market share), delivering record FY26 PAT of ₹80,032 cr (+12.88%) at >1% ROA — among the very few global banks at this scale to sustain this return.

## Current state

Q4FY26 (May 2026): SBI delivered FY26 Net Profit of ₹80,032 cr (+12.88% YoY) — a record high. Operating profit grew 11.25% YoY. Domestic NIM for FY26 stood at 3.03%, meeting the >3% guidance. Q4FY26 specifically had a ₹4,520 cr MTM treasury loss (vs ₹143 cr Q3) that pulled Q4 PAT below Q3, but this is non-recurring. Credit growth for FY26 was 16.87% — above the 14–16% guidance — driven by RAM and corporate segments. GNPA improved to 1.49% (-33 bps YoY), a two-decade low. CASA ratio 39.46%, Q4 domestic NIM at 2.93% (exit). ROE 18.5% in Q4. For FY27: credit growth guided at 13–15%, domestic NIM >3% maintained, credit cost 50 bps, ROA >1% through cycles. ECL transition (April 2027) expected smooth over 4 years; models ready.

## Thesis / view

SBI is the proxy trade on India's economy — 22% market share, present in every district, the default lender to government capex and retail India alike. The thesis is simple: at >1% ROA on ₹76 lakh cr of assets, SBI is the best scale-adjusted return story in Indian public sector finance. The return per rupee is lower than HDFC Bank or Kotak, but the scale of absolute profit generation (₹80,032 cr in FY26) is unmatched.

The three near-term structural stories: (1) **NIM above 3%**: The T-bill-to-MCLR repricing in corporate book gives a yield pickup without rate risk; the repo rate being stable (SBI's base case) means EBLR-linked loans are also stable. (2) **ECL transition**: With ₹29,000–30,000 cr in provisioning buffer and a 4-year phase-in, SBI is arguably the best-positioned Indian bank for the ECL switchover. (3) **NSE and SBI AMC listing**: Two pending value-unlock events in FY27 that will crystallize hidden book value into CET-1.

Key risks: (1) West Asia exposure — minimal direct credit risk, but MSME clusters (Morbi ceramics) have indirect impact; (2) Treasury volatility — the ₹4,520 cr Q4FY26 MTM hit is a reminder that SBI's large AFS book (₹18 lakh cr investments) is sensitive to yield swings; (3) Wage revision cycle — PLI payout classification uncertainty; (4) C/I ratio above 50% — structural feature of a large branch network, managed but not solved.

## Contradictions

None flagged. The full-year NIM compression visible in the investor presentation (whole bank -17 bps, domestic -18 bps YoY) is consistent with — not contradictory to — the transcript's ">3% NIM guidance met" framing; the presentation simply shows the full-year trend behind that headline more clearly.

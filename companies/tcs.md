# Tata Consultancy Services Limited (TCS)

**BSE:** 532540 | **NSE:** TCS | **Parent:** Tata Group

## One-liner

TCS is India's largest IT services company by revenue — a $7.6B/quarter IT outsourcing and consulting firm — executing a five-pillar AI-led strategy including HyperVault AI data centres, with $2.3B annualised AI revenues at Q4 FY26 exit (≈7.7% of annual revenue at run-rate; estimated 4–6% actual FY26 contribution), 25% operating margin (4-year high), and FY27 outlook described by management as entering with "a lot of positivity and confidence."

---

## Current State

*As of Q4 FY26 (quarter ending March 31, 2026; earnings call April 9, 2026):*

TCS delivered its **third consecutive quarter of CC sequential growth (+1.2% QoQ)** in Q4 FY26 — marking the end of the demand trough. FY26 full-year CC revenue was -2.4% (reflecting the industry-wide normalisation), but the exit trajectory is meaningfully positive. Management entered FY27 with "a lot of positivity and confidence."

**FY26 full year**: USD revenue $30.017B (-0.5% YoY, -2.4% CC); INR revenue ₹267,021 crore (+4.6% YoY); TCV $40.7B including 5 mega deals; operating margin 25% (4-year high, +70 bps YoY). Total one-off charges (severance, legal, India wage code): ₹1,300 crore over FY26.

**AI strategy materialising at pace**: AI revenues now **$2.3B annualised** (up sharply QoQ). The **HyperVault** AI data centre business secured customer commitments, land parcels, and ecosystem partnerships (hyperscalers, semiconductor companies, model providers). TCS is the integration partner across infra, engineering, and AI services — positioning as a full-spectrum "infrastructure to intelligence" player.

**TCS-Anthropic formal partnership confirmed** (June 12, 2026): 50,000 TCS employees are being equipped with Claude. TCS becomes Anthropic's primary enterprise delivery channel for regulated industries (BFSI, healthcare, manufacturing). This directly positions TCS vs. Accenture/Cognizant on the Microsoft + OpenAI axis — TCS offers clients enterprise AI delivery with Anthropic's safety-first, regulated-industry positioning. CEO K Krithivasan: "The collaboration will help customers move faster to production, particularly in industries where trust, resilience, and regulatory discipline are critical." Note: Q4 FY26 earnings call (April 9) had flagged this partnership as "to be announced in the near future" — the June 12 formalisation is the fulfilment of that signal. See *2026 06 13 tech ai digest*.

**Q4 FY26 deal momentum**: $12B TCV, 3 mega deals — Marks & Spencer (renewal + expansion), leading UK telecom, leading US healthcare/pharmacy retailer. Every client revenue band saw additions in Q4 for the first time in ~2 years.

**Restructuring complete.** Final dividend ₹31/share; total FY26 dividend ₹110/share.

**Competitive position vs. India IT peers (Q4 FY26):**
- TCS operating margin: **25.3%** (industry-best)
- vs. Infosys: 20.9%
- vs. TechM: ~12.6% (FY26), targeting 15%
- vs. Wipro/HCL: 17–22% range

**Note on AI revenue proportion:** TCS discloses AI revenue as an *annualised run rate*, not a full-year cumulative figure. The $2.3B figure means: at Q4 FY26's pace, TCS would earn $2.3B from AI in a full year — equivalent to **~7.7% of FY26 annual revenue** ($30.017B). On a quarterly basis, Q4 AI revenue was implied at ~$575M, or ~7.5% of Q4 revenue. However, AI revenues ramped significantly through FY26 (Q3 annualised was ~$1.5B, implying Q3 quarterly AI of ~$375M). Since TCS does not disclose the full-year cumulative AI figure, the actual FY26 AI contribution is estimated at **~4–6% of total FY26 revenue** — the exit rate overstates the full-year average. The 7.7% figure is the correct forward-looking proportion at FY26's exit rate.

## Thesis / View

TCS's structural position in India IT remains unassailable: scale (584,519 employees), margin leadership (25.3% vs Infosys 20.9%, TechM ~12.6%), and client relationship depth ($100M+ club at 66 and growing). The FY26 demand trough (CC -2.4%) appears to be passing — three consecutive quarters of QoQ CC growth confirm the recovery trajectory.

The **AI story has become concrete**: $2.3B annualised AI revenues, HyperVault AI data centre business, and the now-confirmed Anthropic strategic partnership (50K employees on Claude, June 12) all signal TCS is not just a services incumbency story — it's positioning as a full-stack "infrastructure to intelligence" player. The TCS + Anthropic axis creates a specific competitive differentiation: Anthropic's safety-first positioning is a natural fit for large regulated-industry clients (banks, hospitals, insurers) who cannot partner with OpenAI's commercial-first culture. This directly targets the Microsoft + OpenAI + Accenture/Cognizant consulting stack. This is the clearest articulation of a post-T&M commercial model TCS has offered.

**FY27 outlook**: Management entered FY27 with "a lot of positivity and confidence" — expects stronger H1 consistent with historical seasonality. The $40.7B FY26 TCV (including 5 mega deals) provides strong revenue visibility. Clients are not deferring spends due to model transitions (CEO specifically confirmed this on the call).

**Key risks:**
- HyperVault capex execution — 1 GW AI data centre is a large bet; delays or hyperscaler competition could impair returns
- H-1B immigration restrictions affecting US onshore delivery
- GCC growth reducing long-tail outsourcing demand
- Anthropic/OpenAI-native tools enabling clients to bypass SI intermediaries

## Contradictions

- INR revenue +9.6% vs. CC revenue -0.6%: a 10+ percentage point currency-driven divergence that will compress when INR stabilises or appreciates. Not an operational contradiction — but a visibility risk for shareholders who track INR numbers.

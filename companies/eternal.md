# Eternal Limited (formerly Zomato)

**One-liner:** India's dominant food delivery and quick commerce platform, parent of Zomato (food), Blinkit (quick commerce), and District (going-out events), rebranded as Eternal Ltd in FY26.

---

## Current State

*As of Q4 FY26 (quarter ending March 31, 2026)*

Eternal (formerly Zomato Ltd) is India's largest consumer commerce platform by NOV. FY26 marked two milestones: (1) the company processed $10B+ in combined NOV across its three B2C businesses for the first time in a single year, and (2) the parent entity was renamed "Eternal" — a deliberate institutional signal from founder Deepinder Goyal.

**Q4 FY26 highlights:**
- Consolidated Adjusted Revenue: ₹17,680 Cr (+186% YoY reported; LFL +64% YoY accounting for 1P model shift in Blinkit)
- Consolidated Adjusted EBITDA: ₹429 Cr (+160% YoY, +18% QoQ from ₹364 Cr Q3)
- B2C NOV: ₹26,880 Cr (+54% YoY, +4% QoQ)
- FY26 total: 109 million transacting customers, $10B+ NOV

**Food Delivery (Zomato brand):**
- NOV growth: +18.8% YoY (−0.9% QoQ) — third consecutive quarter of YoY acceleration
- GOV growth: +22.5% YoY
- Adjusted EBITDA: ₹532 Cr (5.5% of NOV, +24% YoY)
- Long-term target: 20%+ YoY NOV growth (current 18.8% approaching)

**Quick Commerce (Blinkit):**
- NOV growth: +95.4% YoY (+8.2% QoQ) — high growth off large base
- Store count: 2,243 total (+216 net new in Q4 alone)
- Dark store + warehouse space: **17 million square feet**
- Adjusted EBITDA: ₹37 Cr (0.3% of NOV) — turned profitable in Q4
- 3-year NOV CAGR guidance: >60% (implies 4x current scale by FY29)
- TAM headroom: top 15-20 cities, narrow SKU categories currently — geographic and assortment expansion are the two major growth levers
- Top 8 cities: 80-90% pin code coverage approaching maturity; growth from cities 9–50+ is the next phase
- Delhi NCR: 80k SKUs, 2x store density vs. next 7 cities, 8x vs. cities beyond

**District (Going-Out):**
- NOV growth: +46.5% YoY (+5.8% QoQ)
- Adjusted EBITDA: loss of ₹81 Cr (improved from −₹121 Cr Q3; margin −3.0% vs. −4.7% Q3)
- Category: event ticketing, dining reservations, experiences

**Hyperpure (B2B restaurant supply):**
- Revenue growth: +37% YoY (vs. 33% Q3 — accelerating)
- Adjusted EBITDA: ₹5 Cr (0.5% of revenue — positive and improving from ₹1 Cr Q3)

## Timeline

- **2008:** Deepinder Goyal collecting restaurant menus on a motorbike in Delhi; uploads to website.
- **2010:** Zomato formally incorporated; first advertiser interest.
- **2017:** Food delivery service launched (9 years after founding).
- **2014:** Grofers (later Blinkit) starts as delivery logistics.
- **2021:** Grofers pivots to quick commerce, rebrands as Blinkit.
- **FY24:** First year of Adjusted EBITDA profitability across consolidated entity.
- **FY26:** $10B+ annual NOV; 109M transacting customers; 2,243 Blinkit stores; Blinkit Adj EBITDA turns positive in Q4.
- **Q4 FY26 (Mar 2026):** Parent company renamed **Eternal Ltd** — institutional brand signal from founder.
- **FY29 target:** $1B Adjusted EBITDA (from current ~₹429 Cr/₹5,148 Cr annual run rate at Q4 FY26 EBITDA × 4).

## Thesis / View

**The physical moat is the core competitive advantage.** Eternal's defensibility is not software — it's 17 million sq ft of dark store space, 400k+ restaurant relationships, 1M+ delivery partner network, and the operational systems to run high-frequency commerce in real-world India (imprecise addresses, informal supply chains, fragmented merchants). This is extremely hard to replicate quickly.

**Blinkit is the company's most valuable asset.** At 95%+ YoY NOV growth, 2,243 stores, 17M sq ft of space, and just turning Adj EBITDA positive, Blinkit is at the inflection between high-growth investment mode and early-profitability mode. The 3-year >60% CAGR guidance (4x current scale by FY29) makes it the dominant value driver.

**AI as market expander, not disruptor.** Management's key AI thesis: AI doesn't replace the need to move food through traffic, stock dark store shelves, or manage crowd flow at events. Rather, AI expands the market by lowering the participation bar for customers (conversational search, intent-led discovery), delivery partners (conversational onboarding), and restaurant/seller partners (AI-driven growth guidance). Healthy Mode and Natural Language Search are early live examples.

**AI chat interfaces not a near-term threat.** Management explicitly addressed the "will AI assistants displace Zomato/Blinkit?" concern: no, for the same reason Google failed to pull transactional behavior into Google Flights/Hotels/Shopping despite unmatched distribution. High-frequency habit-based transactions (food 4x/week, groceries every 2 days) don't re-route through chat windows. Brand associations ("hungry → think Zomato; groceries → think Blinkit") are deeper than any interface.

**The Eternal rebranding signal.** Naming the parent "Eternal" is a deliberate signal from Goyal: *"it is a name you have to earn every single day... a commitment to building something that deserves to last."* Companies that survive multiple disruption cycles in India do so through resilience and pivoting — not smarts. The cultural framing of resilience as institutional advantage is important context for how management thinks about competition.

---

## Contradictions

- **Blinkit competition vs. guidance.** Albinder (Blinkit CEO) acknowledges that "high competition can have adverse impact in certain periods... aggressive discounting is leading to poor-quality growth centred around select low-margin SKUs." Yet 3-year CAGR guidance is >60%. Reconciliation: competition is near-term margin pressure but net-positive for market expansion in a nascent category. Watch: if competition intensifies from Swiggy Instamart, Zepto, and Flipkart Minutes beyond current levels, the 60%+ CAGR may come with lower profitability than the current trajectory suggests.

- **Food NOV vs. GOV divergence.** Food NOV +18.8% but GOV +22.5% — a 370 bps gap suggests discounting or Zomato Gold free delivery impact on net order value. Management has not explained this gap explicitly in the letter.

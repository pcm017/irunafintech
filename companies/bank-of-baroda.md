# Bank of Baroda

## One-liner

India's third-largest public sector bank by business size, with a significant international franchise (~24% international advance growth) and among the lowest cost-of-deposits in the PSU banking system at 4.78%.

## Current state

Q4FY26 (May 2026): Bank of Baroda delivered record quarterly PAT of ₹5,616 cr (+11.2% YoY) and record FY26 PAT of ₹20,021 cr — both "highest ever" milestones. Global business crossed ₹30 lakh cr. Advance growth of 16.2% significantly exceeded BoB's own guidance of 11–13%, driven by RAM segments: agri +20.7%, retail +17.9%, MSME +15.6%. Asset quality is the best in a decade: GNPA 1.89%, SMA1+2 at 0.18%, PCR at 93.94%. The headline Q4 credit cost of 0.76% is a one-quarter head-fake — ₹1,500 cr was a voluntary ECL transition floating provision; underlying credit cost was 0.32%. NIM stabilized at 2.89% (+10 bps QoQ), with domestic NIM at 3.04%. The NIM management challenge for FY27 is deposit stickiness (4.78%) vs asset repricing opportunity (T-bill linked loans to MCLR). Capital raise pipeline: ₹8,500 cr equity + ₹6,000 cr AT-1/Tier 2. ROA >1% for 15 consecutive quarters.

## Thesis / view

Bank of Baroda's turnaround narrative (FY21–FY26) is now complete: from stressed PSU bank to sustained ROA >1%, record profits, lowest-in-class cost of deposits (4.78%), and best-in-decade asset quality. The market question is whether this gets re-rated like private peers or stays at a PSU discount. The discount is partly structural (lower CASA vs private banks: 38.9% vs HDFC 34%, Kotak 43%), partly capital structure (needs equity dilution every 2–3 years to fund growth), and partly international franchise risk (24.4% international advance growth — exposure to geopolitical disruptions in trade corridors).

The ECL transition is a manageable event (~18 bps CET1 impact per management). The bigger FY27 story is NIM management: deposit stickiness at 4.78% (management explicitly stated no room for further reduction) means the NIM lever must come from repricing T-bill-linked assets to MCLR as the rate environment normalizes post West Asia conflict. NIM guidance of 2.75–2.95% is achievable with this lever.

The ₹8,500 cr equity raise overhang is a structural feature of PSU banking — but BoB's ROE at 15.39% FY26 and improving trajectory means the dilution is value-accretive if deployed into advances growing at 12–14%.

## Contradictions

Flagged (2026-06-22): the transcript describes the ₹1,500 cr ECL-transition floating provision as a discrete Q4FY26 action, but the investor presentation's provisions bridge shows only ₹190 cr in the "Provision for Standard Advances" line for Q4FY26 alone (vs ₹1,204 cr for the full FY26 year in that same line). The ₹1,500 cr figure does not map cleanly onto any single disclosed line item in the presentation — possibly spread across FY26 cumulative standard-advances provisioning rather than booked entirely in Q4. Needs reconciliation against the next quarter's disclosures or a direct read of the full presentation's provisioning notes.

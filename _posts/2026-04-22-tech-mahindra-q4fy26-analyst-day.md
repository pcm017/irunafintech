**TL;DR**
- FY26 is TechM's "Stabilisation Phase" — flattish revenue growth but EBIT margin expanded to **12.6% (+290 bps YoY)** from 9.7% in FY25; 10 straight quarters of margin expansion
- Revenue run rate approaching $1B quarterly; LTM large deal wins **$3.8B** (+41.6% latest quarter to $3,794 Mn)
- **Free cash flow: $616 Mn** (115% FCF-to-PAT ratio); highest ever dividend declared in FY26
- AI transformation metrics: 80% AI trained, 76% certified; 350+ service-specific agents created; 7% revenue-per-employee improvement from AI
- Q4 FY26 EBIT: **13.8%** (highest in 5 quarters; trajectory: Q4 FY25 10.5% → Q1 FY26 11.1% → Q2 12.1% → Q3 13.1% → Q4 13.8%)
- FY27 roadmap: "Reaping Returns" phase — topline growth > peer average, industry-standard margins, high predictability
- Key risks flagged: tariff war, H-1B regulatory changes, agentic AI wave, rise of GCCs in India, macro-economic challenges

**Key data / quotes**
- EBIT % trajectory: 10.5% (Q4 FY25) → 11.1% → 12.1% → 13.1% → **13.8%** (Q4 FY26)
- FY26 EBIT: **12.6% (+290 bps)** from FY25's 9.7%; target FY24 was 15.0%
- LTM Deal Wins: $3,168M (Q4 FY25) → $2,680M → $2,955M → $3,519M → **$3,794M** (Q4 FY26); +41.6% in the quarter
- Free cash flow: **$616 Mn** (115% FCF to PAT ratio)
- Revenue YoY CC growth: Q1 FY26 −1.0% → Q2 −0.3% → Q3 +1.3% → Q4 **+2.4%** (acceleration — first positive trajectory in 5 quarters)
- AI metrics: 80% client-facing talent AI-ready; 76% workforce AI-certified; 350+ service agents created; 2 LLMs; ~95% Turbocharge clients have GenAI embedded
- Revenue per employee: +7% improvement in FY26 from AI productivity
- Deal win cohort: 182 clients added; 63 "must-have" accounts added; $20Mn+ accounts reached $1B quarterly run rate
- ROCE: Q4 FY26 26.90% (Q4 FY25: 22.60%)
- Dividend: highest ever declared in FY26; 91% FCF as dividend
- Portfolio company integrations: BPS + Comviva — 100% front, middle, back office integration achieved
- NPS: 29% YoY increase, 142% over two years — industry-leading
- Project Fortius: structured margin discipline via utilization rigor, delivery governance, pricing control — still in progress
- FY27 "top bets": chatbots-to-agentic wave, GCC rise in India, cybersecurity, H-1B regulatory risk, macro headwinds
- New commercial model: token-based pricing and "vector squads" — moving beyond billable hours
- "Beyond the Billable Hour": Vector Squads and Service Tokens as AI-native commercial model
- Orion: GA (generally available) + live on 3 hyperscaler marketplaces; 350+ pre-built agent library

**Signals**
- TechM's turnaround is real but fragile — margin expansion has come predominantly from cost (Project Fortius) not revenue growth; until revenue growth exceeds peer average, the recovery is cost-driven, not demand-driven
- The +2.4% CC growth in Q4 FY26 is the first sign of demand-side recovery after 5 quarters of decline; FY27 is the "proof year"
- AI transformation metrics (80% trained, 350+ agents) are among the most detailed AI disclosure from any India IT firm — positions TechM alongside Bajaj Finance as an "active rebuilder" rather than "passive waiter"
- Token-based pricing and vector squads represent a genuine commercial model innovation: shifting from time-billed to outcome-billed AI delivery. If this scales, it disrupts the traditional IT T&M / fixed-price model that sustains TCS/Infosys/Wipro's revenue base
- GPUaaS for telcos (mentioned in May 24 tech digest) is a new revenue stream being evaluated — aligns with TechM's telecom heritage (Communications vertical) and could be a differentiating bet
- H-1B regulatory risk is TechM's most India-specific macro risk — more than TCS or Infosys because TechM has a higher onshore delivery ratio and shorter bench depth

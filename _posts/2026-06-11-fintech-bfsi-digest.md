## TL;DR

- **US consumer credit is bending, not broken — yet:** Bankruptcies +14% YoY; new card vintages (Q1-Q2 2025) delinquent at higher rates than 2023-24 cohorts; subprime auto delinquencies at 20-year highs; personal savings near historic lows. "A system with less room for error than a year ago." India lenders with US-origin capital or US-linked risk models should monitor closely.
- **RBI annual report: lending boom with fewer borrowers** — India is running a credit expansion (bank credit +16.2% YoY) but the base of active borrowers is shrinking. Credit is concentrating. Systemic risk implication: if quality deteriorates, there's less distribution of losses.
- **RBI FDI measures may unlock $70Bn inflows** — new 15-, 30-, 40-year G-Sec maturities added to Fully Accessible Route (FAR); ETBFSI reports $70Bn potential inflow estimate. Banks' spreads face pressure as funding landscape shifts.
- **Zepto IPO arrives as a risk event**: "high-risk, unfinished, cash-guzzling" company; overtook Instamart in order count but trails Blinkit; SEBI lowered bar for loss-making startups to list. The market will price public appetite for pre-profitability growth stories.
- **SBI fraud signal**: ₹6,300 Cr banking frauds in 3 years; UPI identified as the single largest fraud gateway. Shadow AI (unauthorized AI tools in enterprises) flagged as a new exposure vector.

## Key data / quotes

**US consumer credit stress (Alex Johnson / Dave Wasik, Fintech Takes):**
- Bankruptcies: **+14% YoY** (Feb/Mar/Apr all well above historical seasonal pattern — seasonal pattern broke)
- Card vintages: Q1-Q2 2025 cohorts already delinquent at higher rates than 2023 and 2024 cohorts; "early vintage performance is highly predictive. If they start bad, they stay bad."
- Subprime auto: **delinquencies at 20-year highs**; borrowers stretching to 7-year terms (vs. standard 5-year); "the borrower ends up owing more than the car is worth, sometimes for years"
- University of Michigan consumer sentiment: **worst reading in survey's long history** — future sentiment "significantly worse" than current
- K-shaped economy: top 10–20% earners still OK (~50–60% of spending); if they crack, "yellow flags change to red"
- Personal savings rate: near historic lows
- Regulatory patchwork: Trump April 2025 EO revoked disparate-impact credit rules at federal level; California's Newsom-Chopra agency filling void — lenders face inconsistent state-level requirements

**India RBI annual report (The Daily Briefing):**
- India is experiencing a lending boom but with a **shrinking base of active borrowers**
- Credit is concentrating in fewer borrower hands

**RBI FDI / FAR expansion (ETBFSI):**

- Potential inflows: **$70Bn** (ETBFSI estimate)
- Banks' spreads may face pressure as funding landscape deepens
- India-US $70Bn estimate vs. the June 5 RBI capital inflow package (~$5B/month) — FAR expansion is the longer-duration structural play

## Signals

- **US credit stress → India BFSI monitoring:** Indian NBFCs with US-linked funding (ECB, ADR, FII investors) should watch for contagion in risk appetite. Specific concern: if US subprime auto stress deepens, US investors de-risk EM consumer lending — India NBFC valuations could re-rate. Not imminent, but directional.
- **RBI lending boom / fewer borrowers** confirms the credit concentration theme that the June 6-9 digests flagged (corporate deposits filling 400bps credit-deposit gap; bank credit +16.2% YoY with deposit growth +12.2%). If the active borrower base is shrinking while credit grows, average ticket sizes and risk exposure per borrower are rising. Watch for GNPA inflection.
- **FAR $70Bn inflow potential** is large relative to current forex reserve movements (~$0.9Bn WoW). If realised, provides material INR support and RBI room to allow more rate flexibility. Timeline: 12–24 months for institutional positioning to build.
- **Zepto IPO** is a market confidence litmus test for India's pre-profitability growth story. If it prices well and performs post-listing, it opens the pipeline for Meesho, PhysicsWallah, Ola Electric round 2, etc. If it fails, the IPO window narrows for loss-making tech.
- **UPI-as-fraud-gateway** is a regulatory and reputational risk for NPCI, SEBI, and the broader India fintech ecosystem. If fraud volumes scale with UPI's 23.2Bn/month transaction base, NPCI will face pressure for stronger authentication mandates — potential friction on transaction growth.
- **Fed 4% effective target** re-prices India's capital inflow math. If the India-US spread compresses further (US rates higher, India holds), the attractiveness of RBI's June capital inflow package diminishes at the margin.

## Signals

1. **Oil price at $104 Brent is a macro regime-changer for India.** India sources ~60% of seaborne crude through Hormuz-linked routes. At $100+ crude for a sustained period, India's oil import bill rises materially, widening CAD and putting INR under pressure. India's FY27 budget assumption was likely ~$75–80/bbl. Every $10/bbl above assumption adds ~$12–15 Bn to the import bill (~0.3% of GDP).
2. **Yield curve normalisation supports medium-cycle optimism.** US 10Y–2Y at +49 bps (un-inverted since mid-2025) removes the recession signal that was priced for much of 2024–2025. If curve remains normal, growth trajectory in the US is still intact despite Hormuz disruption.
3. **India CPI at 3.4% gives RBI room — but Hormuz can take that away.** RBI has neutral stance at 5.25%. If oil sustains above $100 and food inflation rises (Kharif fertiliser risk), imported inflation feeds through by Q2–Q3 FY27, potentially constraining RBI cuts.
4. **FII selling offset by DII strength.** ₹14,275 Cr FII selling MTD in May 2026 — but DIIs have absorbed >₹3 lakh Cr net Jan–Apr 2026. Retail SIP flows and LIC allocation create structural demand floor for India equities even during FII selling episodes.
5. **India corporate earnings Q4 FY26 broadly strong** (India Policy Watch: sales +16% YoY, EBIT +11%, 42% beat rate). Domestic demand resilient. Geopolitical stress not yet visible in reported numbers — forward guidance is where the caution is emerging.
6. **Gold at $4,730 is near all-time high** — risk-off asset premium building. Real yield environment still challenging for USD bulls; central bank buying continuing.

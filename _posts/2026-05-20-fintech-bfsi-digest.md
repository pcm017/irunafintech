## TL;DR

- **RBI flagging AI cybersecurity risks to banks** — adversarial attacks, model drift, data poisoning in AI credit/fraud systems; RBIH accelerating MuleHunter.ai adoption
- **RBI tightening ODI scrutiny** — $27B India Inc. overseas investments under OI framework review; layered structures and round-tripping targeted
- **AI deepfake KYC fraud** — fraudsters using AI-generated deepfakes and voice clones to bypass KYC at banks and NBFCs; multiple Q4 FY26 incidents reported to RBI/CERT-In
- **MobiKwik NBFC licence** — RBI approval (April 27); aims to begin disbursals by end-2026 via subsidiary MFSPL
- **ChatGPT personal finance** — OpenAI launched bank-account-connecting PFM feature; direct threat to fintech aggregators; Indian ChatGPT users generated 1 billion images since launch (platform depth)
- **Circle Arc** — $222M token presale, $3B valuation; USDC on 34 blockchains; agentic payments (AI agents paying autonomously) as key use case

## Signals

- **AI cyber risk now officially a priority for RBI:** The regulator's discussions with banks on adversarial AI attacks signal that AI governance in BFSI is moving from guideline to active supervisory focus. Banks deploying AI in credit/fraud will face increasing scrutiny of model risk management.
- **MuleHunter.ai as industry standard:** RBIH pushing adoption signals a move toward centralized/shared AI fraud infrastructure in India — could compress margins of banks doing their own fraud analytics.
- **MobiKwik NBFC play:** Follows the Paytm playbook of building a lending balance sheet to diversify from payments. Key questions: underwriting capability, capital adequacy, and whether MobiKwik's user base is credit-eligible.
- **ChatGPT PFM = existential threat for aggregators:** If OpenAI captures the personal finance data layer, it displaces Mint-type apps AND could build a credit intelligence moat. India context: aggregation via Account Aggregator (AA) framework — OpenAI would need AA integration to tap India's financial data.
- **Circle Arc / agentic payments:** The "AI agent pays for the user" use case is Circle's big bet. As AI agents gain financial autonomy (book hotels, buy services), the payment rails they use matter enormously. Circle's USDC-on-Arc positioned for this.
- **Bond vigilantes → India G-Sec implications:** Higher US long yields compressing India–US spread narrows the carry. FII debt flows to India under pressure — watch for sustained FPI selling.

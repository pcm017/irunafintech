## TL;DR

- **RBI re-sanctions smartphone device locking for EMI defaults** — a practice banned in 2024 is back with regulatory blessing; major implication for device-linked lending (consumer durables, handset finance).
- **Bajaj Finance succession deep-dive (The Ken):** Annual "Ruminate" retreats where leadership red-teams the company from the floor up — institutionally rare. The challenge now is succession, given how founder/CEO-centric the model has been under Rajeev Jain.
- **SoFi + Airwallex + Stripe all betting on stablecoins for global rails** — three major fintech players converging on stablecoin-based cross-border payment infrastructure.
- **Agentic payments asymmetry:** Agent economy has built buyer-side infrastructure; merchant-side tooling (accepting, settling, reconciling AI-agent payments) is severely underdeveloped — a major gap in the agentic stack.
- **SC 28% GST on online gaming retrospectively upheld** — confirmed from prior day; significant financial exposure for Dream11, Games24x7, MPL.

## Key Data / Quotes

**Device locking (digital lending):**
> "A practice the RBI banned in 2024 has returned, this time with the regulator's blessing." — The Ken

- Lenders can now remotely disable smartphone functionality for missed EMIs. RBI previously banned this in 2024 citing borrower protection concerns but has reversed position.
- Implications: Poonawalla Fincorp, Bajaj Finance (Consumer Durables / Electronics), and device-linked NBFC lenders benefit from improved collection leverage.

**Bajaj Finance (The Ken):**
> "Every November or so, Bajaj Finance's senior leadership lets loose by travelling to places like Kodaikanal or the Himalayas, trekking over rocky outcrops, and 'tearing their company apart.' 'Ruminate', as the non-banking financial company's retreat is dubbed, is somewhat of an annual ritual."

- The institutional culture of self-disruption is the Bajaj Finance moat. The succession question: this culture requires active stewardship that may not survive a CEO transition.

**Stablecoin convergence:**
- SoFi, Airwallex, and Stripe are all making the same bet on stablecoin-based cross-border payment rails.
- Infrastructure race is "very real" — whoever wins the rails layer wins the B2B payment stack.

**Agentic payments gap:**
> "The agent economy has built the buyer side. But the merchant side has barely started." — Lex Sokolin

- AI agents can initiate payments; merchants lack the tooling to accept, settle, and reconcile agent-initiated transactions.

**AI in consumer credit (Fintech Takes):**
- AI fully automating: bureau pulls, data aggregation, identity verification.
- Human judgment still needed: exception handling, adverse action notices, collections conversations.
- Question: does AI remove friction appropriately or remove the human backstop protecting borrowers?

**Byju's:**
- Byju Raveendran jailed by Singapore court for contempt — marks serious escalation; $22B → zero valuation trajectory.

**BIS insurer resolution framework:**
- BIS releases insurer resolution framework addressing systemic implications of insurer failures.

## Signals

- **Device locking return = credit discipline tool:** RBI's reversal signals the regulator is prioritising NPA reduction and lender rights over the 2024 consumer protection framing. For NBFCs in consumer durables (Bajaj Finance, Poonawalla Fincorp), this materially improves collection leverage on phone-linked EMIs. Watch for NPA improvement in Q1/Q2 FY27 in device-linked portfolios.
- **Bajaj Finance: culture > moat:** The Ken framing builds on the prior "Intermission" (May 17) and "call centre as moat" (May 23) readings. The convergence: Bajaj Finance's real moat is cultural — the Ruminate retreats, the red-teaming, the self-disruption. The succession risk is therefore about preserving culture, not just capability.
- **Stablecoin rails race:** SoFi + Airwallex + Stripe converging on stablecoins. India's cross-border payment stack (UPI One World, NPCI International) faces a structural challenge if dollar-denominated stablecoin rails win in corridors India doesn't dominate.
- **Agentic merchant gap = opportunity:** Lex Sokolin's framing identifies a major white space. India's B2B payments stack (Cashfree, Razorpay, PayU) that wins the "merchant agent SDK" layer early will have a structural advantage.
- **Byju's finale:** Singapore jail signals creditors have exhausted negotiation. India's edtech sector's ZIRP-era excess is being decisively resolved.
- **Anthropic funding ("Breathtaking"):** Consistent with earlier signals (Anthropic valuation reaching $1.4T implied in mid-May). A large new round in late May consolidates Anthropic's position as the benchmark AI company for enterprise trust and capability.

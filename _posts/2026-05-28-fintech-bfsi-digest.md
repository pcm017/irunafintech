# Fintech & BFSI Digest — 2026-05-28

## TL;DR

- **SBI pivots to wealth management:** India's largest PSB is targeting leadership in wealth architecture — a strategic shift alongside HDFC Bank and Kotak Private in the wealth space.
- **Bajaj Finance succession risk:** The Ken deep-dives on how Bajaj Finance mastered self-disruption (annual "Ruminate" retreats, red-teaming, internal competitive tension) but faces the harder challenge of leadership succession after Rajeev Jain.
- **Supreme Court: 28% GST retrospective on online gaming upheld** — major ruling affecting Dream11, Games24x7, MPL; fintech/payments exposure to gaming platforms at risk.
- **BIS Project Agorá reaches real-value testing:** Tokenisation of unified central bank + commercial bank money demonstrably improves wholesale cross-border payments.
- **Robinhood adds agentic AI to trading:** Retail brokerage agent capability expanding — AI agents now executing trades autonomously within Robinhood accounts.
- **Starling Bank 2.5× Monzo's profit on half the revenue** + £70M SaaS pipeline (Engine platform) — B2B banking-as-a-service as neobank differentiation strategy.

## Key Data / Quotes

- **Coal India OFS:** Drew ₹19,000 crore in bids — signals institutional appetite for PSU equity divestment, positive for disinvestment pipeline.
- **India–Russia rare earths:** Deepening strategic cooperation in rare earth minerals — linked to India's semiconductor ambitions and supply chain diversification away from China.
- **PFC strategy:** Shifting capital allocation toward renewables and energy transition financing; gas-led growth for ONGC.
- **Bank FD rates rising:** Credit-deposit ratio tightening; FD rate increases expected — supportive of NIM recovery for banks in Q1 FY27.
- **LIC margins improving:** Potential re-rating trigger — margin trajectory strengthening.
- **SEBI relaxing debt disclosure norms:** Chairperson Pandey signals potential easing for debt issuers — could improve corporate bond market depth.
- **Ather Energy insurance subsidiary:** IRDAI application filed — embedded insurance in EV/mobility sector expanding.
- **Drip Capital + YES BANK:** Trade finance fintech partners with YES BANK for cross-border trade lending.
- **Zerohash national trust bank charter:** Crypto infrastructure company applying for regulated bank status (CLARITY Act context).
- **"Who Pays for the Pipes?"** — Fintech Takes examines payment infrastructure economics; sustainability of current pricing models questioned as AI increases transaction volumes.

## Signals

- **Bajaj Finance succession = the key risk:** The Ken's framing confirms institutional awareness that Rajeev Jain is Bajaj Finance in many respects. The red-teaming culture is institutionally rare (annual Ruminate retreats) but the succession challenge is real — see *bajaj finance* Contradictions section.
- **SC gaming GST ruling:** Retrospective 28% GST on online gaming is now confirmed — financial exposure for Dream11, MPL, Games24x7 could run to thousands of crores. Cross-platform payments processing companies (Razorpay, Cashfree) that process gaming transactions face indirect exposure.
- **Starling vs. Monzo contrast:** Starling generates 2.5× Monzo's profit on half the revenue — driven by B2B Engine SaaS platform (£70M pipeline). Highlights the divergence between neobanks with B2B moats (Starling) vs. consumer scale (Monzo).
- **Agentic finance arriving in retail:** Robinhood's agent launch marks the first mainstream US retail brokerage deployment of autonomous trading agents. Pattern to watch: similar launches in India (Groww, Zerodha, Angel One).
- **BIS tokenisation real-value testing:** Regulatory-grade endorsement of tokenised money infrastructure. Relevant for India's CBDC (e-rupee) and UPI evolution.
- **Bank FDs + NIM recovery:** If FD rates rise and the credit-deposit ratio normalises, bank NIM recovery supports BFSI equity thesis for FY27. Positive signal for HDFC Bank, Axis Bank, ICICI Bank.

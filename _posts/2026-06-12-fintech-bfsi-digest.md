## Signals

**RBI trifecta of credit tightening:** Three simultaneous actions — 135 NBFC cancellations, REIT/InvIT lending restriction, FCNR(B) rate hikes — signal RBI is proactively tightening the credit supply structure ahead of a projected FY27 credit slowdown (ICRA: 11–11.7% vs 16.2% FY26). This is not crisis management; it's cycle management.

**FCNR(B) rate hike follows the 2013 playbook:** When INR was under severe pressure in 2013, Raghuram Rajan launched FCNR(B) deposits at attractive rates to pull in NRI forex. The same mechanism is being triggered now. Historically, a successful FCNR(B) mobilisation stabilises INR within 6–8 weeks. If successful, this becomes a RBI policy win; if uptake is weak, it signals deeper structural INR pressure from the West Asia conflict.

**REIT/InvIT lending restriction is a significant real estate credit signal:** Only listed entities can now receive bank credit. This eliminates a large portion of project finance for developers who haven't listed their real estate investment vehicles. Combined with the NBFC cleanup, this reduces the risk of unregulated shadow-banking credit flowing into real estate. Directionally: higher-grade listed entities benefit; unlisted developers face credit constraints.

**Agentic AI governance gap is becoming a systemic concern:** The FSB/BIS/IOSCO watchdog flag is significant — it moves agentic AI in finance from a fintech debate to a central bank/regulator debate. India's RBI has been cautious about GenAI deployment in banking (2025 guidelines on AI governance). Expect RBI to reference this FSB framework in its next policy communication.

**Visa/Mastercard volume problem = the "build vs. use" gap in payments:** Both networks built agentic payment infrastructure in 2025–26. Real transaction volumes by AI agents remain thin. This is the core paradox of agentic finance: the plumbing is ready but the pipes are mostly dry. Watch for whether AI agent adoption in enterprise treasury, procurement, and B2B payments accelerates through 2026.

**SpaceX IPO as a market milestone:** With SpaceX priced on June 11, the IPO market has its first true mega-listing of 2026. Chamath's framing of SpaceX as an AI compute/broadband infrastructure play (rather than just a rocket company) has important implications: it suggests the market will price SpaceX at tech/AI multiples rather than aerospace multiples — with valuation outcomes accordingly.

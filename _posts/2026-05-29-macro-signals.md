## Signals

- **India sold off on Hormuz re-escalation:** Nifty −547 points on May 28 (−2.3%) — PSU banks, metals, power led the decline. IT and telecom defensive. This is the market's read: Hormuz deal is not imminent; Iran revenge threats are credible.
- **INR reversal:** 95.40 → 96.09 in two days — the rupee is sensitive to every Hormuz update. RBI intervention likely active again. If Iran attack on US assets materialises, INR could retest 97+.
- **India-US yield spread widening:** At ~260 bps (vs 250 bps two days ago), India G-Sec is rising as oil/WPI pressures persist. This compresses FII debt flows and adds to rupee pressure.
- **Natural Gas +5.81%:** Overlooked signal — Hormuz disruption is now seeping into gas markets. This has implications for India's power sector costs and LNG import bills.
- **BoK hawkishness:** South Korea raising rates while India holds (constrained) is a reminder that EM central bank divergence is widening. Capital will flow to higher-rate EM markets if India can't cut.
- **US records vs India selloff:** The US-India divergence is stark — S&P +0.58% and Nifty −2.3% on the same day. US dollar-denominated safe-haven + AI capex narrative vs. India oil-dependent macro.

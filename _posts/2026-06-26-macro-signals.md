## Signals

- **WTI below $70 = strongest crude relief signal of 2026.** India's annualised crude import savings vs. May WTI $111 peak now exceed $170B+ at current levels. Tanker rate surge 897% is a partial offset — shipping costs rising even as cargo value falls. Net: India's crude import bill is still materially lower, but the tanker rate spike introduces a new friction in the supply chain normalisation.

- **US 10Y fell 9bps to 4.40% in a single session** — oil deflation feeding through to US inflation expectations, releasing bond market pressure. India's FII debt inflows (₹23,600 Cr MTD, 2-year monthly high) are the direct downstream: lower US yields + India's G-Sec tax exemption + FCNR(B) expansion = India bond flows at their strongest since the JPMorgan GBI-EM inclusion.

- **Micron beat materially alters AI narrative.** After weeks of "AI spending skepticism" (Amazon/Walmart cuts, Broadcom guidance miss), Micron's $41.5B Q3 revenue and $50B Q4 guidance is the clearest counter-signal: AI hardware demand is real and accelerating. Data center >$100B annualised is a new milestone. This supports India's semiconductor infrastructure thesis and reaffirms global AI capex trajectory.

- **RBI AI kill-switch proposal = governance ahead of adoption.** RBI is the first major central bank globally to propose specific AI governance mechanisms for regulated entities — kill-switch, independent validation, explainability, board accountability. Operationally, banks with embedded AI in credit scoring and fraud detection face a new compliance layer. For fintech lenders and NBFCs using credit bureau + ML models: this raises the governance bar.

- **India G-Sec spread +244bps (vs prior 230bps tightest).** Minor widening from the post-deal tightest, but FII debt flows are still accelerating. The ₹23,600 Cr June MTD pace is the market confirmation that India's three-pillar capital inflow playbook (FCNR(B) + G-Sec exemption + LCR pricing) is working.

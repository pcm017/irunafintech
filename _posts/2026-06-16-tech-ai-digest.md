## Signals

**Anthropic's US model pullback is a watershed sovereign AI moment.** The US government ordering a private AI company to pull both its Mythos and Fable lines — including Claude Fable 5, which was already reviewed by external parties — establishes that advanced AI models are now treated as controlled technology similar to export-controlled hardware. This has three immediate India implications: (1) Any India enterprise or government agency relying on US frontier AI models faces unilateral supply-chain risk. (2) NITI Aayog reviewing local AI infrastructure is the correct institutional response. (3) Sarvam AI's $234M raise, timed within days of this episode, captures a new narrative premium: Indian-language, India-sovereign AI as a necessity rather than an aspiration.

**Stratechery's "safety superpower" thesis changes the Anthropic investment narrative.** If Anthropic's safety positioning gives it license to challenge US government directives — and still raise capital at ~$965B implied valuation — then safety is not a cost centre but a pricing moat. For India's Fable 5-dependent users (TCS-Anthropic 50K partnership, enterprise AI deployments): the model pull creates short-term disruption but medium-term opportunity to negotiate direct API agreements with model providers at sovereign access terms.

**SpaceX's "unbundled IPO" changes late-stage private company exit calculus.** No traditional roadshow, direct listing variant. If successful at >$2T market cap (per June 15 reports), this template will be replicated by Anthropic, OpenAI, and others. India implication: the IPO playbook shift affects how Indian VCs and late-stage growth funds (SoftBank Vision, Tiger Global) plan secondary liquidity. SpaceX + Russell 2000 record high on the same day = textbook risk-on signal; US small-cap rally suggests the Iran deal relief is broadening beyond large-cap.

**TSMC's 11x foundry lead is self-reinforcing.** At Q2 guidance of $39–40.2B (+10% QoQ), TSMC's quarterly revenue now approaches semiconductor equipment makers' annual revenues. Apple evaluating Intel 18A-P and Samsung for A21 is a hedge, not a primary shift — TSMC's CoWoS packaging for AI chips is irreplaceable at scale. The WFE market at $145B (+32% YoY) confirms that capital investment in fabs is accelerating, not slowing; India's ATMP-first approach (ISM 2.0) remains the right short-term bet while advanced node fabs mature globally.

**US export control closure is structurally bullish for India's semiconductor mission.** By extending controls to all Chinese-headquartered firms globally, the US forecloses the "offshore China affiliate" workaround. The Remote Access Security Act closing the cloud GPU loophole (if Senate passes) further constrains Chinese AI development timelines. For India: the US is actively incentivising non-China alternatives; India's ISM 2.0 with 12 approved fabs and $10B in subsidies is now more strategically attractive to US allies (TSMC, Intel, Micron — all have India exposure via ISM or ATMP) who want to diversify from China.

# Tech & AI Digest — 2026-07-04

## TL;DR

- **Meta in talks with Samsung Foundry for a >10 trillion won (~$7.2Bn) custom AI chip order** — seeking to diversify away from TSMC, whose lead times now exceed a year amid aggressive 6-month chip iteration cycles.
- **TSMC 2026 capex guided at $52-56Bn** (up 27-40% YoY); Fab 21 Phase 2 (Arizona) equipment move-in targeted Q3 2026; Apple also reportedly exploring Intel/Samsung foundries for A21 chips.
- **Palantir CEO Alex Karp publicly and sharply criticizes OpenAI and Anthropic** ("Something Has Gone Completely Wrong") — a new, notable instance of AI-industry public friction between major leadership figures.
- **Claude Fable 5 is back**, with a closing free-plan inclusion window — a builder-monetization signal.
- **India Semiconductor Mission**: 13 approved projects as of May 2026; Bhiwadi ATMP live; VLSI design startups raised $92M YTD; ISM 2.0 gets ₹1,000 Cr FY27 allocation.
- **BIS export-control bifurcation continues**: NVIDIA's China AI-chip market share down to ~50% (from >90% pre-controls) under the case-by-case review regime.

## Signals

**Meta-Samsung talks are a direct consequence of TSMC capacity constraints extending through 2027** — this is a clean Change Detection + Root Cause pairing: the change (Meta diversifying its custom silicon foundry away from TSMC) is driven by a named, quantifiable cause (TSMC's >1-year lead times under 6-month iteration cycles). The sizing is meaningful: a >$7Bn order is large enough to matter for Samsung's foundry turnaround thesis, and Apple's parallel exploration of Intel/Samsung for A21 suggests TSMC's near-monopoly on leading-edge foundry demand is cracking for the first time in years — a genuine structural shift in AI chip supply chain concentration, not vendor noise.

**Karp's public criticism of OpenAI/Anthropic is a notable escalation in AI-industry public discourse** — though the specific substance wasn't captured in this digest's snippet-only extraction, the mere fact of a major AI-adjacent CEO (Palantir, itself a significant AI/defense-tech player) going public with sharp criticism of the two leading US frontier labs is worth tracking as a sentiment/positioning signal, especially set against the same-week Claude Fable 5 relaunch.

**India's semiconductor mission continues its steady, incremental build-out** (13 approved projects, Bhiwadi and Sanand OSAT facilities operational, $92M in VLSI design funding) — none of this is a step-change from prior digests, but the consistency of execution (facilities opening on schedule, funding flowing to design startups) is itself the signal: India's ATMP-and-design-first strategy (as opposed to a leading-edge fab bet) continues to avoid the EU Chips Act's execution-speed mismatch flagged in earlier wiki entries.

## Signals

**Gold/silver rally is a clean Change Detection + Root Cause case:** both metals broke out (+1.25% / +2.48% in a single session) on a specific, identifiable trigger — the June US jobs report missing forecast by roughly half (57K vs. ~110-115K). This is the clearest data-driven macro signal of the week; unlike prior gold moves this cycle (largely geopolitical/Iran-driven), this one is a pure US-macro-fundamentals move, reinforcing gold's dual character as both a geopolitical and a rates-driven asset in 2026.

**India G-Sec 15-week low (6.71%) continues the multi-week compression trend** that began with the Iran de-escalation and FCNR(B)/FII capital inflow package — but the pace of forex reserve accumulation has slowed sharply (+$0.963Bn this week vs. +$20.46Bn the week before). This is a sizing signal: the exceptional reserve-build pace of late June was a one-off catch-up, not a new sustained run-rate.

**India VIX at 11.98 with Nifty PCR at 1.33 is a mild divergence worth flagging**: very low realized/implied volatility alongside a put-heavy options skew is not a typical pairing (usually low VIX correlates with call-heavy positioning). This could reflect pre-weekend hedging ahead of the US holiday-thinned session, rather than a genuine bearish read — treat as noise pending confirmation Monday.

**Indian banking leadership churn (Kotak CEO exit, HDFC CEO reappointment + new CFO/new hire) is now a multi-week, multi-source confirmed story** — see entity page updates below. ETBFSI's framing ("caretaker vs. transformer") is the most useful lens: Indian banks are entering a multi-year succession-planning stress-test as digital-native competition and AI-cyber risk accelerate.

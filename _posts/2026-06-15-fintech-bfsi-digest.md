### India CPI May 2026 — 3.93%

**Source:** Filter Coffee (June 15, 2026)

India's retail inflation rose to **3.93% in May 2026** (from 3.48% in April), moving closer to but remaining below RBI's 4% target. Food inflation: **4.78%**. Biggest price jumps: gold and silver jewellery, diamonds, platinum, tomatoes, ginger. Potatoes, peas, cumin, cars, two-wheelers saw smaller increases.

Important note: India changed CPI base year to 2024 (from 2011-12) in January 2026 — direct YoY comparisons with 2025 figures are **not valid**.

_India macro: Below-4% CPI + Brent falling = stagflation fears easing. August MPC rate cut becoming more plausible if June CPI also below 4%._

### FCNR(B) Scheme — ₹4,000 Cr Annual Savings for Banks

**Source:** ETBFSI Newsletter (June 15, 2026)

Banks are poised to save approximately **₹4,000 crore annually** by raising deposits through FCNR(B) accounts (RBI covers hedging costs, making it an attractive funding channel). Key numbers:
- NRI dollar deposits offering **up to 7.1%** return (250bps+ over US rates)
- India NRI remittances: **$155 billion in FY25-26** (total)
- Additional **$50 billion** estimated inflow via FCNR(B) scheme
- Scheme runs until **September 2026**
- Bank Nifty traders reversing from bearish to bullish derivative bets on scheme impact
- DBS Bank CEO: India "long-term structural growth opportunity" for AI hardware demand + wealth management

## Signals

**Iran deal confirmation = immediate India macro relief.** Every element of India's energy/currency stress resolves simultaneously: Brent falling toward $75–80 (oil import bill down ₹5,000–7,000 Cr/month), INR recovering, RBI gaining easing space, OMC turning profitable, GCC remittance corridors de-risked. The only residual risk: formal treaty ratification complexity + nuclear verification terms. Watch Switzerland June 19.

**FCNR(B) scheme quantified at ₹4,000 Cr/year bank savings + $50B targeted inflow.** If $50B arrives by September 2026 (scheme expiry), that's ~20% of India's current forex reserve buffer in 3 months. Combined with FII G-Sec inflows from the tax exemption, India's CAD financing picture improves materially. Bulls returning to bank stocks (Bank Nifty +0.85% on June 15, +3.01% on June 12) reflect this.

**Razorpay + NSE IPO signals** are the sharpest bullish indicator for Indian capital markets in months. Post-Paytm, post-LIC volatility, these are high-quality issuers. NSE IPO would be one of the largest in Indian history. Razorpay's IPO timing signals confidence in IPO window despite US-Iran war uncertainty — the confidence is justified given the Iran deal trajectory.

**India CPI May at 3.93%** is a key data point for August MPC. RBI's FY27 CPI forecast is 5.1% (Q3 spike to 5.9%). If Brent falls to $80 and monsoon is normal, Q3 spike may not materialise — that forecast revision is the key variable for RBI's rate-cut timeline. If June CPI also <4%, RBI has a clear window.

**MSME insurance gap (80-85% uninsured)** is a structural fintech opportunity. As digital lending (SMFG, NBFC stack) deepens into MSME, the next wedge product is insurance. This is the India analog to credit card + insurance bundling in mature markets.

# Tech & AI Digest — 2026-07-17

## TL;DR

- **TSMC committed an additional $100Bn to Arizona fabs (2nm logic + advanced packaging), bringing its total US semiconductor investment to $265Bn** — leading-edge wafer demand is running at 110-120% of available capacity.
- **Samsung Foundry has picked up orders from/negotiations with Google, NVIDIA, Tesla, AMD, and BYD** — several previously TSMC-exclusive customers now diversifying; **Apple is reportedly considering shifting some foundry production away from TSMC** toward Samsung/Intel.
- **Intel Foundry's 14A process has reportedly slipped beyond 2030** — further delay in Intel's advanced-node roadmap.
- **NVIDIA's China AI-chip market share has fallen to ~50% from over 90%** under the bifurcated export-control regime; BIS separately levied a record $252M penalty against Applied Materials, alongside easing China chip-license review to case-by-case for H200-class chips (effective Jan 15, 2026).
- India's **Semicon 2.0 (₹1.27 lakh crore) and MPMS (₹62,500 crore) detail firmed up**: ISM 1.0 has 12 projects approved (₹1.64 lakh crore), 3 facilities in commercial production (Micron ATMP, Kaynes Semicon, CG Power OSAT), and 2 new ISM projects approved (CRYSTAL MATRIX Dholera, SUCHI SEMICON Surat) — largely corroborating detail on the Semicon 2.0 Cabinet approval already logged Jul 15/16.
- L&T Technology Services is rolling out Microsoft Copilot to 1.4 lakh employees — a large-scale enterprise-AI adoption move already referenced in the Jul 16 digest, reconfirmed here.

## Signals

**Change detection:** The $100Bn incremental TSMC Arizona commitment and the broadened Samsung Foundry customer list (now including Google and BYD, beyond the NVIDIA/Tesla/Qualcomm set logged Jul 16) mark a genuine widening of the foundry-diversification trend, not just a reconfirmation — more hyperscalers/chip-designers are now actively spreading orders across TSMC, Samsung, and (potentially) Intel simultaneously.

**Root cause traversal:** NVIDIA's China AI-chip share falling to ~50% from 90%+ is the direct, measurable consequence of the BIS export-control regime (even with its Jan 2026 easing to case-by-case review) — the clearest hard-number evidence yet in this wiki of the bifurcated global chip ecosystem's commercial impact on a specific company.

**Sizing / impact:** For India, the Semicon 2.0/MPMS detail (12 ISM 1.0 projects, ₹1.64 lakh crore, 3 already in commercial production) sizes the existing chip ecosystem against the newly approved ₹1.27 lakh crore Semicon 2.0 envelope — a near-doubling of committed incentive capital arriving exactly as TSMC's 2026 2nm capacity is fully booked and Intel's roadmap slips further, strengthening the strategic rationale for India's fab-independent (ATMP/OSAT-first) capacity build tracked since early June.

## Signals

- **Samsung strike averted → KOSPI +7.6%:** The HBM/memory supply chain risk highlighted in prior week's watch list resolved. Samsung stock +6%. Memory chip availability for AI training remains intact near-term. Watch for next union cycle.
- **Iran deal signal → Crude -5–6%:** The sharpest single-day crude drop since the crisis began. If an Iran nuclear deal materialises, meaningful supply (Iran's ~3–4 Mbpd) would return to market. India would benefit disproportionately (lower oil import bill, INR stabilisation, WPI relief). But "final phase" has been said before.
- **INR at record low:** Rupee crossed 96.73 — a new all-time low. The $38B drawdown in reserves since February is significant. At this pace, reserves would reach $650B by year-end. RBI appears to be defending gradually, not aggressively.
- **Global bond rout ongoing:** Germany Bund at highest since May 2011, JGB at highest since 1997. This is a synchronized global sell-off in government bonds — driven by fiscal concerns, not just inflation. The "term premium" is rising globally. No sign of reversal.
- **India bank credit at 15.9% YoY (FY26):** Robust credit growth despite rate hikes — key data point for BFSI sector health. NBFCs, trade, and commercial real estate drove growth. Watch NIM compression risk as deposit costs rise.

# Tech & AI Digest — 2026-07-16

## TL;DR

- **Union Cabinet approved ₹1.27 lakh crore for India Semiconductor Mission 2.0 ("Semicon 2.0")** (Jul 15, chaired by PM Modi) — expected to attract ~₹4 lakh crore in investment and generate ₹2 lakh crore in production during the scheme period; ETTelecom separately reports a related ₹1.9 lakh crore push combining chip and phone manufacturing (likely Semicon 2.0 + MPMS combined).
- **TSMC's entire 2026 2nm capacity is fully booked** (Apple >50%, plus Qualcomm/MediaTek/AMD/NVIDIA); **Intel's 14A has reportedly slipped beyond 2030**; **Samsung Foundry is booming on HBM4 demand**, picking up NVIDIA/Tesla/Qualcomm as new clients.
- **IBM warned the AI boom is squeezing enterprise software budgets**; IBM shares fell in what multiple newsletters independently described as the company's **worst trading day in its 115-year history**.
- **NVIDIA's Feynman GPU (2028)** is reportedly being built on **TSMC's A16 node** (NVIDIA's first 1nm-class mass-produced chip), with possible Intel outsourcing for I/O dies.
- **Emergent, an Indian AI/agentic-tooling startup, reportedly reached unicorn status.**
- **L&T is driving a Microsoft Copilot deployment across 1.4 lakh employees** — a large-scale enterprise AI-adoption data point in India.

## Key data / quotes

- Semicon 2.0: ₹1.27 lakh crore approved; covers chip design, advanced packaging, semiconductor equipment, specialty materials, research, talent development. Expected ~₹4 lakh crore investment attraction, ~₹2 lakh crore production generation.
- Tata Electronics' Dholera fab (with PSMC): ~₹91,000 crore investment, 50,000 wafers/month planned capacity — still the largest actual India semiconductor investment to date.
- TSMC 2nm: fully booked for 2026; Apple >50% of initial capacity.
- Intel 14A: reportedly slipped beyond 2030.
- Samsung Foundry: booming on HBM4 demand; new clients include NVIDIA, Tesla, Qualcomm.
- NVIDIA Feynman GPU: targeted 2028 launch, built on TSMC A16; possible Intel 14A/18A outsourcing for I/O dies plus EMIB packaging.
- US BIS reaffirmed (June 2026 clarification): AI chip export-control licensing requirements apply to all China-headquartered firms' subsidiaries, even those located outside China — effectively re-restricting NVIDIA Blackwell shipments to China-HQ companies outside China.
- IBM: AI boom squeezing enterprise software budgets; shares fell sharply (described elsewhere as worst trading day in 115-year history).
- US reportedly considering a "Standards Body" to regulate frontier AI models.
- Emergent (Indian AI/agentic-tooling startup): reached unicorn valuation.
- L&T (via LTM): driving Microsoft Copilot deployment for 1.4 lakh employees.
- Demis Hassabis (Google DeepMind): proposed a blueprint for AI regulation.
- ILO: nearly 80 million ASEAN workers could see jobs reshaped by AI.
- Early-stage VC deal activity slowing in the Middle East amid the Iran war.

## Signals

**Change detection:** Semicon 2.0's ₹1.27 lakh crore approval is the largest single India-semiconductor policy commitment logged in this wiki to date — nearly double the ₹1.25 lakh crore ISM 2.0 framework figure the wiki flagged clearing the finance ministry's expenditure finance committee on Jul 13, now formally Cabinet-approved two days later.

**Root cause traversal:** The approval is a direct Modi-government industrial-policy action (Cabinet chaired by PM Modi, Jul 15) — the same delivery-mode pattern the wiki has tracked since the Sanand chip-plant inauguration (Jul 6) and the Rs 8,000cr FY27 ISM budget outlay (Jul 14); this is the funding mechanism catching up to the plant-commissioning momentum already underway.

**Sizing / impact:** At ₹1.27 lakh crore (~$15Bn), Semicon 2.0 is a step-change in India's semiconductor policy scale versus ISM 1.0's ~₹1.64 lakh crore cumulative approved-facility figure tracked through May — effectively a near-doubling of the incentive envelope in a single Cabinet decision. Combined with TSMC's fully-booked 2nm capacity and Intel's 14A slippage, the global foundry bottleneck (which has been driving Samsung/Meta/Apple toward alternative fabs since early July) strengthens the strategic case for India's own fab-independent capacity build — though Tata-PSMC's Dholera fab remains the only facility of comparable scale actually under construction.

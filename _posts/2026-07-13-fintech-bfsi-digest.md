## TL;DR

- **HDFC Bank cut its non-supervisory workforce by 8,000+ in FY26** (3,343 net headcount reduction) while ramping management hiring; **Kotak Mahindra Bank's FY26 hiring focused on AI/cloud/engineering talent**, yet overall workforce shrank by 1,269 despite 28,846 new hires — both banks are running an AI-driven headcount restructuring in parallel.
- **HDFC Bank's annual report addressed former chairman Atanu Chakraborty's exit directly**: an independent review found no evidence to substantiate the governance concerns raised at the time — closing a thread the wiki has tracked since June 30.
- **At RBI-licensed NBFCs, 600%+ annualised interest is board-approved and legal** (The Ken) — a sample of 25 NBFCs saw revenues rise a median 4.5x between FY24-FY25, exploiting the RBI's 2022 decision not to cap rates.
- **US bank-charter race intensifies**: Circle got final OCC approval for a national trust bank (Circle National Trust); Klarna filed for a Utah industrial-bank charter; Sony's Connectia Trust got conditional OCC approval — all three explicitly cite funding-cost/stablecoin-infrastructure motives, a direct read-through for AU Small Finance Bank's own universal-banking application.
- **Kalshi lost a New York gambling-law ruling** — its fifth state-level district loss (Maryland, Nevada, Massachusetts, Michigan, New York); Minnesota criminalises sports-contract trading from August 1.
- **Trump-linked ALT5 Sigma's MSwipe subsidiary is tied to "no-KYC" crypto cards marketed for Iran sanctions evasion** (Fintech Business Weekly) — up to $537M of a $750M raise could flow to the Trump family via DT Marks DEFI LLC.
- RBI is unwinding its $100bn short-dollar forward position and stepping up scrutiny of corporate India's foreign investments and AML disclosures.

## Signals

**Change detection:** HDFC Bank and Kotak's simultaneous FY26 headcount reductions (8,000+ and 1,269 respectively), both explicitly tied to AI/automation, mark the first time this wiki has tracked concrete workforce-shrinkage figures at two of India's largest private banks in the same cycle — a shift from "AI investment announced" to "AI headcount impact realised and disclosed."

**Root cause traversal:** The NBFC 600%-interest story and the device-locking-tool return are both direct consequences of the RBI's 2022 decision not to impose an interest-rate ceiling — a regulatory gap two independent Ken investigations have now identified as being actively exploited at scale.

**Sizing / impact:** The US bank-charter wave (Circle, Klarna, Sony) reinforces the AU Small Finance Bank universal-banking-license storyline already tracked in this wiki — the same cost-of-funds compression logic (fintech/NBFC → bank charter to access cheaper deposit funding) is playing out in both markets simultaneously.

# HDFC Bank Q4 FY26 Earnings Presentation

**Presentation date:** April 18, 2026
**Raw file:** `raw/results/q4fy26-earnings-presentation.pdf`

---

## TL;DR

- PAT ₹192 bn (+9.1% YoY) in Q4 FY26; FY26 full-year PAT ₹746.7 bn (+10.9% YoY) — India's largest private bank delivering steady growth post-HDFC merger integration
- NIM stable at 3.38% — management intentionally range-bound while TD repricing tailwind unwinds; ROA 1.96% Q4, 1.94% FY26
- Credit quality excellent: GNPA 1.15% (ex-agri 0.91%), NNPA 0.40%, credit cost 35 bps (down from 48 bps in Q4 FY25)
- Borrowings being structurally reduced: 21% → 11% of liabilities over HDFC merger integration period — balance sheet becoming more deposit-funded
- Subsidiary group (HDB Financial, HDFC Life, HDFC AMC, HDFC ERGO, HDFC Securities) all growing — group ecosystem compounding

## Signals

- **NIM plateau** at 3.38% is a deliberate management choice — HDFC Bank is choosing ROA optimisation over NIM maximisation. TD repricing (40–50 bps still to transmit) will provide tailwind if rate cycle accommodates. NIM will likely recover as borrowings (costlier) decline and deposit mix improves.
- **Credit cost compression** (35 bps from 48 bps) is the most significant improvement: implies either improving asset quality or over-provisioning in prior periods being released. Combined with GNPA 1.15%, this is a best-in-class balance sheet.
- **Borrowings declining structurally** (21% → 11% of liabilities) — this is the most important balance-sheet normalisation happening post-HDFC merger. When completed, CoF will structurally decline, supporting NIM recovery even without rate cuts.
- **Subsidiary flywheel:** HDB Financial (+41% PAT), HDFC Life, HDFC AMC, HDFC ERGO all growing — HDFC Bank is not just a bank but a financial services group generating P&L from five distinct entities. Group ROE will be higher than standalone.
- **CASA dilution** (34% from 38%) is the key watch: HDFC merger brought in a large volume of high-cost borrowings; deposit mix shift back to CASA is ongoing but slow.

# Swiggy Q4 FY26 Shareholders' Letter

**Raw file:** [raw/results/45b61b34-69c4-4e90-a7d8-1e1bbc84b072.pdf](../../raw/results/45b61b34-69c4-4e90-a7d8-1e1bbc84b072.pdf)

## TL;DR

- Food GOV +22.6% YoY to ₹9,005 Cr — 15-quarter high; food Adj EBITDA margin 3.3% (best ever).
- Instamart GOV +68.8% YoY; 1,143 stores in 129 cities; contribution margin improving to −1.1% (March); Adj EBITDA loss ₹858 Cr.
- Platform MTU 25.2M (+27.2% YoY); consolidated Adj EBITDA loss −₹652 Cr (improving from −₹712 Cr Q3).
- Instamart strategy = differentiation (Noice clean food brand, premium upgrades) over price/value war.
- Snacc (quick food) proactively shut down during Q4.
- Dineout: GOV +43% YoY; 52k restaurant partners; EBITDA margin 0.8% — profitable and growing.

### Key quotes (CEO Sriharsha Majety)
- *"Our Food GOV grew 22.6% YoY, which is the highest since demand normalised in the post-COVID period."*
- *"We continue to power ahead on the profitability journey with disciplined improvement in contribution margin and operating leverage with our best ever Adjusted EBITDA margins of 3.3%."*
- On quick commerce strategy: *"every participant in the category has to choose between being a differentiated platform or a value-led/price-led one. Having reflected hard... we feel definitively that the right answer for Instamart's long-term success will be more differentiation-led."*
- On Snacc: *"We proactively shut down Snacc during the quarter as we were not convinced on the steady state size of the category for the incremental complexity involved to run the business."*
- On social security: ~100,000 delivery partners registered on E-Shram — accident, health, life insurance, pension access.

## Signals

1. **Food delivery recovery is structurally real.** 15-quarter high GOV growth driven by volume (orders + users), not AOV — management explicitly notes this is the more durable signal. At 3.3% EBITDA margin, food delivery is a mature, profitable business.

2. **Instamart's contribution margin trajectory is the single most important metric.** From −6%+ in early FY26 to −1.8% in Q4 to −1.1% in March — the slope of improvement suggests Q2/Q3 FY27 could see CM breakeven. If Instamart reaches CM breakeven while maintaining 60%+ GOV growth, the business profile changes dramatically.

3. **Store additions stalled in Q4** (+7 net to 1,143). This needs watching — either it's a deliberate pause to improve unit economics per store, or it signals a real estate / capital constraint. Contrast with Blinkit's +216 stores in the same quarter.

4. **Differentiation vs. value war** in quick commerce is Swiggy's defining strategic bet. If Indian consumers prove willing to pay a premium for quality (Noice, Triply), Swiggy wins a high-margin segment. If price remains the primary selection criterion, Blinkit's cost leadership and scale win.

5. **E-Shram registration for 100k gig workers** is a social signal that may also have regulatory implications — if gig worker protections under Code on Social Security 2020 are enforced, platforms with higher registration rates (Swiggy) may face lower regulatory risk.

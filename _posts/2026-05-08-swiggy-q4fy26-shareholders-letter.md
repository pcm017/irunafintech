## Signals

1. **Food delivery recovery is structurally real.** 15-quarter high GOV growth driven by volume (orders + users), not AOV ‚Äî management explicitly notes this is the more durable signal. At 3.3% EBITDA margin, food delivery is a mature, profitable business.

2. **Instamart's contribution margin trajectory is the single most important metric.** From ‚àí6%+ in early FY26 to ‚àí1.8% in Q4 to ‚àí1.1% in March ‚Äî the slope of improvement suggests Q2/Q3 FY27 could see CM breakeven. If Instamart reaches CM breakeven while maintaining 60%+ GOV growth, the business profile changes dramatically.

3. **Store additions stalled in Q4** (+7 net to 1,143). This needs watching ‚Äî either it's a deliberate pause to improve unit economics per store, or it signals a real estate / capital constraint. Contrast with Blinkit's +216 stores in the same quarter.

4. **Differentiation vs. value war** in quick commerce is Swiggy's defining strategic bet. If Indian consumers prove willing to pay a premium for quality (Noice, Triply), Swiggy wins a high-margin segment. If price remains the primary selection criterion, Blinkit's cost leadership and scale win.

5. **E-Shram registration for 100k gig workers** is a social signal that may also have regulatory implications ‚Äî if gig worker protections under Code on Social Security 2020 are enforced, platforms with higher registration rates (Swiggy) may face lower regulatory risk.

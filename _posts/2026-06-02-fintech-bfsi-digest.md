## TL;DR

- Rupee structural analysis (Fintech Inside #109): INR moved from 83/$ to 97/$ in 12 months (took 9 years to move from 64→83). Three structural forces + four acute shocks identified. The rupee moved from most overvalued in years to genuinely undervalued — a "permanent new floor" thesis.
- Indian banking stress signals: NBFC margins under pressure from rising bond yields/funding costs; West Asia heat beginning to affect bank books; credit card perks being quietly cut.
- RBI expanding digital rupee (CBDC) pilots — tokenisation and cross-border bilateral pilots.
- Project Agora complete (BIS + 7 CBs + 40 firms incl. JPMorgan, HSBC, Mastercard, Visa, SWIFT) — programmable cross-border settlements. Canada joined.
- Mastercard BitLicense for stablecoin settlement — NYDFS approval lets Mastercard directly transmit and settle stablecoins.
- SoFi stablecoin (SoFiUSD) live on banking app; BIS publishes stablecoin regulation research.

## Key data / quotes

- **Fintech Inside — "97 and Free Fallin':"** INR at 97/$. Nine years to move 64→83; twelve months to move 83→97. "One permanent new floor" — the rupee went from most overvalued in years to genuinely undervalued in 17 months. Analysis: three structural forces (CAD structural deficit, FII outflows, energy import bill) + four acute shocks (Iran war, US tariff uncertainty, FPI outflows, dollar strength). Second/third order for startup founders: dollar-denominated costs rise, SaaS pricing power improves, export-facing business models benefit.
- **DCB Bank CEO Praveen Kutty:** Individual deposits top priority; "20% deposit growth possible without being a top rate payer." Shifting from DSA-led sourcing to organic growth.
- **NBFC margins under pressure:** Rising bond yields + potential rate hikes lifting funding costs. Incremental cost of funds rising significantly for NBFCs. Margin compression ahead.
- **RBI CBDC pilots:** Expanding to tokenisation of financial assets; intends to operationalise bilateral CBDC pilots with partner countries.
- **West Asia heat on banks:** Indian banks anticipate asset quality stress from Q2 FY27 due to West Asia conflict. Rising fuel prices reducing consumer spending — demand-side transmission to credit quality.
- **Credit card perks quietly cut:** Multiple banks reducing rewards, cashback limits, and perks — a margin-preserving move as funding costs rise.
- **Term deposits below 7%:** Share jumped to 61.8% in FY26 after 125bps of policy rate cuts; depositors shifted to longer maturities.
- **Mastercard BitLicense:** NYDFS granted BitLicense to Mastercard Transaction Services — fewer than 50 issued since 2015. Mastercard can now directly transmit and settle stablecoins without a third-party licensed intermediary. Turns stablecoins into core settlement layer.
- **SoFiUSD live:** First US national bank stablecoin on a banking app.
- **Project Agora:** BIS + 7 central banks (Fed NY, BoE, BoJ, BoK, BofMexico, SNB, Banque de France) + 40+ firms. Two-layer architecture: central banks retain full sovereign control; smart contracts enforce AML/sanctions at token level before any cross-border transfer. Canada joined.
- **BIS research:** Minimum reserve requirements can stabilise stablecoin issuers' portfolios but introduce tradeoffs between stability and yield.

## Signals

- **Rupee "permanent new floor" thesis** — if correct, Indian businesses with USD revenue streams (IT exports, remittance-adjacent fintechs) have a structural tailwind. Dollar-denominated liabilities (foreign debt, import-dependent NBFCs) face structural headwind.
- **NBFC margin compression** — rising bond yields + RBI rate hike expectations + Goldman's "2 more hikes" call = multi-quarter NIM pressure for India's NBFC sector. Bajaj Finance, Poonawalla Fincorp, on-emi all affected.
- **West Asia → bank asset quality Q2 FY27** — delayed but real transmission: fuel inflation reduces household cash flow → elevated EMI stress for unsecured and personal loan portfolios.
- **Project Agora + Mastercard BitLicense + SoFi stablecoin** = stablecoin settlement rails are being built by incumbents, not just fintech challengers. India's e-rupee (CBDC) and UPI cross-border strategy must account for this.

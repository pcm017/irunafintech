## Signals

**Change detection:** The Iran conflict has escalated for a third distinct round since the Jul 8 ceasefire breakdown — Brent's move toward $80 is now the sharpest single-day jump of the current flare-up (+~4%), exceeding the pace of the Jul 9-10 Gulf-state strikes.

**Root cause traversal:** The proximate trigger is the US's third strike round plus an IRGC hit on a cargo ship, with Iran's retaliation hitting Bahrain/Kuwait/Qatar — the same GCC-remittance and Hormuz-transit risk channel tracked since May. Gold's inverse move (down on the oil spike) reflects the market pricing near-term inflation/rate risk over safe-haven demand — an unusual but not unprecedented divergence.

**Sizing / impact:** If Brent sustains a push toward/through $80, it directly threatens the August 3-5 RBI MPC rate-cut thesis built on sub-4% CPI and cheap crude — bank credit at 18.6% YoY (a fresh 2-year high) shows the domestic credit cycle still has momentum, so the RBI's calculus will weigh crude-driven imported inflation against continued strong credit growth. The RBI's gradual $100bn dollar-forward unwind is a structural rupee-support measure that pre-dates today's escalation, suggesting the central bank is positioning for continued volatility regardless of how this particular flare-up resolves.

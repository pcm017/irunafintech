### US Macro / Fed

**US CPI April 2026: 3.8% YoY** (up from 3.3% March, 2.4% February)
- Energy: +18% YoY (post-Iran war), responsible for >40% of m/m increase
- Food: highest monthly increase since 2010; tomato prices +15% (2nd month running, Mexico tariffs)
- Core CPI: 2.8% — airfares, Netflix, rental cost adjustment
- Real wage growth: negative — wages grew 3.6% but CPI at 3.8%; first time since 2023 real wages declined
- FedWatch: higher probability of rate hike by year-end despite incoming chair's dovish bias

**Kevin Warsh (new Fed chair, effective ~May 16):**
> "Warsh has similar convictions that the explosion of AI could be the foundation for future rate cuts while avoiding inflation." — Morning Brew
- Senate confirmed 51-45 (slimmest Fed chair margin in history)
- Powell remains as governor through 2028
- Warsh analog to Greenspan (1987): ignored rate hike calls because productivity boom (personal computing) would absorb inflation. Warsh's bet: AI productivity → lower inflation → room to cut. But first, CPI is re-accelerating.

### Geopolitical / Trade

**Trump–Xi summit (China, May 13-14):**
- Two leaders agreed: Hormuz must remain open; Iran cannot have nuclear weapon
- Trade context: US-China "decoupling" ongoing but partial
  - US share of China imports plummeted since mid-2010s
  - Ohio manufacturers: 9% reshored some production to US in 2025 (up from 4% in 2021); 60% from China
  - Transshipment estimated <18% of China's lost US exports (DiPippo analysis)
  - Real story: China moved up value chain to components; final assembly shifted to Vietnam/Mexico/India/Indonesia
  - New arrangement: Chinese components → third-country assembly → US consumers
  - Risk: Trump may trade decoupling progress for a soybean deal or short-term trade terms

## Signals

- **US CPI re-acceleration changes everything for global rates.** At 3.8%, the Fed has no room to cut regardless of Warsh's inclinations. US 10Y at 4.463% (highest since Jul 2025) and a steepening yield curve (+47 bps) — bond markets are pricing in "higher for longer" resuming. This flows into EM risk appetite, FII outflows from India, and dollar strength (DXY 98.49).
- **India macro bifurcation deepening.** CPI 3.48% (benign, domestic) but INR at 95.65 (weakening, external) and FII outflows at record pace (-₹1.92 lakh crore YTD). The gold duty hike to 15% is a fiscal defence of forex reserves — government signalling that CAD pressure is real.
- **Copper at near-all-time highs (+40.86% 1Y)** alongside DRAM prices tripling YoY — both are leading indicators of AI infrastructure buildout intensity. Copper = data centre wiring/transformers; DRAM = GPU memory. The physical commodity complex is pricing in the AI capex cycle before any revenue materialises.
- **Wheat limit-up on USDA WASDE** (May 12) — adds to food inflation concerns on top of Hormuz disruption. India as net wheat importer is watching carefully; Kharif fertiliser supply already under Hormuz stress.
- **Nifty YTD -10.55% and record FII outflows** — India is not a safe haven in this risk-off environment. Despite domestic earnings resilience, the external account (CAD, FII, INR) is deteriorating. The gap between micro (corporate earnings strong) and macro (external shock) is the defining India investment tension of H1 2026.
- **Trump-Xi summit: Hormuz agreement** (both powers want it open) is the most significant geopolitical signal — if US and China agree Iran cannot be allowed to close Hormuz, there is diplomatic weight behind de-escalation. This is a mild positive for oil.
- **Decoupling is real but partial.** Vietnam/Mexico have captured final assembly but China still dominates components. India has gained marginally (reshoring mentions) but hasn't captured major manufacturing flows at scale yet.

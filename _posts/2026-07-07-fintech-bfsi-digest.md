# Fintech & BFSI Digest — 2026-07-07

## TL;DR

- RBI imposed a ₹66.7 lakh penalty on Bank of Baroda and GIC Housing Finance for regulatory non-compliance (specific violation not detailed in available reporting).
- The Ken reports that because RBI enforces no interest-rate ceiling on NBFCs, some RBI-registered NBFCs are legally charging 600%+ annualised interest on unsecured personal loans, and older-vintage NBFC licenses are being bought/sold like commodity assets (example: a 1990-vintage NBFC listed for sale at ₹1.90 Cr; Dev-Aashish Capital's revenue jumped from ₹1.7 lakh to ₹81 Cr in one year — a ~4,800% increase — per MCA filings).
- NaBFID is forming a self-regulatory organisation (SRO) for infrastructure financiers; Indian banks are preparing to tap overseas debt markets.
- IRDAI/Manipal Health (Temasek-backed) received SEBI approval for its IPO, eyeing a July listing.
- A 140-company consortium has launched a stablecoin intended to rival Tether; Plaid is reportedly weighing an IPO; Nubank is reported to be acquiring a bank.

## Key data / quotes

- RBI penalty: ₹66.7 lakh on Bank of Baroda + GIC Housing Finance.
- Dev-Aashish Capital (1995-vintage NBFC): revenue ₹1.7 lakh (FY24) → ₹81 Cr (FY25), ~4,800% jump.
- Solomon Capital (NBFC): revenue ₹22 lakh (2024) → ₹105 Cr (2025), ~500x jump.
- FPI debt buying: ~₹324 Bn net since June (consistent with today's macro-signals snapshot).

## Signals

The RBI penalty on Bank of Baroda is a new, unreconciled data point for the Bank of Baroda entity page — the specific violation wasn't captured this run (extraction-limited), so it doesn't yet map to the bank's existing Contradictions note on the ₹1,500 Cr ECL floating provision; flagged for a targeted follow-up before drawing any connection. The NBFC rate-cap story (The Ken) is a structural regulatory gap worth tracking as a sector-level risk: the absence of an RBI interest-rate ceiling, combined with NBFC licenses trading as tradeable assets independent of the underlying lending book, describes a shadow-banking arbitrage that could eventually draw RBI supervisory attention given the government's broader BFSI-governance push (AI kill-switch proposal, deposit-mobilisation focus tracked in recent digests). The stablecoin consortium and Plaid/Nubank items continue the global-fintech stablecoin-infrastructure thread tracked since late June.

## TL;DR

- US equities consolidated at record levels (S&P 7,520, Nasdaq 26,675); KOSPI extended its run to 8,229 (+2.52%). India Nifty 24,197 / Sensex 78,494 (May 27 close) — India up strongly.
- Brent jumped +2.13% to $96.30 after **US "self-defense strikes" near Strait of Hormuz killing Iranian personnel (May 27)** — fresh escalation, market re-priced deal-optimism downward; Brent had briefly hit $97.29 during May 26–27 session.
- US 10Y eased to 4.50% (−5–6 bps); India G-Sec 7.00%; India–US spread +250 bps.
- INR ~95.40 (recovering from 97.15 record low); Nifty PCR 0.92 (mild bearish lean).
- WGC: 68% of central banks plan to increase gold holdings in 2026 (up from 62% in 2025) — both PBoC and RBI at highest-ever gold reserves.
- BIS Project Agorá advancing to real-value testing: tokenisation of central bank + commercial bank money on a unified ledger demonstrates improved cross-border payment efficiency.

## Signals

- **Hormuz re-escalation:** US "self-defense strikes" killed Iranian personnel on May 27. Brent +2.13% despite deal hopes — market now pricing continued disruption. If Iran retaliates, oil could spike back toward $105–111.
- **India — Nifty recovery:** May 27 close 24,197 (up from 23,914 on May 26) — India bounced. Context: Bank Nifty +0.85%, IT and select financials led.
- **US bond easing signal:** 10Y at 4.50% (vs 4.65% peak) suggests bond market pricing in slower growth or lower rates ahead. Positive for EM currencies including INR if sustained.
- **BIS tokenisation milestone:** Project Agorá advancing to real-value testing signals institutionalisation of wholesale CBDC/tokenised money — a multi-year structural shift in cross-border payment infrastructure with direct implications for India's BFSI digital rails.
- **Gold reserves concentration:** 68% CB gold accumulation intent + PBoC/RBI at ATH = structural bid under gold prices regardless of short-term COT signals.

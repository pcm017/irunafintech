# Macro Signals Snapshot — 2026-07-07

## TL;DR

- Dow closed above 53,000 for the first time (53,055.91, +0.29%), a new closing record; S&P 500 (7,537.43, +0.72%) and Nasdaq (26,121.16, +1.12%) also gained on the Jul 6 session.
- India equities extended a 4th straight up session — Nifty 24,430.35 (+0.66%), Sensex 78,285.07 (+0.67%), Bank Nifty 58,291.50 (+0.61%) — with banking, auto, realty, metal and energy leading and IT lagging.
- Crude (Brent ~$69, WTI $67.83-69.20) near its lowest since late February on Hormuz shipping recovery plus an expected OPEC+ output hike — a supply-glut setup rather than a demand story.
- India G-Sec 10Y held at a 15-week low (6.71-6.72%); FPIs have been net buyers of ₹324 Bn in Indian govt bonds since June (tax relief, steadier rupee, potential Bloomberg Global Aggregate Index inclusion), compressing the India-US spread to ~+222bps despite rising US yields.
- UPI June volume: 22.72 Bn transactions (-2.1% MoM from May's 23.20Bn, +23% YoY) — a rare monthly dip within a still-strong annual growth trend.

## Signals

The Dow's first close above 53,000 and broad US index gains reflect continued risk-on momentum into early July, even as the underlying crude story (Brent near 5-month lows on Hormuz recovery + expected OPEC+ supply increase) points to a supply glut rather than demand strength — a distinction that matters for India's import-bill read, since a supply-driven low keeps the disinflation tailwind intact without signalling global demand weakness. India's own market strength (4th straight up session, broad-based sector participation ex-IT) plus the ₹324 Bn FPI debt-buying since June reinforce the same "calm, rate-cut-anticipating" domestic macro regime tracked through late June, with the India-US spread compression (+222bps) now the tightest read in recent weeks despite US yields holding near 4.5%. UPI's -2.1% MoM dip is likely seasonal/reporting noise rather than a trend break, given the +23% YoY growth rate remains intact.

## Signals

**RBI collateral rule — structural change for India capital markets.** Requiring 100% collateral for proprietary trading eliminates the leverage-driven volume that characterized India's F&O markets. This follows the regulator's June 2025 crackdown on retail derivatives speculation. The transmission: lower prop desk leverage → lower F&O volumes → lower exchange revenues (NSE, BSE) → potentially lower market liquidity in stress periods. Consolidation in smaller prop desks likely.

**India-Nepal UPI-NPI link — UPI now spans 9 countries.** Nepal is a major remittance corridor (India is Nepal's largest remittance sender). Linking NPI enables cheaper, faster transfers, potentially reducing hawala and informal remittance channels. India's payments diplomacy (UPI global) is now a foreign policy tool; it directly competes with SWIFT and Western payment rails in South Asia.

**Sarvam AI unicorn status is geopolitically timed.** The $234M raise coming days after the U.S. government ordered Anthropic to pull Fable/Mythos gives Sarvam AI enormous narrative tailwind: India's sovereign AI alternative to US frontier models. With NITI Aayog reviewing local AI infrastructure and the Rs 1 lakh crore R&D fund in play, Sarvam AI is positioned as the anchor of India's sovereign AI stack. The Rs 5L crore ambition articulated by The Ken (~$60B) is the scale at which India can develop competitive frontier infrastructure — vs. the US ($500B+ committed by hyperscalers) and China ($295B government plan).

**US-Iran peace deal → India export boost transmission.** The Hormuz reopening expected by June 19 is not just an energy story — India's merchandise exports (pharma, chemicals, textiles, engineering goods) through the Gulf shipping lanes have been delayed/disrupted for 100+ days. Reopening = normalised freight rates + restored trade flow + GCC consumer spending recovering (Indian diaspora remittances + B2B trade).

**Pagaya-Klarna litigation.** Pagaya is an AI credit underwriting network that partners with lenders; Klarna is a global BNPL player. A lawsuit signals a dispute in their partnership/revenue-sharing arrangement. Given Klarna's imminent IPO (filed in 2025, listed 2026), timing suggests Pagaya is using legal pressure to resolve a commercial dispute before Klarna's public valuation is locked in.

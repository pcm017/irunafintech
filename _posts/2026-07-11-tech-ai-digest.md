# Tech & AI Digest — 2026-07-11

_Backfilled during the 2026-07-13 compile — this file sat in `raw/newsletters/` unprocessed since its 2026-07-11 collect run (only that day's macro-signals snapshot was ingested at the time)._

## TL;DR

- **Big Tech's five largest US data-centre spenders (Alphabet, Amazon, Meta, Microsoft, Oracle) have collectively added ~$350 billion in debt over five years** — the clearest quantification yet of the AI-capex-on-credit thesis tracked since SemiAnalysis's >$7T-by-2029 projection (Jul 8).
- **TSMC's foundry market share has surged to 64%**, even as the stock saw a sharp intraday sell-off (Jul 7) amid a broader semiconductor rout and Goldman removed it from its Asia-Pacific Conviction List (Jul 1).
- **Samsung Foundry continues picking up Google/Nvidia/Tesla/AMD/BYD orders**; Tesla's next-gen A16 chip will be fabricated exclusively by Samsung's Texas facility.
- **Intel Foundry stock dropped 21% (from $140 to $110) in seven days** on a BofA semiconductor-bubble warning plus delayed 18A profitable-yield milestones.
- **Tata Electronics-PSMC's Dholera fab has crossed the halfway mark in construction**, trial production still targeted for December 2026; Tata Electronics also signed a strategic ASML agreement anchoring the ~$11Bn Dholera wafer plant.
- Xbox layoffs under CEO Asha Sharma triggered US H-1B visa political backlash; SK Hynix debuted in US markets via the second-largest share sale ever.

## Signals

**Change detection:** The $350Bn Big Tech debt figure is the first hard, named-company quantification of the AI-infrastructure-debt thesis SemiAnalysis floated in aggregate terms on Jul 8 — moving the story from projection to confirmed balance-sheet fact.

**Root cause traversal:** TSMC's 64% share plus its Jul 7 sell-off is a valuation/sentiment disconnect from fundamentals — the share gain is structural (AI chip demand), the sell-off is sector-wide risk-off (Samsung earnings, DeepSeek-linked news) rather than TSMC-specific.

**Sizing / impact:** For India, the Dholera fab crossing the halfway mark plus the ASML anchor agreement is the most concrete forward-looking data point — first silicon in December 2026 remains the credibility gate for the entire India Semiconductor Mission narrative tracked across prior digests.

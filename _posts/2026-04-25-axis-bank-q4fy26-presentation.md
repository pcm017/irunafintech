## TL;DR

- Q4 FY26 PAT ₹7,071 Cr (+9% QoQ, −0.6% YoY); FY26 full-year PAT ₹24,457 Cr (−7% YoY — impacted by lower trading income, higher provisioning). However, **two one-time items net to zero** on P&L: tax credit of ₹2,193 Cr (Citi intangibles deferred tax) and ₹2,001 Cr voluntary standard asset provision.
- Balance sheet grew 17% YoY to ₹18.87 Tn; advances +19% YoY (Wholesale +38%, SME +24%, Retail +8%); deposits +14% YoY with CASA at 40%.
- Asset quality improved sharply — GNPA 1.23% (−17 bps QoQ), NNPA 0.37% (−5 bps QoQ), Net credit cost 0.37% (−39 bps QoQ). Bank holds ₹15,473 Cr in cumulative non-NPA provisions (standard asset coverage 1.26%).
- NIM compressed to 3.62% (FY26: 3.69%, −29 bps YoY) due to repo rate cut transmission; management reaffirms 3.80% through-cycle NIM target achievable 15–18 months after last rate cut.
- Digital leadership: #1 UPI Payer PSP (36% share by volume), #2/3 credit card issuer (14% CIF share), 16 mn MAU on Axis Mobile, 187% YoY growth in UPI transaction value.

## Signals

- **Macro hedge:** The ₹2,001 Cr voluntary provision is stress-tested against oil at $150, inflation at 7.4%, and INR depreciating 20% — significant macro tail-risk buffer, unique among Q4 FY26 BFSI reporters.
- **NIM trajectory:** At 3.62%, Axis is below its 3.80% through-cycle target. With 125 bps of rate cuts transmitted in FY26 and residual TD repricing benefit remaining, NIM recovery depends on repo rate cycle and retail book rebalancing (currently 55% retail vs. 70% target).
- **Wholesale surge:** Corporate book +38% YoY is the key driver of balance sheet growth — driven by power/renewables, commercial real estate, data centres, NBFCs, and manufacturing. This is NIM-dilutive short-term but RAROC-neutral per management.
- **Digital moat deepening:** 36% UPI payer market share, 187% YoY UPI value growth, 98% digital transactions by individual customers — Axis is extracting significant transaction banking revenue from its digital dominance.
- **AI strategy (AXIOM):** Only ISO 42001-certified bank globally; expects "meaningful bottom-line impact" from AI over next 18–24 months.
- **Subsidiary engine:** FY26 domestic subsidiary PAT ₹2,051 Cr (+16% YoY) — Axis Finance, AMC, Capital all growing 19–61% YoY.

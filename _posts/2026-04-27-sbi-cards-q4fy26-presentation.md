## TL;DR

- **FY26 PAT ₹2,167 Cr (+13% YoY)** — recovery driven by sharply falling credit costs after two difficult years of asset quality stress
- **Asset quality inflected decisively:** GNPA 2.41% (−67 bps YoY), credit cost 8.6% FY26 vs. 9.0% FY25, with Q4 at 7.7% (lowest in several quarters) — Stage 2 and Stage 3 stocks declining for 6+ consecutive quarters
- **Spends breakout, receivables flat:** Total spends ₹4.3 Tn (+29% YoY) led by corporate spends +195% YoY; but receivables only +2% YoY — revolver mix declining (22%), growth is transactor/EMI heavy
- **Capital strong, balance sheet underleveraged:** CAR 25.5%; management holds ₹220 Cr ECL overlay against geopolitical uncertainty; interim dividend ₹2.50/share declared
- **Medium-term ROA target 4–4.5%** vs. current 3.2%; C/I guided 55–58% for FY27

## Signals

1. **Credit cost recovery is real and durable.** Six consecutive quarters of declining Stage 2/Stage 3 stocks; GNPA declining sequentially since at least Q2 FY26. This supports the India consumer credit cycle recovery thesis tracked across wiki.
2. **The corporate spends boost is structural-but-thin.** Corporate spends grew 195% YoY, added ₹25,564 Cr in Q4 alone (vs. ₹8,656 Cr in Q4 FY25). However, cost-to-income moved up by ~600 bps because the corporate pass-back model adds to both numerator and denominator at thin margins. Watch for this to normalise in FY27.
3. **Revolver declining structurally** — 22% of receivables (was ~24% historically). Management expects "slight downward bias" in FY27. Offset strategy: installment lending (EMI book). If revolver falls to 19–20%, NIM compression of ~25–27 bps per 1% drop without EMI offset.
4. **UPI on credit card momentum building.** Rupay-UPI spend growing ~10% QoQ; strongest in Tier 2+ markets. SBI Card's Rupay card base gives structural exposure to this growth vector.
5. **India credit card industry consolidating around quality.** New account additions industry-wide muted — underwriting tightened post-FY25 asset quality issues. Applications not slowing; issuers are simply more selective. Expect growth re-acceleration once vintage performance stabilises.
6. **AI investment increasing.** ML for underwriting, fraud, collections, customer engagement — SBI Cards plans to "harness full potential in FY27." Less quantified than Bajaj Finance FinAI; but directionally consistent.

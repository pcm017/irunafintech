## TL;DR

- Gold ($4,182.28, +1.25%) and Silver ($62.86, +2.50%) extended their rally for a second session, still riding the weak June US nonfarm payrolls miss (57K vs. ~110-115K forecast) that reduced Fed rate-hike odds; gold on track for its first weekly rise since late May (+2.3% WoW).
- India forex reserves fell sharply to $666.93Bn (-$5.65Bn WoW, 15-month low) — a reversal from the prior week's reported recovery, driven mainly by a $5.39Bn drop in the gold-reserves valuation component.
- India G-Sec 10Y held at a 15-week low (6.71-6.72%); India-US 10Y spread compressed further to ~+223bps.
- Oil (Brent ~$72, WTI ~$68) trading near its lowest levels since late February on recovering Hormuz shipping flows and US-Iran negotiations.
- RBI's FY27 forecasts (from the June MPC) stand: CPI raised to 5.1% (from 4.6%), GDP cut to 6.6% (from 6.9%), citing West Asia conflict, energy prices, and monsoon/El Niño uncertainty.

## Signals

Gold/silver's second consecutive rally session confirms the NFP miss is a durable, single-cause repricing of the Fed path rather than a one-day blip — distinct from the geopolitical drivers that dominated H1 2026 commodity moves. For India, the forex reserve reversal (-$5.65Bn, driven by the gold-valuation component) is a reminder that reserve levels remain sensitive to gold-price marks, not just flow dynamics; it does not change the underlying G-Sec/FII-inflow story, which remains constructive (10Y at a 15-week low, spread compressing to +223bps). RBI's own FY27 CPI-up/GDP-down revision continues to frame the "resilient but conflict-exposed" macro read heading into the August MPC.

**Raw file:** *Q4 2025 26 Fact Sheet.pdf*

---

## TL;DR

- **TCS Q4 FY26 INR revenue: ₹706,980 Mn** (+9.6% YoY, +5.4% QoQ) — accelerating YoY growth after a period of single-digit growth
- **USD revenue: $7,621 Mn** (+2.1% YoY, +1.5% QoQ); CC growth -0.6% YoY — currency-adjusted growth slightly negative, showing industry demand softness
- **Operating Margin: 25.3%** — industry-high among India IT majors (vs. TechM 12.6%, Infosys ~21%)
- **TCV order book: $12.0B** — strong pipeline; North America leads ($5.4B), BFSI largest vertical ($3.9B)
- **Headcount: 584,519** — India's largest employer in tech; attrition 13.7% (stable)
- **FCF quality: 106.7% of net profit** — very high cash conversion

## Signals

1. **TCS operating margin 25.3% sets the industry benchmark**: TechM's target of 15% EBIT and Infosys at ~21% show the 400–1,200 bps structural margin advantage TCS has from scale, client relationships, and delivery maturity. This margin moat is not easily replicated.

2. **CC growth -0.6% = industry demand reality**: Despite strong INR revenue growth (aided by currency), the real demand signal is negative constant-currency growth. This confirms industry growth is in the 2–4% range that TechM also cited — and validates the secular slowdown narrative for traditional IT services.

3. **BFSI remains the dominant vertical at $3.9B TCV**: BFSI (banking, financial services, insurance) IT outsourcing demand is sustaining at scale even as other verticals slow. This is relevant for Indian BFSI sector's IT modernisation agenda (per HDFC Bank, Bajaj Finance earnings) — the demand is real and being captured by the large IT players.

4. **North America $5.4B TCV = US market dominates**: Despite tariff uncertainty and H-1B pressure, TCS's North America deal wins remain the largest geography. US enterprise IT modernisation continues regardless of trade policy noise.

5. **High cash conversion (106.7%)**: Confirms TCS's capital-light model. No major capex required — software delivery generates cash reliably.

6. **Client base expanding at $1M+ level (+14 QoQ)**: New client additions at the $1M+ level is a leading indicator of future revenue growth — suggests demand pipeline is broadening even if existing account growth is muted.

---

## Contradictions

- CC revenue -0.6% YoY but INR revenue +9.6% YoY. The divergence is almost entirely currency (INR depreciation boosting reported numbers). This creates a risk of optically strong results masking real demand weakness — watch for CC growth to turn positive before declaring demand recovery. *No conflict with other sources.*

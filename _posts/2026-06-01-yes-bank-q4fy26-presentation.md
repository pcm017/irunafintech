# Yes Bank Q4FY26 & FY26 Investor Presentation — Filed June 1, 2026

**Management (Analyst Meet, May 2026):** Management of Yes Bank Limited
**Key shareholders:** SMBC (Sumitomo Mitsui Banking Corporation), SBI, Advent International (Verventa Holdings), LIC

## TL;DR

- PAT Q4FY26 ₹1,068 cr (+44.7% YoY, +12.3% QoQ) — highest ever quarterly profit since the 2020 reconstruction; FY26 PAT ₹3,476 cr (+44.5% YoY)
- NIM expanded 20 bps YoY to 2.7% in Q4, driven by lower RIDF burden and improved funding mix; FY26 NIM at 2.6% (+20 bps YoY) — still below mid-private peer range of 4–5%
- Asset quality continues to normalize: GNPA 1.3% (from 1.6% Q4FY25), NNPA 0.2%; slippage ratio 1.6% vs 2.0% in Q4FY25 — retail slippages at 9-quarter low
- Operating efficiency improving sharply: C/I ratio 63.0% (from 67.3% Q4FY25); positive jaws — Total Income +8.8% YoY, Opex +1.8% YoY in Q4; PSL shortfall deposits declining (-24.5% YoY) which structurally reduces the NIM drag

## Signals

**Yes Bank's reconstruction story is entering the "compounding" phase, not the "repair" phase.** The 2020 reconstruction (SBI-led bailout + ₹15,000 cr capital raise) stabilized the bank. FY22–FY24 was repair — recapitalization, PSL compliance, deposit rebuilding. FY25–FY26 is something different: positive operating leverage (income +11.7% YoY, opex +4.6% YoY FY26), GNPA at 1.3% (from a peak of 15.4% in FY22), CASA crossing ₹1 lakh cr, and ROA reaching 1.0% in Q4. The question is whether this is sustainable or SR-gain-supported.

**The SR gain is significant but not the whole story.** ₹1,559 cr P&L gain from security receipt settlements in FY26 (₹446 cr in Q4) materially supports PAT. Stripping this out, FY26 PAT would be closer to ₹2,600 cr — still up ~8% YoY on normalized basis. So the operating momentum is real, even if the headline 44.5% PAT growth overstates the run rate.

**PSL shortfall burden declining is the structural NIM driver to watch.** Yes Bank still has ₹27,931 cr in PSL-shortfall deposits (6% of assets) — low-yield mandatory placements that drag NIM ~100 bps below where it would otherwise be. This is declining at ~24% YoY. As PSL compliance improves (NIL shortfall achieved in FY26), this drag fades and NIM structurally improves toward 3%+ over 2–3 years — without any organic loan yield expansion needed.

**India macro signal:** Yes Bank's 6th-largest-private-bank status and ₹2.73 lakh cr loan book means its health is a signal for mid-tier private banking credit conditions. Card fee growth (+17% YoY) and third-party product fees (+16%) confirm consumer financial services demand is firm despite West Asia headwinds.

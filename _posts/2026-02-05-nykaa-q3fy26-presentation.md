## TL;DR

- Nykaa Q3 FY26: GMV ₹5,795 Cr (+28%), NR ₹2,873 Cr (+27%), EBITDA ₹230 Cr (8.0%, highest ever), PAT ₹68 Cr (+156%).
- Beauty EBITDA margin hits 10.1% — all cylinders firing.
- Fashion EBITDA improves sharply to −2.0% (from −5.4% YoY) — profitability path visible.
- New exclusive brand wins: La Roche-Posay, NYX Makeup, Kiehl's full-stack + H&M Beauty.
- 276 physical stores, 94 cities; Nykaa Perfumery (new format) launched.
- Own brands: 12 significant labels, $400M annualised GMV, 16M+ customers served.

## Signals

1. **13-14 consecutive quarters of mid-20s GMV growth** is rare in Indian e-commerce. Nykaa's consistency signals that the India beauty market is expanding structurally, not just cyclically.

2. **Beauty EBITDA at 10.1% = mature business.** At 10%+ EBITDA margins with 27% GMV growth, Nykaa Beauty is the most profitable segment — and the growth rate is not decelerating meaningfully.

3. **Fashion improvement is the quarter's most important signal.** From −5.4% to −2.0% EBITDA margin in one year. Key driver: H&M brand win, improved customer acquisition ROI, reduced discounting. At this rate, Fashion approaches EBITDA breakeven in Q1/Q2 FY27.

4. **L'Oreal exclusive wins (La Roche-Posay, NYX, Kiehl's)** signal Nykaa has earned a trust position with the world's top beauty company that competitors cannot easily replicate. La Roche-Posay specifically (world #1 dermatologist-recommended skincare) entering India exclusively via Nykaa will drive significant skincare category GMV.

5. **eB2B (Superstore) at 485k retailers** across 1,100+ cities is an underappreciated asset — it's a distribution network for premium beauty brands into India's general and modern trade, at a scale that rivals dedicated FMCG distributors. As own-brand GMV grows, this becomes a high-margin owned-brand distribution play.

# Kotak Mahindra Bank Q4FY26 Investor Presentation — May 2, 2026

## TL;DR

- Standalone PAT ₹4,027 cr (+13% YoY, +17% QoQ); FY26 PAT ₹14,008 cr (+2% YoY) — NIM compression from 100 bps repo cuts weighed on FY26, but Q4 showed a sharp recovery in credit quality
- NIM 4.67% in Q4 (adj. for day-count = 4.54%, same as Q3), FY26 NIM 4.60% vs 4.96% FY25 — 36 bps full-year compression from repo cuts partially offset by liability management (SA rate cut, low-cost deposit growth)
- Credit cost 39 bps in Q4, down sharply from 63 bps in Q3 — slippages collapsed to ₹1,018 cr from ₹1,605 cr; GNPA improved to 1.2%, net NPA 0.25%
- Customer AUM ₹7.47 lakh cr (+12% YoY); consolidated ROA 2.18% for Q4; book value/share ₹182 (+15% YoY)

## Signals

**NIM compression story is a FY26 theme, now partially behind.** FY26 NIM fell 36 bps to 4.60% from 100 bps of repo cuts — Kotak offset this by cutting SA rates and growing low-cost granular deposits. The CASA ratio held at 43.3% (a peer-leading position). Going into FY27, at least one more cut is expected — but the repricing of term deposits downward should help. Q4 day-count adjusted NIM essentially flat QoQ shows stabilization.

**Credit quality turnaround in Q4 is the standout signal.** Slippages fell 36% QoQ to ₹1,018 cr, driven by improved collection in CV, MFI, and credit cards. GNPA improved to 1.2%. This is coming off elevated credit costs in H1FY26 (MFI stress, credit card seasonality) — the improvement suggests the NBFC/MFI sector stress that hit Kotak's Sonata Finance portfolio is starting to normalize.

**Group ROA/ROE below FY25 for structural reasons.** FY26 consolidated ROA 2.06% vs 2.36% — entirely attributable to NIM compression + FY26 provision cycle. Capital position extremely strong (group CET1 22.1%) — Kotak is over-capitalized relative to loan growth.

**West Asia + below-normal monsoon = watch the tractor/rural book.** Kotak is the second-largest tractor financer in India (10.9% market share). IMD has forecast below-normal FY27 monsoon — tractor demand and collection efficiency in this book is a H1FY27 risk.

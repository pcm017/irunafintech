## TL;DR

- Eternal (formerly Zomato) reports FY26 milestone: $10B+ B2C NOV, 109M transacting customers; parent entity renamed from Zomato Ltd to Eternal Ltd.
- Blinkit (quick commerce) NOV +95.4% YoY; turned Adj EBITDA positive (₹37 Cr) in Q4; 2,243 stores; 3-year CAGR guidance >60%.
- Food delivery (Zomato): NOV +18.8% YoY (third consecutive quarter of acceleration), Adj EBITDA ₹532 Cr (5.5% of NOV).
- Consolidated Adj Revenue ₹17,680 Cr (+186% YoY, LFL +64%); Adj EBITDA ₹429 Cr (+160% YoY).
- AI is framed as a market *expander* (not a disruptor) — enables new customers, partners, delivery agents to participate; not replacing physical logistics.
- Target: $1B Adj EBITDA by FY29.

### Key quotes
- Deepinder Goyal: *"In 2008, I was collecting restaurant menus on a motorbike in Delhi... Last year (FY26), 109 million Indians completed transactions worth over $10 billion through Blinkit, District, and Zomato."*
- *"Our first annual $10 billion in NOV took 18 years. This doubling to $20 billion annual NOV will take less than two years from here."*
- *"It took 16 years to reach Adjusted EBITDA profitability in FY24. From there, we expect to reach $1 billion of Adjusted EBITDA, hopefully by FY29."*
- On AI threat: *"General-purpose interfaces are good for general-purpose queries. They are poor interfaces for complex, high-frequency, habitual transactions."* Cites Google's failure to displace Booking.com, Expedia, Amazon despite monopoly distribution.
- On AI as market expander: *"AI reduces the friction required for each of them to participate"* — expanding the supply and demand side of the marketplace.
- On Eternal brand: *"it is a name you have to earn every single day... culture outlasts strategy, where systems outlast individuals."*
- Albinder (Blinkit CEO) on competition: *"High competition can have adverse impact in certain periods... aggressive discounting is leading to poor-quality growth. But more broadly and longer term, good competition will aid our growth."*
- On Q1 FY27: *"we expect Q1FY27 to be meaningfully stronger on a QoQ basis"* — seasonal Q4 weakness in quick commerce resolved, April demand signals strong.

### Blinkit Deep Dive
- Delhi NCR: 80k SKUs, 2x store density vs. next 7 cities, 8x vs. rest
- Cities beyond top 8: <30% pin code coverage, store ramp faster and profitability comparable to mature markets
- 3 growth levers: (1) Assortment expansion, (2) Geographic expansion, (3) Demand density flywheel
- Competition is short-term margin pressure but long-term category accelerant — quick commerce is "nascent" and not zero-sum yet

## Signals

1. **Eternal at the inflection between growth and profitability.** Blinkit's first positive Adj EBITDA quarter (₹37 Cr) is the moment the quick commerce business has crossed from "growth investment" to "early profitability." This is the inflection investors have been waiting for since 2021.

2. **$20B NOV in <2 years** is a credible target given Blinkit's 95% growth trajectory and Zomato's 18-20% trajectory compounding together. At current trajectory, Eternal could be the first Indian consumer tech company to process $25B+ annual GMV by FY28.

3. **AI doesn't disrupt physical commerce — it widens participation.** The Google analogy is the strongest articulation yet of why Amazon/Booking.com/Zomato-type vertical transaction platforms are defensible against AI chat disruption. The TAM of people who can use Blinkit expands when conversational search replaces app-based search — the platform benefits, not loses.

4. **District (going-out) is the unpriced option.** Growing 46.5% YoY with losses narrowing sharply — District is the third leg of the stool. If entertainment/events spending in India grows with Gen Z income and urban migration, District could be a standalone significant business by FY28-29.

5. **Eternal brand** signals institutional longevity intent — the company is transitioning from a startup narrative to a generational-institution narrative. Changes how management, employees, and capital partners think about time horizons.

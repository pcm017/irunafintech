## Key Data / Quotes

### RBI MPC June 5, 2026 (Full Detail)
- Repo rate **5.25%** (3rd consecutive hold). SDF: 5.0%. MSF: 5.50%. Neutral stance.
- Capital inflow package: **~$5B/month** estimated by analysts.
- **LCR-linked pricing**: Proposed differential interest rates on bulk rupee deposits based on liquidity treatment — reform in bank deposit pricing.
- **FII G-Sec exemption**: Income-tax (Amendment) Ordinance, 2026 promulgated; FIIs exempt from capital gains on G-Sec holdings.
- **Decision context**: Weakening rupee (INR ~95.11), West Asia conflict threatening GDP growth + inflation.

### Regulatory / Banking
- **Sahamati as AA SRO**: RBI formally recognised Sahamati — governance layer for Account Aggregator ecosystem now formalised.
- **HDFC Bank cleared**: Courts, RBI, and independent reviewers found no material adverse findings. "Persistence of crisis narrative raises questions about source and motivation of negative coverage." Rajesh Exports responding to SEBI interim order with documents and evidence.
- **Canara Bank fine**: RBI ₹41.80 lakh penalty for non-compliance.
- **Operation XENOFISCAL**: SideCopy group; XenoRAT malware; targeting Indian finance and revenue systems via phishing + fileless techniques; RBI advisories issued for Mythos threat.

### Macro / Consumer
- **India Inc CFOs top 3 risks**: (1) Rupee depreciation, (2) Rising input inflation, (3) Softening consumer demand.
- **Nestle India**: Geopolitical churn impacting input costs.
- **Small packs gaining**: Consumer FMCG budgets shrinking; pack downsizing = consumer stress signal.
- **FDI equity inflows**: +18% YoY FY26; US investments in India doubled YoY.

### Payments / Credit
- **UPI May 2026 ATH**:
  - Volume: **23.2 billion transactions** (MoM +4% vs. April 22.3B; YoY +24%)
  - Value: **₹29.9 trillion**
  - Daily avg: **737.79 million txns / ₹84,423 Cr**
  - Note: NPCI excludes Credit Card on UPI and Credit Line on UPI from these figures — actual usage is higher.
- **Bank credit**: 16.2% YoY (fortnight ending May 15) — fastest in 2 years. Credit book ₹211.87T. Deposits: +12.2% to ₹256.88T. Credit-deposit gap: **400 bps** (persists).

### Fintech / Lending
- **Truhome Finance**: SEBI IPO approval — home loan/housing finance. Continued investor interest in mortgage lending sector.
- **Community banks** (Alex Johnson / Fintech Takes): Survival strategies for community banks facing fintech disruption. Key themes: differentiation via local relationships, specialised lending, community presence — capabilities digital-first challengers lack.

## Signals

- **Dual RBI-Govt capital inflow response** (LCR pricing + FII G-Sec exemption + neutral stance + $5B package) is the most coordinated bond market liberalisation move in recent memory. Watch FII debt flow data for May/June — if flows increase, India's CAD financing becomes structurally easier.
- **UPI 23.2B txns in May** = India's digital payments are decelerating from prior month's record in volume terms (+4% MoM) but the absolute scale (737M txns/day) is extraordinary. The UPI stack is now infrastructure, not a product — the competitive moat question shifts from adoption to who captures monetisation.
- **Bank credit 16.2% YoY — fastest in 2 years**: Credit-deposit gap at 400 bps is the banking sector's structural tension. Banks need to grow deposits faster or slow credit growth; NIM compression risk if they pay up for deposits.
- **HDFC Bank cleared by all reviewers** but narrative persists = this is now a governance and communications problem, not a regulatory risk. Management needs a proactive narrative strategy.
- **Operation XENOFISCAL** targeting Indian finance + RBI Mythos advisories = the cyber threat landscape for Indian BFSI is escalating simultaneously on state-sponsored (XENOFISCAL) and AI-generated (Mythos) vectors. Two distinct threat architectures requiring different responses.

## TL;DR

- Q4 FY26 insurance premium: ₹29,934 Cr (+46% YoY for Q4 alone); FY26 full year +42% YoY. New protection premium +67% YoY in Q4 (health +68%, term catching up fast).
- FY26 PAT: ₹670 Cr (2.2% of premium); operating revenue ₹6,794 Cr (+37% YoY); Q4 operating revenue ~₹2,000 Cr.
- Renewal revenue ARR hit ₹1,126 Cr in Q4 (+63% YoY) — the primary compounding engine for future profitability.
- Paisabazaar turned EBITDA-positive in Q4 FY26 (small but positive milestone); management guided for "significantly positive" FY27.
- Commission regulation risk downplayed: management has seen no signal from IRDAI; media speculation only. Two ongoing debates (deferred revenues in life, EOM structure in health) both framed as positive for PB if implemented.

### Segment highlights

- **Health insurance:** Persistency 87-89% (13th month); loss ratio 51% (down from 54% FY25) — PB operating well below industry LR; only 5% of lives claim more than once in 10 years.
- **Term insurance:** Strong Q4; taking market share from offline; FY27 expected to challenge health for growth leadership.
- **Savings business:** Returned to growth after soft 3-4 quarters; Gulf disruption (West Asia conflict) limited NRI segment in March.
- **Motor:** Continued growth; private car 8.6% share, 2-wheeler 11.8% share.
- **PB Partners:** 450,000 advisors; 99% of PIN codes covered; most diversified channel.
- **UAE:** 54% YoY; cross-border health + life; learning applied from India Policybazaar model.

### Paisabazaar update

- EBITDA-positive in Q4 FY26 (small positive — first milestone).
- FY27: management expects "significantly positive" EBITDA. Fixed costs stable (~₹200 Cr base); operating leverage to drive margin expansion as disbursals scale.
- New initiatives (FY27): no incremental cost additions expected.
- Credit card issuances remain subdued (quality tightening maintained).

### AI framing

- "The biggest use case in insurance for AI is risk, not efficiency" — fraud detection and underwriting risk profiling are primary AI investments.
- Company-wide AI adoption; not a few teams — "the entire company is on it."
- Productivity going up across sales, customer service, and developer teams.
- 3-5 year AI ROI: hard to quantify but directionally positive. Primary goal: drive fresh growth + customer excellence. P&L structure already in good shape.

### Commission regulation

- Management position: "Only the media knows." No signal from IRDAI or insurance companies.
- Two real regulatory debates: (1) deferred revenues in life insurance — PB welcomes it; (2) EOM structure rationalization in health — PB welcomes it ("no kind of comment to offer").
- GST rationalization on health/life insurance (prior episode): PB demonstrated resilience. Commission squeeze survived.
- Bima Sugam: management notes phygital model not easily replicable; structural moat against digital disruption.

### PB Health

- Raising capital externally (not yet approached PB Fintech).
- PB Fintech has ~26-28% stake with pro-rata right to maintain it; board will consider participation when asked.

## Signals

- **Renewal ARR growth of 63% YoY** is the most important number: it is near-pure EBITDA at ~80% margin, and it confirms that FY26 new business was written at high quality. FY27 profitability trajectory is largely already locked in from renewals alone.
- **Paisabazaar EBITDA-positive** is a turning point — the drag on consolidated PAT from credit business is structurally reversing. FY27 PAT guidance of ₹1,000 Cr is now more credible.
- **Commission regulation: non-event for now.** Management's calm framing and IRDAI silence are more important than media noise. The two actual debates (deferred revenues, EOM health) would both help PB.
- **Health insurance is PB's structural moat** — 51% loss ratio, superior persistency, and modular co-created products mean insurers need PB more than PB needs any single insurer.
- **UAE compounding quietly** — 54% YoY, cross-border health + life growing. No margin disclosure, but premium scale already meaningful.

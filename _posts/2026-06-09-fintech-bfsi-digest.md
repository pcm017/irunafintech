## Signals

**RBI inflation revision — stagflationary drift.** Raising CPI to 5.1% (FY27 full year) with a Q3 spike to 5.9% while lowering GDP to 6.6% is a structurally unfavourable combination for rate-sensitive assets. The Q3 monsoon-driven food price spike (El Niño + uneven distribution) is now the dominant inflation risk. If the Q3 CPI print of 5.9% materialises, RBI will face pressure to pause any easing cycle or consider a hike — inverting market expectations. NBFC funding costs are the first transmission channel.

**SEBI-Rajesh Exports: regulatory benchmark case.** ₹15 lakh crore in flagged transactions at India's largest declared gold exporter is a systemic signal — SEBI's enforcement capacity and willingness to pursue large industrial conglomerates is being tested. The SC ruling on the fraud-finding evidentiary framework (running concurrently) will determine whether SEBI's "non-genuine transaction" standard holds legally. If upheld, it sets precedent for the agency to pursue other large-volume suspicious traders. If narrowed, it constrains SEBI's toolkit. Watch: June 18 Lendingkart hearing and parallel SC schedule.

**Agentic payments — infrastructure race is on.** Four global payment infrastructure incumbents are simultaneously building the same 5-layer agentic stack. The developer tooling race (Stripe vs Adyen vs Coinbase vs Mastercard) mirrors the cloud wars of 2010–2015. For India: RBI's UPI infrastructure is well-positioned for domestic agentic payments (structured APIs, account aggregator ecosystem, Sahamati SRO now formalised). The gap is in cross-border agentic flows — UPI international is nascent compared to what Stripe/Adyen are building globally. Cross-link: *upi*

**Corporate deposits as bank funding patch — systemic risk accumulating.** Banks filling a 400bps credit-deposit gap with corporate/bulk deposits creates rate sensitivity: corporate depositors are more sophisticated, negotiate harder, and will redeploy at higher rates faster than retail. Banks that are bulk-deposit-heavy face asymmetric NIM pressure when rates move. Watch: Q1 FY27 bank results for deposit mix disclosure (bulk vs. retail %).

**IPO market as geopolitical barometer.** Curefoods and other India startups delaying listings are effectively pricing in continued market volatility from Hormuz/Iran uncertainty. IPO pricing depends on secondary market stability; with Nifty at −11.6% YTD and FII outflows ₹32,963 Cr in May, the pricing window remains difficult. Watch: first sustained Nifty rally above 23,500 as the potential trigger for IPO queue to reopen.

# Tech & AI Digest — 2026-07-14

## TL;DR

- Demis Hassabis reportedly said AGI arrives in 2-5 years, with bio and nuclear AI risks landing first — headline-only capture from The AI Corner.
- Apple sued OpenAI — an unusual pre-product, former-partner litigation framing worth tracking as AI-industry legal precedent.
- Meta's Muse AI image generator was discontinued just four days after launch.
- TCS CEO Krithivasan disclosed AI is forcing IT services giants to concede contract value in renegotiations even as new revenue stays lumpy — a concrete extension of the AI-margin-pressure thread already tracked at HDFC Bank/Kotak, now specifically at TCS on the pricing/contract-value side.
- Bosch's Commerce Dept CHIPS Direct Funding Agreement (signed Jul 13) confirmed its $2Bn Roseville, CA plant reaching sample production — the first US Bosch semiconductor fab; TSMC's capacity lead over Samsung/Intel widened to a projected 7-9x by 2028, with Samsung's 1.4nm mass production slipping to 2029 and Intel 14A external production slipping beyond 2030.
- India Semiconductor Mission 2.0 got a Rs 8,000cr FY27 budget outlay (largest single-year ISM allocation to date); 13 projects now approved across 7 states; Tata-PSMC Dholera fab remains on track for first silicon December 2026.

## Key data / quotes

- "AI is forcing IT services giants to give away a chunk of renegotiated contract value, even as new revenue remains lumpy and uncertain" — The Morning Context, on TCS CEO Krithivasan's disclosure.
- Bosch: "$2 billion including the Commerce Department funds" total cost for the Roseville, CA plant reconfiguration (bought from TSI Semiconductors in 2023); commercial production expected later in 2026.
- TSMC projected to hold 7-9x the advanced-node (3nm/2nm) capacity of Samsung and Intel combined by 2028; TSMC's 3nm lead times now exceed one year.
- Samsung's SF1.4 (1.4nm) mass production pushed from a planned 2027 to 2029; Intel Foundry's 14A external production now expected to slip beyond 2030.
- India Semiconductor Mission 2.0: Rs 8,000 crore FY27 outlay (largest single-year ISM allocation since programme launch); 13 projects approved across 7 states including Tata Electronics-PSMC Dholera (28nm logic/power fab), multiple OSAT/ATMP units, and new silicon-carbide/substrate projects. India now has three operational OSAT/assembly plants: Micron (memory), Tata Electronics (logic/display), CG Power (automotive, inaugurated by PM Modi in Sanand July 4).
- TSMC Q2 FY26 earnings due 2026-07-16 — first read on whether the 3nm/2nm capacity crunch and CoWoS/HBM bottlenecks show up in guidance.

## Signals

TCS's contract-value-concession disclosure sharpens the AI-margin-pressure thread this wiki has tracked across HDFC Bank and Kotak Mahindra Bank's FY26 workforce cuts — where banks are cutting headcount, IT services giants are instead giving up pricing power in renegotiations, suggesting the AI productivity dividend is being captured by customers rather than vendors across both sectors, just via different mechanisms (headcount vs. contract value). The Bosch CHIPS funding agreement and the TSMC/Samsung/Intel foundry-gap update (7-9x capacity lead by 2028, with both competitors' roadmaps slipping further) reinforces the wiki's running thesis that the foundry race is bifurcating into a clear leader and two laggards rather than narrowing — directly relevant to India's ATMP/OSAT-first semiconductor strategy, which sidesteps the advanced-node contest Intel/Samsung are losing. India's Rs 8,000cr ISM 2.0 FY27 outlay corroborates and sizes the Rs 1.25 lakh crore incentive-framework figure already logged Jul 13, confirming budget allocation is following the cabinet-level announcement rather than remaining aspirational.

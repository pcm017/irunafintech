## TL;DR

- Management framing: HDFC Bank is "in a different phase" — the integration phase (HDFC Ltd merger) is complete; now in optimisation phase; focus on deposits, liabilities, and ROA, not headline growth
- NIM 3.38% — management said NIM is "range-bound" and they are targeting ROA (1.96%) as the primary return metric, not NIM; TD repricing tailwind still incomplete (40–50 bps transmitted vs. full asset repricing)
- Technology-first: HDFC Bank built a unified AI platform (Agentic Mesh + Agentic Studio + MCP integration); 5 use cases in production, 14 in development — most detailed AI disclosure from any Indian private bank
- "Unprecedented events" disclosure: reference to chairman resignation and Dubai branch matter; legal review ongoing; management declined to quantify
- MSME franchise strength: #1 MSME bank in 15 of 28 states; business banking grew 20% YoY

## Key data / quotes

### NIM and ROA philosophy

> "We are focused on ROA, not NIM. NIM will fluctuate quarter to quarter as mix changes. What matters is that ROA is expanding — 1.94% FY26, 1.96% Q4. This is the right metric to track."
> — Srinivasan Vaidyanathan (CFO)

> "Of the 125 bps of repo rate cuts we've seen, 40–50 bps has been transmitted on the deposit side so far, versus full transmission on the asset side. As TD repricing unwinds gradually, CoF will decline and NIM will recover."
> — Paraphrased from Vaidyanathan Q&A

### Credit growth guidance

- **FY26 credit growth:** 12% — below system (13.5–13.9%)
- **Reason:** Deliberate. HDFC Bank is building the liability side first; loan growth will normalise as deposit market share improves
- **Deposit growth:** 14.4% in FY26 — ahead of credit, which is the intended sequencing
- FY27 guidance: loan growth to converge toward system growth as deposits normalise

### HDFC merger synergies

- **Liability penetration of home loan customers:** 36% at time of merger → **50%** in Q4 FY26 (home loan customer cross-sell into savings/deposits)
- Cross-sell success is the clearest evidence the merger is delivering expected synergies

### Technology — Unified AI Platform

HDFC Bank has built a three-layer AI operating model:

1. **Agentic Mesh** — unified data and model orchestration layer; enables AI agents to access bank systems
2. **Agentic Studio** — internal development environment for building AI agents
3. **MCP integration** — Model Context Protocol (Anthropic's MCP standard) integrated for external AI connectivity

Status: **5 use cases in production, 14 in development**

This is the most specific, structured AI disclosure from any Indian private bank. The MCP integration in particular signals HDFC Bank is building interoperable AI infrastructure, not just chatbots.

> "We are committed to being a technology-first bank. Our Agentic platform allows us to deploy AI across the customer journey — from acquisition to servicing to collections. We are seeing early productivity gains, and we expect meaningful contribution over the next 2–3 years."
> — Sashidhar Jagdishan (paraphrased)

### MSME / business banking

- **#1 MSME bank in 15 of 28 states** — India's largest MSME banking franchise by reach
- **Business banking grew 20% YoY** — the fastest-growing segment in Q4 FY26
- MSME credit quality: better than system; no specific slippage breakout shared

### Deposits and LCR

- LCR: **114%** (target band: 110–120%)
- CASA ratio 34% — declining trend as term deposits grow faster; management said this is expected and not concerning
- FII deposit flows improving; NRI deposits growing

### "Unprecedented events" — Chairman resignation and Dubai branch

Management referenced two "unprecedented events" during FY26:
1. **Chairman resignation** — Atanu Chakraborty stepped down; Keki Mistry serving as Interim Chairman
2. **Dubai branch matter** — regulatory inquiry; legal review ongoing; management said no material financial impact expected but declined to quantify

> "These are unprecedented events for an institution of our standing. We are handling them with appropriate legal rigour. We do not expect a material financial impact." — Paraphrased, Jagdishan

### Rate cycle and RBI posture

- Management: rate cycle has **paused** (RBI's Feb 2026 repo cut to 5.25% may have been the last for now given Hormuz/inflation risk)
- Impact: TD repricing tailwind is slower to materialise if no further cuts; NIM recovery timeline extends
- Management chose not to issue NIM guidance for FY27 — "We will see where deposits land"

### 3P product revenue

- 3P (third-party) product revenue growth: **3.5% YoY** in FY26 — tepid
- Management acknowledged this; said insurance and mutual fund distribution will recover as customer penetration deepens
- PB Fintech context: HDFC Bank is a major Paisabazaar and Policybazaar partner for credit and insurance respectively — cross-sell revenue growth flatness is notable

## Signals

- **HDFC Bank is the most conservative large private bank in India right now** — growing deposits ahead of loans, NIM-last mindset, AI-in-development, waiting for merger synergies to fully compound. This is a deliberate multi-year compounding approach, not a near-term profit maximiser.
- **Agentic Mesh + Agentic Studio + MCP** is the most sophisticated AI infrastructure framework disclosed by any Indian bank. If HDFC Bank converts 14 use cases in development into production at scale, the productivity dividend could be significant. Timeline: 2–3 years per management.
- **Chairman departure + Dubai branch** is an overhang — not quantified, legal review ongoing. This was the most unusual governance disclosure in a major Indian bank transcript in recent memory.
- **Deposit repricing tailwind is the key FY27 NIM lever** — 40–50 bps of TD repricing still incomplete; as older TDs mature and reprice lower, CoF will decline and NIM will recover without any RBI action.
- **MSME franchise** is a structural moat — #1 in 15/28 states is a penetration metric that takes decades to build. Business banking at 20% YoY is the bank's fastest-growing segment and a high-ROA segment.

# Bajaj Finance Q4 FY26 Investor Presentation

**Raw file:** `raw/results/Bajaj Finance Q4 FY26 Investor Presentation.pdf`

---

## TL;DR

- AUM crossed ₹5 lakh crore milestone, ending Q4 at ₹509,975 Cr (+22% YoY); PAT (before one-timers) +27% YoY to ₹5,660 Cr — a strong quarter across all key metrics.
- Credit quality inflecting: GNPA 1.01%, NNPA 0.41%; loan loss to AUM improved from 1.97% → 1.65% (new metric) YoY; stage 2+3 assets declined ₹430 Cr sequentially.
- FinAI transformation is the defining strategic bet: 203 dedicated AI employees, 27 autonomous agents live, 800+ agents targeted for FY27; "transformation, not use-case deployment" is the framing.
- FY27 guidance: AUM growth 20–24%, credit cost 145–160 bps, ROA 4.4–4.6%, ROE 19–20%; all contingent on geopolitical stabilisation.
- Gold loan scaling fast: +115% YoY, now 3.5% of AUM; targeting 5%+ by FY27 via aggressive branch expansion (1,507 gold loan branches).

## Signals

- **NBFC credit cycle turning.** Bajaj Finance's improving credit quality across vintage performance (3/6/9 MOB), stage 2+3 reduction, and GNPA stabilisation points to a broader NBFC credit cycle improvement — particularly as the MSME stress from FY25-H1FY26 works its way through.
- **Gold loan secular shift.** 115% growth isn't a one-off — BFL is investing heavily (1,507 branches, doubling down). Gold-backed credit gaining mainstream NBFC acceptance signals structural formalisation of this segment.
- **FinAI as operating leverage.** AI call center agent = one-third the cost; 6x loan processing capacity at peak. If BFL executes FY27 roadmap (800+ agents, full BOT coverage), opex/NTI improvement could be structural.
- **Geopolitical contingency flagged twice.** Management's FY27 guidance is explicitly conditional on Hormuz stabilisation and macro stability — unusual candour suggesting genuine concern.
- **Paytm competitive context.** Morning Context newsletter on same date asks: "how will Paytm battle Bajaj Finance without an NBFC licence?" — the implicit answer from this deck is that BFL's 119M customer franchise + AI-powered underwriting + NBFC licence is a significant moat.

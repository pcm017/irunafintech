## Key Data / Quotes

### Indian Banking
- **SBI acquisition financing:** First-ever deal completed. Acquisition/leveraged finance historically dominated by Citi, Deutsche, JPMorgan, and ICICI Bank. SBI's entry signals PSU banks expanding product capabilities beyond vanilla corporate credit.
- **SMFG India Credit** (formerly Fullerton India, now 100% owned by Sumitomo Mitsui Financial Group): Supply chain/trade finance product rollout by Q3 FY27. Targeting vendor and dealer ecosystems of corporates — a sticky, secured lending segment.
- **PCI (Payment Council of India) embraces AI** — the industry body representing payment aggregators and gateways has formally adopted AI for fraud detection, customer onboarding, and transaction monitoring.

### Regulatory
- **SEBI vs. Rajesh Exports:** Interim order alleges massive irregularities. "The long arc of unfinished probes, hearings and appeals makes closure distant" — enforcement trajectory uncertain. Rajesh Exports is a BSE index component and one of India's largest gold jewellery exporters.
- **India govt weighing FPI tax incentives:** Considering tax incentives on G-Sec coupon income for foreign investors — mechanism to reduce sovereign borrowing costs post India's JPMorgan GBI-EM inclusion.
- **US-Iran conflict → Indian corporate margins:** Brent above $90-95/bbl squeezing margins in transport, logistics, aviation (IndiGo "next quarter could look worse"), FMCG with freight exposure, and banks/NBFCs lending to these sectors.

### Global Fintech / Payments
- **Stripe + Visa + Mastercard + Coinbase stablecoin:** If all four launch a joint stablecoin backed by payment rails + crypto exchange infrastructure, it commoditises Circle's USDC. Relevant for India: RBI's stance on USD stablecoins, VASP framework, and competitive positioning of INR-denominated UPI ecosystem.
- **Ramp (B2B spend management):** Upgraded accounting automation — steady product build-out in corporate financial workflow automation.
- **CFOs running AI strategy:** In financial services, CFOs (not CTOs) are becoming the primary AI investment decision-makers — fintech's early ROI measurement discipline gave the sector a first-mover advantage in AI adoption.

### Insurance / Wealthtech
- **Honeycomb Insurance:** $40M raise for AI-driven commercial property underwriting — challenger to Chubb. Continued VC appetite for AI-driven commercial insurance despite consumer insurtech slowdown.
- **Kotak Alternate Asset Managers:** Closes 14th real estate fund at ~$1B (₹~8,300 crore) — signals institutional appetite for Indian real estate as an alternative asset class remains strong.

### IPO / Capital Markets
- **SpaceX IPO:** Index providers (MSCI, S&P, FTSE Russell) pre-emptively rewriting inclusion methodology. SpaceX's projected weight would force passive funds to absorb massive simultaneous position changes. Parallel to Aramco Saudi inclusion. Pre-IPO perpetual futures already traded on Santos, Frec platforms.

## Signals

1. **PSU banking product expansion:** SBI's acquisition financing entry is structurally significant — PSU banks competing with foreign/private banks in high-margin products signals deepening of India's capital markets.
2. **Stablecoin consolidation risk for Circle/USDC:** A Stripe+Visa+Mastercard+Coinbase stablecoin would be a formidable regulated competitor. Circle's first-mover advantage could be eroded by this institutional combine.
3. **India FPI bond flows:** Government considering tax incentives to sustain foreign inflows into G-Secs. This is a positive signal for India sovereign bond yields — lower borrowing costs at the margin.
4. **Corporate margin pressure continuing:** US-Iran conflict transmission to India Inc margins is now documented across sectors — aviation (IndiGo), logistics, FMCG. This is a credit risk signal for banks/NBFCs lending to these sectors.
5. **ECB CBDC strategic hardening:** Digital euro is now explicitly framed as geopolitical sovereignty, not just payments product. RBI's digital rupee strategy will likely adopt similar framing in external communication.

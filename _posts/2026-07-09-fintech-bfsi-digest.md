# Fintech & BFSI Digest — 2026-07-09

## TL;DR

- **PM Modi announced UPI will integrate with Indonesia's payment system** during his bilateral visit, alongside defense and trade cooperation agreements.
- **SEBI shifted FPI registration fees from USD to INR denomination**; **RBI reiterated its crypto-prohibition stance**, with the tax department flagging evasion risk.
- **CIBIL data shows credit card growth turning flattish** as new card issuance slows, even as bank credit growth more broadly is "seen moderating."
- **Madhabi Puri Buch's Choti SIP (₹250 SIP) financial-inclusion scheme has failed to gain traction** more than a year after launch.
- NFRA raised the bar for audit sign-offs and opened a probe into Rajesh Exports; ICAI paused "global networking norms" after Big 4 lobbying.
- No fintech-funding stories cleared the relevance filter today.

## Key data / quotes

- ETGovernment: PM Modi announced UPI-Indonesia payment-system integration; told the Indonesian parliament "India follows path of development, not expansionism"; India urging the US to reconsider a proposed 12.5% forced-labor tariff.
- ETLegalWorld: SEBI notified FPI Regulations amendments replacing the USD-denominated registration fee with rupee denomination; RBI reiterated its crypto policy "leaning towards prohibition," warning crypto assets/stablecoins pose evasion risk (tax department corroborating).
- ETBFSI: CIBIL data shows credit card growth turning flattish on slower new-card issuance; bank credit growth more broadly "seen moderating"; Star Health and SBI Life flagged for strong FY27 growth.
- The Morning Context: Madhabi Puri Buch's Choti SIP (Rs 250 SIP) scheme, launched ~a year ago with an explicit financial-inclusion goal, has not caught on with mutual fund investors.
- ETLegalWorld: Irdai now requires state-run insurers to provision wage costs annually; NFRA (Chairperson Nitin Gupta) raised the bar for audit sign-offs and opened a probe into a Rajesh Exports financial-misstatement case; ICAI paused "global networking norms" after Big 4 lobbying.
- ETLegalWorld: a Haryana consumer court ordered Tata AIG to pay a policyholder over Rs 60K after wrongly rejecting a mediclaim, noting "insurance companies are only interested in premiums."
- India CPI 3.93% (May 2026), WPI 8.3% (April 2026, provisional) — corroborated by ETRetail's report that thali (meal) costs rose 5% in June on food/LPG prices.

## Signals

**Change detection:** The UPI-Indonesia integration extends UPI's cross-border footprint (following an earlier 8-country milestone) — a continuation, not a new direction, but a concrete deliverable from Modi's Indonesia visit.

**Root cause traversal:** The Choti SIP's failure to gain traction, more than a year post-launch, suggests India's retail-investment-participation policy tools may need product-design rework rather than just marketing — worth tracking against ULI and other RBI-backed inclusion initiatives for a pattern.

**Sizing / impact:** The credit-card-issuance flattening, set against still-strong overall bank credit growth (17.65-17.7% YoY per the same-week macro-signals snapshot), signals a bifurcation between unsecured consumer credit and broader lending — a thread worth watching in upcoming NBFC/bank card-issuer results.

## Signals

**1. Gold breaks below $4,000 for the first time in weeks.** Gold fell to $3,982.20 (-1.40%), its lowest print since before the mid-June safe-haven bid built up ($4,040 on June 27). The driver is a rotation into risk assets — Opening Bell Daily's "market losers are winning" theme (small-caps/average stocks outperforming Big Tech) signals broad risk appetite is returning, reducing gold's safe-haven premium even as COT positioning (+181,300 net long) stays elevated. For India: a sustained sub-$4,000 gold price eases the import bill for the world's largest gold consumer market and reduces the drag gold/silver have been placing on headline CPI (core CPI ex-gold/silver has been running ~150bps below headline).

**2. Goldman cuts Q4 Brent forecast to $80 from $90 — the supply-normalisation thesis is now consensus among major banks.** Combined with Brent's intraday dip to ~$72 (lowest since Feb 27), this confirms institutional forecasters are pricing Persian Gulf exports returning to pre-war levels by end-July. If this holds, India's crude import relief extends through Q4 rather than being a transient June-July window — reinforcing the case for a deeper, more durable RBI easing cycle rather than a single one-off cut.

**3. India forex reserves jump $20.46Bn in a single reporting week to $702.78B.** This is the sharpest weekly reserve recovery of 2026, re-crossing the $700B milestone after dipping to ~$672.587B (week ended June 20). The combination of FCNR(B) inflows (Canara Bank and peers ramping per the June 28 fintech digest), continued FII debt buying, and RBI stepping back from active dollar-selling as INR stabilised near 94.35 explains the scale of the jump. This materially expands RBI's intervention capacity heading into H2 FY27 and is a strong tailwind into the August MPC.

**4. India equities show a rare combination: low VIX (13.43), bullish PCR (0.808), but a -1.2 to -1.3% pullback.** Nifty and Sensex both pulled back on June 29 even as options positioning stayed constructive and volatility stayed unusually low — consistent with profit-taking after the multi-week Iran-deal rally rather than a sentiment reversal. The FTSE India rejig effective June 30 (expected ~₹5,000 Cr passive inflow) is a near-term technical support event that should offset some of the pullback.

**5. SCOTUS blocks the firing of a Fed governor — a Fed-independence signal during the Warsh transition.** With a new Fed chair (Warsh) already reshaping rate-path expectations this year, the Supreme Court's intervention to block a governor's removal reinforces that the Fed's institutional independence remains legally protected even amid political pressure. This reduces one tail risk (a politically-compromised FOMC) that had been an implicit overhang on the "4% is the new 2%" inflation-target-erosion narrative tracked earlier in June.

# PB Fintech — HSIE Research Update: "Scaling with purpose; right-to-win intact"

**Raw file:** `raw/results/PB Fintech - Update - Jun26 - HSIE-202606010635031047409.pdf`
**Published:** June 1, 2026 | HDFC Securities Institutional Equities (HSIE)
**Analysts:** Shobhit Sharma, Krishnan ASV

---

## TL;DR

- HSIE maintains BUY rating with TP INR 2,180 (implying 61x FY28E EPS of ₹35.5); CMP INR 1,703 (May 29, 2026).
- Policybazaar's right-to-win (RTW) is intact: superior CACs, better conversion, profit-sharing model with insurers, data-driven risk profiling — collectively building structural moat vs. traditional agency distribution.
- Paisabazaar: FY27 is an inflection year — management guided for bottom-line accretive status. Fixed costs stable; operating leverage to drive PAT margin expansion.
- Commission regulation risk is manageable: Policybazaar survived the GST rationalization episode; phygital model not easily replicable by Bima Sugam.
- No change to EPS estimates (FY27E/FY28E); FY28E revenue upgraded slightly (+2%); operating profit revised up +11% on better margin assumptions.

### Key HSIE thesis points

**Policybazaar RTW:**
- Top-5 life insurance distributor nationally
- 3rd largest retail health player (18-20% market share)
- Protection persistency: 93-94% (13th month); savings persistency: 85-86%
- Retail health loss ratio: 54% FY25 → 51% 9MFY26 (significantly below industry)
- Only ~5% of 10-year health customers have claimed more than once — data validates PB's customer quality
- Profit-sharing model shifting: PB now a data-driven partner for insurers, not just distributor

**Paisabazaar:**
- Meaningful strategic reset post leadership transition
- FY27E: inflection year — management guided bottom-line accretive
- Fixed cost base stabilized (~₹200 Cr+); operating leverage to unlock
- Revenue compression (lower credit card issuances, take rate pressure in unsecured) offset by disbursal recovery

**Commission regulation:**
- GST rationalization (prior episode): Policybazaar demonstrated resilience
- Phygital model not easily replicable by Bima Sugam (motor + health disruption risk initially limited)
- Management track record of navigating regulatory headwinds without structural damage

**PB Health:**
- Positioning as meaningful healthcare ecosystem enabler
- Improving outcomes for policyholders and insurers across value chain
- External capital raise underway; PB Fintech retains pro-rata rights (~26-28% stake)

## Signals

- **HSIE's FY28E OP margin of 18% (vs 7% FY26) is the key call.** The implied expansion of ~11pp over two years is driven by renewal revenue compounding (near-100% EBITDA flow-through) + Paisabazaar operating leverage. If renewals continue at 40%+ CAGR, this is achievable without heroic assumptions.
- **CMP of ₹1,703 vs. TP ₹2,180 = 28% upside** at time of note — suggesting the market is not fully pricing the profitability trajectory. Potential de-rating risk from commission regulation hangs over sentiment.
- **Bima Sugam is the key regulatory watch item.** HSIE draws comfort from PB's phygital edge but flags it as an ongoing structural risk — initially motor + health, then extending to term.

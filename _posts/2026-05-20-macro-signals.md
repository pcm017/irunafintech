## Signals

- **Bond vigilantes winning early rounds:** 30Y Treasury at 5.19% signals markets will not simply trust Warsh/Trump fiscal promises. This creates a structurally higher global rate environment — negative for EM capital flows, including India.
- **Oil shock partially absorbed:** Trump calling off Iran strike is the first clear de-escalation signal in the West Asia crisis, but Brent still elevated. India fuel price hikes (two in one week) confirm government is now passing through costs.
- **India–US rate spread compressing:** As US long yields rise faster than Indian yields, the FII carry-trade incentive narrows. Adverse for rupee and FII debt flows.
- **Gold/precious metals pullback:** Gold and silver declining on "higher for longer" signal from US inflation data — counters the safe-haven bid.
- **RBI gold repatriation:** 77% of India's gold now held domestically (up from ~50%+ previously). Fastest repatriation since 1991. Reduces counterparty risk but does not change the monetary balance.
- **WPI/CPI divergence:** WPI at 8.3% (fuel-driven) vs. CPI at 3.48% (food-benign) — the divergence is unusual. If fuel costs seep into food/transport CPI in coming months, RBI's rate pause may be challenged.

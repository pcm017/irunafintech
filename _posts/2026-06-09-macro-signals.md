## Signals

**Global risk-off intensifying:** Broadcom's AI chip revenue miss triggered the market's second major selloff of June (after the May jobs report selloff on June 5). This one targets semiconductor/AI equities specifically — the highest-multiple part of the market. The $1.3T wipeout in two sessions is a valuation reset for AI chip names and implies slower-than-expected near-term revenue ramp from custom AI silicon. Nvidia supply chain (SK Hynix) remains intact but demand-side guidance disappointment raises questions about AI capex ROI materialisation timelines.

**Gold 200-day MA break — regime signal.** Gold breaking below its 200-day moving average for the first time since October 2023 is a structural chart signal watched by systematic funds and trend-followers. At this level, momentum models will begin shorting gold, adding selling pressure on top of the rate-driven fundamental headwind. The COT net long at ~22,800 is modest and below June 3 levels, reducing the risk of a forced liquidation cascade. Resolution: either Iran-Hormuz re-escalation pulls gold back above $4,400 (safe-haven bid), or higher-for-longer US rates push gold to $4,000–4,100 test.

**India: GDP beats but outlook revised lower.** Q4 FY26 GDP at 7.8% confirms India's domestic demand resilience through the Hormuz shock. But RBI lowered FY27 GDP estimate to 6.6% and raised CPI to 5.1% — the policy outlook is simultaneously more inflationary and less growth-supportive. This combination (higher inflation, lower growth) reduces RBI's ability to cut rates and support equity valuations in H2 FY27. The Q3 CPI spike to 5.9% (if it materialises) would push RBI back toward a hawkish posture.

**Nifty PCR <0.80 = contrarian buy signal.** Historically, Nifty PCR below 0.80 marks extreme bearish positioning that has often preceded near-term bounces (weekly expiry June 11). Combined with India VIX at 17.09, the market is pricing in significant downside — which is itself a mean-reversion signal for domestic buyers and SIP-driven DII flows.

**Iran partial ceasefire = oil relief catalyst.** Brent +4% on Iran's statement about ending military operations against Israel is the first positive geopolitical signal in weeks. If confirmed, this could be the trigger for a more sustained oil reversal — critical for India's macro relief (lower CAD, INR recovery, RBI policy space). Pending confirmation and Hormuz traffic normalisation.

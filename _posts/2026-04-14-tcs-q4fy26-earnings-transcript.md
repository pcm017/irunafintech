# TCS Q4 & Full Year FY26 — Earnings Conference Call Transcript

**Filed:** April 14, 2026 (call held April 9, 2026 post-board meeting)
**Raw file:** `raw/results/07ae2d32-1050-4e80-96ff-eb2d98378d4e.pdf`
**Management:** K. Krithivasan (CEO & MD), Aarthi Subramanian (COO & ED), Samir Seksaria (CFO), Sudeep Kunnumal (CHRO)

---

## TL;DR

- Q4 FY26: third consecutive quarter of sequential CC growth (+1.2% QoQ CC); USD revenue $7.621B; INR revenue ₹70,698 crore. Operating margin 25.3% (best in 4 years).
- FY26 full year: CC revenue declined 2.4% YoY (demand normalisation); $40.7B TCV (5 mega deals); operating margin 25%, expansion of 70 bps — the highest since FY22.
- AI revenue annualised at **$2.3 billion** — accelerating impressively QoQ.
- HyperVault AI data centre business making "significant progress" toward 1 GW capacity; customer commitments secured, land parcels finalised.
- FY27 outlook: "very positive and confident" — expects stronger first half, regular seasonality. No specific revenue guidance provided.
- Restructuring program **completed**. Final dividend ₹31/share; total FY26 dividend ₹110/share.

**Order book highlights (Q4):**
- Q4 TCV: $12 billion including 3 mega deals — Marks & Spencer (renewal + expansion), leading UK telecom operator, leading US healthcare/pharmacy retailer
- Every client revenue band ($1M+, $50M+, $100M+) saw additions in Q4 for first time in ~2 years

**AI & strategic:**
- AI revenues annualised: **$2.3 billion** (up from ~$1.5B implied at prior quarter rate)
- HyperVault: AI data centre build-out targeting 1 GW; engaging hyperscalers, semiconductor companies, model providers; TCS is integration partner across infra, engineering, AI services
- Strategic partnership with OpenAI, AMD, ServiceNow announced in Q4; Anthropic partnership to be announced "in the near future" (COO Aarthi Subramanian)
- "Human + AI" service redesign framework: every service line being redefined
- Build–Partner–Acquire strategy: margin reinvestment into capabilities, ecosystem partnerships, and selective acquisitions

**FY27 outlook (Krithi):**
- "We are getting into our next year with a lot of positivity and confidence"
- Expects stronger first half (Q1/Q2 > Q4), consistent with historical seasonality
- Clients not deferring spends due to new model launches (e.g. Claude Mythos mentioned by analyst Sudheer Guntupalli)

**Notable quote (CEO on AI demand):**
> "As the model capability improves, there'll be more interest in seeing how [clients] can leverage... clients are quite interested in leveraging it. They know this will be constantly evolving. There is no major benefit in waiting for the next best model. Clients are willing to invest now."

## Signals

- **Demand recovery**: Third consecutive quarter of CC growth signals the industry-wide demand trough (FY26 CC: -2.4%) is passing. FY27 likely to see positive CC growth.
- **AI revenue materialising**: $2.3B annualised AI revenue is now a meaningful number; Aarthi confirmed client interest increases with each new model capability.
- **HyperVault is a new business line, not an experiment**: Land, customer commitments, and ecosystem partnerships announced — this is a multi-year capex play positioning TCS as AI infrastructure provider, not just IT services.
- **Anthropic partnership confirmation**: TCS COO explicitly named Anthropic as a strategic partnership "to be announced in the near future" — validates Anthropic's enterprise India footprint expansion.
- **Margin discipline**: Operating margin at 25% despite deliberate reinvestment (external consultants, go-to-market spend, partner investments) shows structural cost advantage over peers.

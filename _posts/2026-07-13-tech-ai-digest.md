## TL;DR

- **Four major coding-capable models shipped within 48 hours**: Grok 4.5 (SpaceXAI), GPT-5.6 (OpenAI, as Sol/Terra/Luna), Muse Spark 1.1 (Meta's first paid closed model), and SWE-1.7 (Cognition) — each a distinct strategic bet on pricing, context window, or specialization.
- **Microsoft cut ~4,800 jobs (2.1% of workforce) on July 6**, mostly from Xbox, spinning out four gaming studios; Xbox's own margin sits at ~3% versus Microsoft's company-wide ~39%.
- **TCS plans up to 8,900 AI deployment engineers** — a direct India-IT competitive response to OpenAI/Microsoft's forward-deployed-engineer hiring push; TCS employee additions hit a 4-year high.
- **India Semiconductor Mission 2.0's Rs 1.25 lakh crore incentive framework cleared the finance ministry's expenditure finance committee** (nearly double ISM 1.0's Rs 76,000 crore) — cabinet approval still pending; focus has shifted from fab-centric to full-stack (equipment, materials, packaging, design services).
- **CG Power's OSAT unit in Sanand was inaugurated by PM Modi on July 4** — India's third such facility after Micron and Kaynes.
- A new "open-web reproduction number" framework (Harvard economist Alex Chan) formalises the AI-is-eating-publisher-traffic thesis: Google Search referral traffic to small publishers fell 60% (2023-2025); AI overview clicks are ~1% vs. 15% without.
- **Cloudflare activated HTTP 402 ("Payment Required") via its x402 protocol** — ~155M cumulative machine-to-machine transactions (~$41M volume) have already settled.

## Signals

**Change detection:** The coding-model release cadence (4 major models in 48 hours) is a step-change in competitive intensity versus the prior pattern of staggered releases — pricing has also compressed sharply (sub-flagship models at $1-2/$4-6 per million tokens vs. $25-50 for frontier tiers).

**Root cause traversal:** Microsoft's Xbox layoffs are a direct consequence of a structural gaming-revenue model shift (Game Pass subscription cannibalising per-game spend, especially among 18-24 year-olds), not a general AI-driven cut — worth distinguishing from the AI-job-displacement narrative running elsewhere in this digest (TCS AI engineers, India tech layoffs).

**Sizing / impact:** For India, TCS's 8,900 AI-deployment-engineer plan plus ISM 2.0's Rs 1.25 lakh crore framework are the two most consequential data points — the former a direct competitive response in the AI-services labor market, the latter nearly doubling ISM's fiscal commitment while explicitly acknowledging a demand-side gap for domestic chip-design startups that the supply-side incentives don't address.

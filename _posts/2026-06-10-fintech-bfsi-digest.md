## Signals

- **MCC loophole (Rs 3,000Cr) is a systemic interchange design flaw — not isolated to one player.** Merchant category codes determine interchange rates; systematic misclassification (e.g., travel merchants coded as retail, luxury as staples) diverts interchange from issuers to merchants/acquirers. ICICI Bank pursuing recovery signals India's largest private bank believes this is material. RBI scrutiny will force card networks (Visa/Mastercard) and acquirers to tighten MCC assignment protocols — operational cost increase for fintechs with creative MCC strategies.
- **UPI in 8 countries at 23.2Bn txns/month is now a geopolitical-payments story.** France and Qatar joining the network positions UPI as a G20-level infrastructure, not just South Asian diaspora rails. India-France diplomatic momentum (Rafale, PM Modi ties) and India-Qatar energy links (LNG) make both additions strategically significant. The remittance corridor opportunity — India receives ~$120B in remittances annually — is the commercial prize.
- **Revolut at $115B (centicorn) and OpenAI S-1 simultaneously signal the "AI-finance IPO supercycle" is beginning.** With Anthropic at $965B and OpenAI at $852B+, the three largest AI companies are all in or approaching public markets within a 6-month window. This will set AI company valuation anchors for 2026-2027. Revolut's valuation ($115B vs $75B in Nov 2025 = +53%) shows investor appetite is accelerating, not cooling.
- **Deel stablecoin float ($22B) — the first major B2B fintech fully converting operating float to stablecoin yield.** If Deel earns even 4% on $22B, that's $880M/year — close to its entire revenue. This is the clearest example yet of fintech companies using crypto yield to subsidise lower pricing and outcompete banks. Banks that hold payroll float income should model for attrition risk. India's Payroll/HR fintech layer (Razorpay Payroll, Zoho Payroll, Keka) should expect Deel to lower pricing aggressively.

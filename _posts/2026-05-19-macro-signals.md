## Signals

1. **The global bond selloff is now a multi-decade repricing event.** Germany Bund at 15-year highs (last seen May 2011 — ECB debt crisis peak) and JGB at 30-year highs (last seen May 1997 — Asian financial crisis era) are not routine moves. This signals a structural fiscal premium being priced globally: high debt + energy shock + geopolitical risk = higher long-end yields everywhere. India's G-Sec at 7.1% is partially insulated (still within historical range) but the India-US spread compression (+250 bps, down from historical 300+ bps) limits RBI rate-cut room.

2. **WPI 8.3% / fuel inflation 24.71% closes the CPI gap argument.** April CPI was 3.48% — deceptively low because retail fuel prices weren't hiked until May 17-18. But WPI fuel inflation of 24.71% (vs. 1.05% prior month) confirms the Hormuz energy cost is already in the producer price system. May CPI will move materially higher; June CPI even more so. This directly affects RBI's rate path calculus.

3. **Brent easing from $109 to $107 but not decisive.** Oil remains elevated and well above the government's fiscal comfort zone (~$80-85). The slight pullback may reflect US-China farm deal signalling (economic normalisation) or simply position taking — not a fundamental Hormuz resolution.

4. **US-China $17B farm goods commitment** is the first concrete, quantified trade deal outcome from the Trump-Xi summit framework. Signals: (1) US-China partial economic re-engagement is real; (2) Agricultural sector benefits directly; (3) No Hormuz resolution embedded in deal terms (energy/Hormuz not mentioned).

5. **India forex reserves recovering (+$6.3B WoW)** after peak-to-trough fall of >$40B. RBI has been intervening to defend INR. Recovery suggests either FII flows stabilising or RBI pausing intervention. Forex reserve headroom matters for INR defence capacity.

6. **COT: EUR/JPY bullish specs = USD softening signal.** If the dollar weakens over coming weeks (as COT positions suggest), this would be modestly supportive for EM assets including India — partially offsetting the Hormuz energy cost headwind.

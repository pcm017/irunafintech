## Signals

**Change detection:** No fresh trading session (Sunday) means this snapshot is primarily a geopolitical-narrative update layered on an unchanged market picture. The one genuinely new element is the "guardian of the waterway" transit-fee framing — a shift from pure military blockade toward a quasi-toll-collection posture on Hormuz shipping.

**Root cause:** Iran's IRGC retaliation (supertanker strikes, Kuwait/Bahrain missile and drone attacks) is the direct trigger for the US "guardian" framing — the administration is repositioning the blockade as a service (safety-for-fee) rather than pure military denial, which may be an attempt to normalise a prolonged, revenue-generating US presence in the strait rather than a temporary war measure.

**Sizing / impact:** A >50% WoW fall in Hormuz transits is a large real-economy disruption if sustained — even though Brent's price reaction (still ~$86-88, not spiking further) suggests markets see this as consistent with the already-priced blockade rather than a fresh escalation. For India, this reinforces rather than changes the existing risk read: crude import bill pressure continues, and the fresh FII debt-inflow breakdown (₹9,853 Cr MTD, split FAR/general route) shows India debt markets remain resilient despite the geopolitical overhang.

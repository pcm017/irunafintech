# Paytm Q4 FY26 Earnings Call Transcript

**Call date:** May 7, 2026 (filed by company secretary May 12, 2026)
**Raw file:** `raw/results/a39d5732-4edd-4b8f-8269-767d21ccd4c2.pdf`

---

## TL;DR

- FY27 revenue acceleration guided across three growth buckets: established businesses (payments, merchant loans), headwind-to-tailwind (advertising + personal loans), and emerging (wealth as third pillar)
- EBITDA target of 15–20% margin in 2.5–3 years (implying FY28–FY29); current 6% starts the compounding
- Paytm Check-in (agentic travel experiment) showing 7–8x funnel conversion vs. tap-to-book; framed as different brand, distinct from core Paytm
- No NBFC licence interest — management explicitly reiterated the asset-light, distribution-first model as a strategic choice, not a constraint
- Merchant loan penetration at 7% of subscription merchants (~1%/year growth from 6%); three equal growth levers: base expansion, penetration rate, ticket size (~15% each)

## Key data / quotes

### Revenue growth framework (FY27)

Three revenue buckets described by management:

1. **Large established businesses** — Payment services + merchant loans (already at scale, compounding at high teens to 20%+ YoY)
2. **Headwind-to-tailwind businesses** — Advertising (recovering from PA-PG restrictions) + Personal loans (recovering from lender caution post-FY25 stress)
3. **Emerging / third pillar** — Wealth management (Paytm Money / investment products)

> "We have three large, compounding engines. The established businesses are running at high growth. The headwind businesses — advertising and personal loans — are both turning. Wealth is building from a small base but will be the third large pillar." [Paraphrased from management commentary]

### EBITDA guidance

- **Current:** 6% EBITDA margin (FY26)
- **Target:** 15–20% EBITDA margin in **2.5–3 years** from the call (i.e., approximately FY28–FY29)
- No quarterly step-up guidance given; management cautioned against linear extrapolation

### Postpaid (BNPL)

> "Postpaid is ramping phenomenally well — faster than the prior iteration of Postpaid before the regulatory pause."

- Active lender partnerships have expanded
- Postpaid is contributing to both financial services distribution revenue and consumer engagement depth

### Personal loans

- **Recovering** — lenders who had pulled back in FY25 on unsecured stress are returning
- DLG (default loss guarantee) proportion: flat to slightly higher vs. Q4 FY26 levels
- Management: personal loan volumes will be a visible tailwind in FY27

### Merchant loans

- **Penetration:** 7% of subscription merchants (up from 6% a year prior; ~1%/year progression)
- **Three growth levers, roughly equal contribution (~15% each):**
  1. Base expansion (more subscription merchants = more addressable pool)
  2. Penetration rate (7% → higher, as data and trust deepen)
  3. Ticket size (rising as merchants grow and borrow more)
- **Repeat borrowers** >50% of disbursements — annuity character confirmed
- Management: the merchant loan business is still early-innings; the 7% penetration has significant runway

### Paytm Check-in (agentic AI experiment)

- **Nature:** Fully agentic AI travel booking app — conversational, no tap-to-book interface
- **Brand:** Separate from core Paytm brand ("Check-in" name, distinct positioning)
- **Conversion:** 7–8x funnel conversion vs. traditional tap-to-book (management claim; Q3 FY26 transcript cited 30%+ overall funnel conversion)
- Management framing: "this is our experiment with agentic AI — we want to see how fully autonomous agents change conversion and user behaviour before we embed this in the core app"

### AI strategy

- **Investment rule:** "Any new investment we make, it will only be in AI." Management stated this as a hard allocation rule for FY27 and beyond.
- AI-only new investments: AI Soundbox (voice, Indian language business insights), AI for merchant acquisition targeting, AI for collections, AI for fraud prevention
- AI monetisation model for Paytm's enterprise AI: subscription (SaaS) + usage (per-agent call) — two-part tariff

### Payment processing margin

- Structural at **>4 bps** (unchanged)
- Two drivers: (1) product improvement (better UPI instruments, cross-sell on payment confirmation page), (2) credit instruments on UPI rails (credit cards via UPI, Postpaid, BNPL) — higher-margin transactions
- Management: margin will not rapidly compress from here; product mix is shifting toward credit instruments

### Online GMV

- Growing at "meaningful double digits" — management avoided specific number but indicated strong recovery
- Online GMV was depressed post-PA-PG restriction implementation; now reverting

### Card machines / EMI

- **>50% of card machines** now enabled for EMI transactions
- EMI via card machine = higher MDR, better margin; this is a structural mix shift

### NBFC licence

> "We are not super excited about taking an NBFC licence. Our model is better as a distributor — we get the economics without the balance sheet risk."

This resolves **Contradiction** in `wiki/companies/paytm.md` (was flagged as pending confirmation from transcript). Position is explicit: no NBFC licence planned.

### PIDF / "Other Operating Revenue" collapse (resolves Open Question #6)

- Management confirmed the 30–40% PIDF offset was achieved in Q4 FY26 as guided
- The sharp drop in "Other Operating Revenue" from ₹92 Cr (Q3) to ₹10 Cr (Q4) is consistent with PIDF incentives winding down (PIDF officially expired December 2025) and reclassification of residual balances
- No new PIDF-equivalent programme announced

## Signals

- **Postpaid recovering faster than prior cycle** — this is the highest-margin consumer credit product for Paytm (distribution fee without balance sheet risk). A faster ramp in FY27 is material to financial services revenue.
- **AI-only investment discipline** is a real operating signal — Paytm is cutting non-AI discretionary spend and concentrating on AI-first product lines. This is consistent with the indirect expense decline in FY26.
- **NBFC position is strategic, not cyclical** — Paytm will not seek an NBFC licence in the foreseeable future. The "asset-light distributor beats single-NBFC" argument is now explicitly on record.
- **Check-in agentic results** (7–8x conversion) are the most concrete AI product metric Paytm has disclosed. If verified, it signals a step-function improvement in consumer engagement economics.
- **FY28–FY29 EBITDA margin target** (15–20%) implies roughly 3x margin expansion from 6%. At current revenue trajectory, this implies absolute EBITDA of ₹2,500–3,500 Cr range. Significant if achieved.

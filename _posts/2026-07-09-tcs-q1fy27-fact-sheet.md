## TL;DR

- INR revenue ₹72,275 Cr (+2.2% QoQ, +13.9% YoY); USD revenue $7,624 Mn (flat QoQ, +2.7% YoY); constant-currency growth just +0.4% QoQ, +3.2% YoY — the INR/USD divergence reflects rupee depreciation more than real growth.
- Operating margin compressed to 24.0% (from 25.3% in Q4 FY26) and net margin to 19.2% (from 19.4%) — the first sequential margin decline in several quarters, driven by employee cost rising to 58.3% of revenue (from 56.8% Q4 FY26).
- Net income ₹13,849 Cr (+8.5% YoY, +0.95% QoQ); EPS ₹38.28 (up from ₹35.27 Q1 FY26, ₹37.92 Q4 FY26).
- Headcount rose to 593,798 (+9,279 QoQ) — first meaningful net addition after several quarters of net reduction; voluntary LTM attrition eased slightly to 13.6% (from 13.7%).
- Order book TCV $9.5Bn (North America $4.7Bn, BFSI $2.5Bn, Consumer $1.4Bn); client additions across all bands ($10M+ +5, $5M+ +8, $1M+ +4 QoQ).

## Key data / quotes

- "Operating Margin at 24.0%... Net Margin at 19.2%... Cash flow from operations at 93.0% of net profit" — Q1 FY27 Performance Highlights.
- Clients $10M+ up by 5; $5M+ up by 8; $1M+ up by 4 QoQ.
- Order book TCV at $9.5 Bn; North America TCV at $4.7 Bn; BFSI TCV at $2.5 Bn; Consumer Business TCV at $1.4 Bn.
- Closing headcount: 593,798 (up from 584,519 at Q4 FY26 close). Voluntary LTM attrition 13.6% in IT Services.
- Revenue by vertical: BFSI 32.1% of total (+3.6% INR YoY), Consumer Business 15.0% (-2.2% INR YoY, the only shrinking major vertical), Life Sciences & Healthcare 10.3%, Manufacturing 8.7%, Technology & Services 8.5%.
- Revenue by geography: North America 48.3% of total (+1.7% INR YoY, +13.0% but that's a mislabel — actually +1.7% QoQ INR / +13.0% YoY INR), India 6.2% (+7.7% QoQ INR, +23.3% YoY INR — the fastest-growing geography by far), UK 17.2%, Continental Europe 15.4%.

## Signals

TCS's operating margin decline to 24.0% (from 25.3%) is the first concrete quarterly evidence behind the qualitative disclosure captured in today's 2026-07-14 tech-ai digest — CEO Krithivasan's admission that AI is forcing the company to concede contract value in renegotiations even as new revenue stays lumpy. The employee cost ratio rising to 58.3% of revenue while CC revenue growth stayed muted at +0.4% QoQ is the numeric expression of that pricing concession: costs are not falling in step with the AI productivity narrative, and margin is absorbing the gap. India, at just 6.2% of revenue but growing 23.3% YoY (INR) — nearly 3x the company average — is now TCS's fastest-growing geography by a wide margin, consistent with the wiki's running India-domestic-tech-demand thread (MeitY AI vendor selection, IndiaAI Mission). The Consumer Business vertical's -2.2% YoY INR contraction is the only shrinking major vertical and worth tracking against Nykaa/Swiggy/Eternal-style consumer-tech demand softness already logged elsewhere in the wiki.

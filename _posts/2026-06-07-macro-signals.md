## Signals

- **Nasdaq ▼4.18% on jobs data** = rate-sensitive AI/tech equity is the primary casualty of higher-for-longer rate environment. The AI capex super-cycle is being tested by the fundamental question: can AI infrastructure generate returns before rates compress valuations?
- **Gold at 2026 lows ($4,342) despite geopolitical risk** = rate expectations dominate safe-haven demand. If US 10Y stays above 4.5%, gold's 2026 run may be effectively over; downside toward $4,100–4,200 possible.
- **COT gold +21,700 WoW** = speculative positioning is building even as price falls — potential divergence to watch. If price and positioning diverge significantly, either the specs are right (gold rallies soon) or we see forced liquidation.
- **WTI managed money net short at 15-year extremes** = structural contrarian signal. If geopolitical flare-up occurs (Iran/Hormuz), the resulting short-squeeze could be violent.
- **India-US spread compressed to 246bps** (from 252bps prior week) as US yields spiked. Lower spread = less carry attraction for FII debt flows; complicates RBI's capital inflow objective.
- **Bank credit 16.2% YoY — fastest in 2 years** = domestic credit cycle is strong despite geopolitical headwinds. Positive for NBFC/bank revenue; credit quality watch for Q1/Q2 FY27.
- **Brent + Chinese crude imports at 10-year low** = China's industrial slowdown is structurally visible in commodity data. Weak China demand = ceiling on Brent price despite Middle East tensions.

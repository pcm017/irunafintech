## TL;DR

- Revenue from operations ₹18,343 Cr (+15.1% YoY, +6.6% QoQ) — a re-acceleration from Q4 FY26's softer growth.
- EBITDA ₹1,527 Cr (+16.3% YoY), margin 8.3% (vs 8.2% Q1FY26, 7.2% Q4FY26) — margin recovering after a Q4 FY26 dip.
- PAT ₹936 Cr (+12.8% YoY, +29.1% QoQ), margin 5.1% (vs 5.2% Q1FY26, 4.2% Q4FY26).
- Like-for-like (LFL, >24 months) growth slowed sharply to 5.5% (from 10.8% in Q4 FY26) — the softest LFL print in the last five quarters.
- Retail business area reached 20.7 million sq ft (flat QoQ, +17.6% YoY), with revenue per sq ft at ₹8,571 (down from a Q3 FY26 peak of ₹9,290).

## Signals

DMart's headline revenue and profit growth both re-accelerated sequentially (revenue +6.6% QoQ, PAT +29.1% QoQ), but the like-for-like growth deceleration (10.8% to 5.5% QoQ) is the more important underlying signal — it indicates that a meaningful share of this quarter's growth came from new store additions (retail area +17.6% YoY) rather than existing-store demand strength. Combined with revenue-per-sq-ft declining from its Q3 FY26 peak, this is an early efficiency-dilution signal worth tracking against India's broader consumption narrative (the wiki's "consumption paradox" thread from Kotak MF research logged in late May) — DMart's value-retail model has historically been a relatively resilient read on discretionary/staples spending, and a genuine LFL slowdown would be a meaningful India-consumption data point if it persists into Q2 FY27.

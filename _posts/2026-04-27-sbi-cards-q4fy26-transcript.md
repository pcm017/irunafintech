## TL;DR

- **New account guidance confirmed at 9–10 Lakh/quarter** going forward — calibrated growth, not volume maximisation. Industry-wide underwriting tightened on unsecured lending.
- **Revolver to drift lower with "downward bias" in FY27** — management's offset strategy is installment/EMI lending, not reward point cuts. No specific floor given.
- **C/I guided 55–58% for FY27** — elevated vs. historical 51–52% because corporate spends (pass-back model) are now structural. CFO expects "no big jump" vs. FY26.
- **ROA target 4–4.5% medium-term** — implies credit cost needs to fall to ~6% and receivables need to re-accelerate. Currently 3.2% FY26.
- **ECL overlay of ₹220 Cr retained** — additional ₹100 Cr built in Q4 FY26 through P&L, explicit geopolitical hedge. If released, would add ~₹165 Cr PAT.
- **Cost of funds:** ~70–75% floating (T-bills/repo); borrowings reprice in 60–90 days. No FY27 COF guidance given; management watching RBI stance post-geopolitical uncertainty.

## Signals

1. **Industry new-account growth recovery will be gradual.** SBI Cards is guiding 9–10 Lakh/Q (vs. 11+ Lakh previously). This is deliberate quality-over-volume. Re-acceleration only begins once this vintage proves out — probably FY28 is when receivables growth can meaningfully re-accelerate.
2. **Revolver/NIM structural challenge is acknowledged but not yet fully solved.** The installment/EMI offset strategy is logical but executing against it has been slow — EMI mix has been flat to declining due to seasonality. FY27 will be the test year.
3. **Corporate spends are now a permanent feature** — they explain elevated C/I (cost of pass-back vs. BAU business), but also demonstrate SBI Card's ability to extend into B2B payment rails. 195% growth in FY26 is not sustainable but 15–20% structural contribution is likely.
4. **ECL management overlay is a hidden asset.** ₹220 Cr overlay was built at a time of falling Stage 2/3 stocks — a conservative, deliberate geopolitical hedge. Watch for release trigger in FY27 H1 once macro clarity improves (Iran/Hormuz resolution).
5. **COF trajectory depends on RBI.** 70–75% of borrowings floating on T-bills/repo — 60-90 day reprice window. Each 25 bps RBI cut flows through to COF with ~1-2 quarter lag, boosting NIM. If RBI cuts 50 bps in FY27, COF could fall ~35–40 bps net.
6. **Rental spend intentionally de-grown.** Company imposed fees on rental card payments early; now rental is negligible. All spend growth (13% retail YoY) comes from consumer + corporate — cleaner, more sustainable.

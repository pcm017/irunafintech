## Signals

**Lendingkart collapse = governance stress test for India MSME lending.** The Lunia-Fullerton NCLT dispute highlights a structural issue in India's digital MSME lending: when a company burns investor capital to acquire market share (Lendingkart's original KreditBee/SME lending model) and then faces a growth+credit quality cliff, founder-investor alignment breaks down at exit. NCLT petitions are slow; the June 18 hearing is the first data point. Watch: if Fullerton is found to have mismanaged, it raises questions about all Temasek-backed India fintech restructurings.

**Corporate deposits propping bank growth.** As retail deposits become more competitive (FD rate increases, fintech sweep accounts), corporate bulk deposits (ETBFSI framing) are filling the liability gap for banks. This is a structurally lower-quality deposit base (more rate-sensitive, more volatile) that explains why RBI's LCR-linked differential pricing reform (June 5 MPC) is targeted precisely at this segment. **LCR-linked pricing** = banks that take high-quality sticky deposits can offer lower rates; those reliant on volatile bulk deposits will face cost pressure.

**AI cost management = next fintech moat.** Uber burning 4 months of AI budget suggests enterprise AI deployment is still "spray and pray" — fintech companies with disciplined AI unit economics (Ramp, Affirm's AI-first 2× PR velocity without headcount reduction) will structurally outperform. Ramp's free AI for accountants strategy: loss-leader for distribution depth (accountants are the B2B fintech kingmakers in the US SMB market; whoever wins accountants wins the CFO stack).

**Wise AML scrutiny — India remittance risk.** Wise's India remittance infrastructure is heavily used for NRI-to-India flows. AML regulatory proceedings could slow product approvals or restrict volumes. Watch for RBI's FX remittance data (NRI remittances track) for any anomalous slowdown in the Wise corridor.

**Global fintech regulatory reckoning cycle.** Bilt (rent rewards → payment transition failures), Aspiration (ESG fraud), GreenSky (Goldman fintech), Wise (AML) — all in the same week. US and UK fintech regulatory environment is tightening post-ZIRP-era excesses. India's SEBI/RBI tightening (150 NBFC registrations cancelled, 28% gaming GST, WhatsApp lending scrutiny) is the same cycle playing out locally.

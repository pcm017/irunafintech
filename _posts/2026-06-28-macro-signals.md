## Signals

**1. WTI $68.86 — oil relief is structural, India savings compounding.** The Iran peace dividend is now fully expressed: at WTI below $69, India's crude import savings vs. the May $111 WTI peak exceed $42/bbl on a spot basis. Annualised against India's ~185M tonnes/year crude imports, this is ~$170–180B in notional savings. The August RBI rate cut thesis is the clearest it has been in 2026: CPI 3.93% (below 4% target) + WTI below $70 = the two-variable condition for rate cut consensus is met.

**2. BIS "policy discipline" framing = higher-for-longer global central bank consensus.** The BIS Annual Economic Report is the closest thing to a central bank consensus document. "Global economic pressure points call for policy discipline" signals that the BIS (and by extension the institutions it represents) believes rates should stay elevated to manage inflation risks. For RBI: domestic case for a cut is India-specific (low CPI, crude relief), but the global central bank community is not easing — RBI will be cutting into a DM-hawkish environment, which constrains pace.

**3. Asian market selloff (Nikkei -4.15%, KOSPI -5.81%) — semiconductor/tech supply chain.** The continued sell-off in Nikkei and KOSPI extends the AI governance / tech supply chain anxiety that drove Nasdaq's -4.6% weekly decline in the prior session. Samsung and SK Hynix (KOSPI) exposure to semiconductor supply chain concerns is deepening. India has decoupled (Sensex +0.14%) — domestic macro resilience overriding global tech sentiment.

**4. COT gold +228,000 — elevated positioning creates downside pressure risk.** Gold large specs are still net long ~228,000 contracts. While gold prices have stabilised ($4,040, +1.20% on June 27), the positioning is crowded. If the BIS "policy discipline" narrative translates into persistently higher US real yields, systematic liquidation of gold longs could push gold toward $3,900–4,000. Counter: COT is lagging; if June 28 data (to be released June 30) shows reduction, the risk diminishes.

**5. India biofuel push — energy security diversification beyond crude.** Market Bites framing: ethanol is working (20% blending target close to met) but India is now betting on a second, unproven biofuel to address residual crude oil dependency. This is structurally positive for long-term CAD compression but carries execution risk on the new biofuel scale-up.

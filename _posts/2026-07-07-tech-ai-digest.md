# Tech & AI Digest — 2026-07-07

## TL;DR

- PM Modi inaugurated India's third chip-packaging facility at Sanand under the India Semiconductor Mission — India went from zero to three operational semiconductor plants in six months; Ashwini Vaishnaw projects five plants operational by end-2026 (3 of 12 approved ISM projects now commissioning).
- Samsung Electronics is projected to post an 18-fold YoY profit jump (from ₩4.7 trillion) on surging AI-driven memory demand — a third consecutive quarter of record operating profit as memory shortage persists.
- Meta is reportedly making a $14Bn bet on smart glasses (Zuckerberg thesis: glasses displace phones within 5 years) and sizing up OpenAI's GPT-5.5 internally via a project codenamed "Watermelon."
- India and Japan are deepening an economic-security partnership spanning AI, semiconductors, and critical minerals, aimed at building resilient supply chains.
- India's startup funding continues to face investor caution amid regulatory concerns, though capital remains available for businesses with strong fundamentals — a more selective funding environment than the growth-at-all-costs phase.

## Key data / quotes

- Samsung Electronics: 18-fold YoY profit jump projected (from ₩4.7 trillion a year earlier); third consecutive record-profit quarter.
- India Semiconductor Mission: 3 of 12 approved projects now in commissioning (Sanand the third); Vaishnaw targets 5 plants operational by end-2026.
- India IT sector: "hardware super-cycle" — AI-driven component shortages predicted to persist to 2027.

## Signals

The Sanand chip-plant inauguration is a concrete milestone for India's semiconductor build-out (zero to three plants in six months) and is being cross-logged to Modi's action log given it's a direct PM-level policy action with clear India industrial-strategy significance. Samsung's projected 18-fold profit jump reinforces the AI-memory-shortage thesis already tracked via the AMD/Qualcomm packaging-innovation story (Jul 5 digest) — memory, not logic, remains the binding AI-hardware constraint, and India's IT sector is now explicitly framing this as a "hardware super-cycle" likely to persist through 2027, pushing enterprises toward software-based scaling instead. Meta's $14Bn smart-glasses bet and internal GPT-5.5 benchmarking ("Watermelon") signal continued big-tech capital intensity in consumer AI hardware and model competition, adjacent to but distinct from the India-specific supply chain and funding stories.

## TL;DR

- Reported PAT ₹832 cr in Q4FY26 (+65% YoY, +25% QoQ); FY26 PAT ₹2,641 cr (+25% YoY) — best quarterly print in the bank's history, driven by NIM recovery and normalizing credit cost
- NIM expanded 16–24 bps QoQ to 5.96% in Q4 (FY26 NIM 5.65% vs 5.94% FY25) on lower cost of funds, seasonal recoveries, and fewer days in February
- Asset quality improved sharply: GNPA down 27 bps QoQ to 2.03%, NNPA 0.74%; credit cost (% of avg. total assets) fell to 0.96% FY26 from 1.30% FY25 as MFI/unsecured stress normalized
- Deposits grew 23% YoY to ₹1.52 lakh cr (vs ~13% estimated private-bank sector growth); advances grew 21% YoY to ₹1.40 lakh cr, with secured book (retail + commercial) leading at +23% YoY
- **Universal bank transition is now a live corporate action**: RBI amended the NOFHC requirement (waiving it unless the Bank or promoter group sets up a new group entity) and the Bank filed its final universal banking license application in March 2026 — a structural re-rating catalyst if approved

## Signals

**NIM inflection is the standout quarter-over-quarter signal.** NIM expanded 16 bps QoQ to 5.96% in Q4, reversing the FY26 compression trend (FY26 NIM 5.65% vs 5.94% FY25) — driven by cost of funds falling 12 bps QoQ to 6.49% and seasonal recoveries. This mirrors the same repo-cut-driven NIM cycle seen at Kotak Mahindra Bank and other private banks in this wiki (see *kotak mahindra bank*) — the sector-wide bottom appears to be Q3FY26, with Q4 showing recovery.

**Credit cost normalization confirms the sector-wide MFI/unsecured stress cycle has turned.** Credit cost fell to 0.96% of average assets for FY26 from 1.30% in FY25, with management (transcript) guiding ~90 bps as a "through-cycle" run-rate for FY27 rather than extrapolating Q4's stronger 0.60% print — a deliberately conservative signal given AU's willingness to take underwriting risk in the SFB-to-universal-bank transition segment it operates in.

**Universal bank license filing is the largest structural catalyst in this print.** RBI's NOFHC waiver removes a key regulatory friction point, and AU filed its final application in March 2026. If approved, this would be the first small finance bank in India's system to transition to a universal bank — a re-rating event given universal banks typically carry lower cost of funds and broader product mandates (credit cards, corporate banking) than SFBs. This is now the single most important open item to track for AU SFB into FY27.

**Deposit growth of 23% YoY vs ~13% sector estimate signals AU is winning share in a competitive deposit market**, though this comes at a cost-of-funds premium typical of SFBs (6.49% vs sub-5% for large private/PSU banks) — the universal bank transition is precisely the lever that would close this gap.

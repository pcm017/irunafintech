# Tech & AI Digest — 2026-07-20

## TL;DR

- Model-release roundup: Anthropic launched Claude Sonnet 5 (most agentic model yet, near-Opus 4.8 performance at lower cost); OpenAI launched GPT-5.6 family (Luna/Terra/Sol); Google DeepMind delayed Gemini 3.5 Pro to Jul 17 for a full architectural rebuild and shipped Nano Banana 2 Lite + Gemini Omni Flash.
- Kimi K3 weights release confirmed for Jul 27 — the open-source-vs-hyperscaler-capex debate now has a concrete date to test against.
- ASML posted a strong Q2 2026 (€9.3Bn sales, €2.9Bn net income, Jul 15) and raised FY26 guidance to €43-45Bn from €36-40Bn — a bullish signal for the equipment layer of the chip supply chain.
- Tata Electronics' Dholera fab will start on far older nodes than planned (90nm/55nm vs. 28nm), pushing commercial operations to mid-2028 — two years later than first announced.
- US semiconductor export controls to China remain on case-by-case review rather than escalating, consistent with ongoing trade talks.

## Key data / quotes

- Global semiconductor capex projected at $200Bn in 2026 (+20% YoY); TSMC accounts for more than a quarter of that total via its $165Bn Arizona GigaFab buildout (six fabs, 2nm volume production targeted by 2030).
- Samsung committed $73Bn (memory + foundry + R&D) — more than 60% above TSMC's planned foundry-only spend; 2026 HBM4 production already sold out.
- Intel Foundry proceeding with $32Bn for new Fabs 52 and 62 in Chandler, Arizona (2nm, completion 2026/2027); Intel's overall 2026 capex outlook flat-to-declining.
- Qualcomm's $3.9Bn acquisition of Modular (announced at its June 26 investor day) aims to build a CUDA-competing software platform.
- ASML Q3 2026 guidance: €11.0-12.0Bn net sales, 55-57% gross margin.
- India's government authorized ₹1,27,500 Cr ($13.3Bn, "Semicon 2.0") on Jul 16 for chip design, production machinery, and supply-chain infrastructure. Tata Electronics' Jagiroad, Assam assembly/test facility is already operational.

## Signals

**Change detection:** The Tata Electronics Dholera fab slip (90nm/55nm instead of 28nm, mid-2028 instead of the original timeline) is the most consequential India-specific development this cycle — it's a concrete downgrade to India's flagship semiconductor manufacturing project, not just a funding announcement.

**Root cause:** The node downgrade likely reflects the difficulty of standing up leading-edge fab capability from scratch versus the technical/execution risk being underestimated at the project's launch — a pattern consistent with global first-fab ramp difficulties (Intel Foundry's own 18A yield struggles at 50-55% vs. TSMC's 70%+ show even established players struggle with leading-edge ramps).

**Sizing / impact:** A two-year slip and two-generation node downgrade meaningfully changes the near-term ambition of India's Semiconductor Mission — India's first fab output will compete in mature-node/legacy chip markets rather than leading-edge, which is actually more aligned with India's ATMP/OSAT-first strategy already noted elsewhere in this wiki, but undercuts the "India as advanced-node player" framing that accompanied the original Dholera announcement. Watch whether the newly-approved Semicon 2.0 tranche (₹1,27,500 Cr) is allocated with revised, more conservative node targets.

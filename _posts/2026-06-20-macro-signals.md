## Signals

**Change Detection:** India's market pullback (-0.78% on June 19) on the day of the Iran deal signing is a textbook "sell-the-news" after a 12-day rally from June 9 lows (Nifty 23,123) to June 19 (~24,042) — a +7.9% recovery. The deal is priced in; the next catalyst is the actual observable Brent trajectory toward $70-75 over July-August.

**Root Cause:** Gold at $4,172.90 (-1.72%) is the clearest signal — the geopolitical risk premium is now actively being removed. The Iran deal signing eliminates the primary safe-haven driver of 2026. COT gold longs still at ~173,800 contracts — if these longs liquidate toward the deal-confirmation baseline, gold could fall toward $3,800-4,000 range.

**Sizing / India Impact:**
- **INR 94.35 vs. 94.74 (June 16):** Modest post-signing strengthening; with Brent at $80 and falling, INR's structural floor is shifting toward 92-94. RBI will likely allow INR to appreciate gradually rather than rebuild reserves aggressively.
- **Jio Platforms IPO:** Potentially the largest Indian IPO ever (Reliance's market cap implies Jio Platforms could be valued at ₹8-10 lakh crore+). Impact on India's capital markets: LIC-scale primary issuance; FII interest in India equity will increase structurally as Jio becomes a benchmark constituent.
- **SEBI revamp:** Covers AIFs (venture capital and private equity fund governance), MFs (distribution and fee structures), buybacks (preventing manipulation), and bonds (deepening India's corporate bond market). A comprehensive upgrade consistent with India's goal of becoming a deep capital market.
- India CPI May 3.93% (vs. 3.90% in prior snapshot's estimate) — minor revision upward but still below 4% target; food at 4.78% is the primary upside risk. With Brent falling, the RBI August rate cut case strengthens.

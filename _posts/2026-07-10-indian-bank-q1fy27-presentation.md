## TL;DR

- Net profit ₹3,273 Cr (+10% YoY, +5% QoQ); operating profit ₹5,557 Cr (+17% YoY); NII ₹7,435 Cr (+17% YoY) — broad-based double-digit growth across the P&L.
- Business crossed ₹15.29 lakh Cr (+13.66% YoY): deposits ₹8.44 lakh Cr (+13.47% YoY), advances ₹6.85 lakh Cr (+13.89% YoY), CASA ratio 39.73% (domestic).
- Asset quality improved sharply: GNPA 1.86% (down 115bps YoY, down 12bps QoQ), NNPA 0.15% (down 3bps YoY), PCR 98.22% — among the strongest coverage ratios in the PSU bank space.
- NIM (domestic) 3.41% (+6bps YoY, +6bps QoQ); RoA 1.31% (+3bps YoY); RoE 19.48% (+78bps YoY, +50bps QoQ).
- RAM (Retail, Agri, MSME) advances at ₹4.17 lakh Cr, 66% of domestic advances — Retail +18.74% YoY was the fastest-growing RAM segment.

## Signals

Indian Bank's Q1 FY27 results are notably strong on asset quality — GNPA improving 115bps YoY to 1.86% with PCR near 98% is among the cleanest PSU bank asset-quality profiles this wiki has logged, and stands in contrast to the private-bank workforce-reduction/AI-margin-pressure narrative tracked at HDFC Bank and Kotak Mahindra Bank. This corroborates the private-vs-PSU divergence theme flagged in today's 2026-07-14 fintech-bfsi digest (Indian Bank hiring 20 AI experts even as private banks cut 7,700+ jobs) — Indian Bank appears to be investing in growth and technology capability from a position of balance-sheet strength rather than defensive cost-cutting. Retail advances growing fastest within RAM (+18.74% YoY) tracks the broader system-wide retail credit growth theme (17.7% YoY system bank credit growth logged in today's macro-signals snapshot).

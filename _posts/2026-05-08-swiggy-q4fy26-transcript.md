## TL;DR

- Management doubled down on Instamart differentiation strategy — Noice (clean-label) as the template across more categories.
- Strategy is not exclusionary: Instamart continues to serve core needs, but "amplifies upgrades" as a distinct positioning vs. Blinkit's breadth/depth approach.
- Quantification of differentiation revenues not shared — still "early"; more detail in subsequent quarters.
- Dineout GIRF event drove advertising monetisation; in-restaurant dining is "secular growth" category.
- Management tone: disciplined, long-term, quality-focused — avoided short-term decisions even at cost of near-term volume.

---

## Key data / quotes

### On Instamart differentiation (CEO Sriharsha Majety)
- *"Even in the shareholders' letter, we talked about one such example, which is Noice that is our clean label brand. Noice operates in categories such as bread, eggs, etc."*
- *"In the case of eggs, you will find high protein eggs... fresh baked bread with much lesser preservatives... expand the category very differentially through some interventions in assortment and price."*
- On breadth of differentiation: *"In the next couple of months, you will see a host of such examples pop up across the Instamart app. The broad theme is Indians are largely always looking for upgrades."*
- *"[The strategy] is not a strategy that is exclusionary... we will continue to serve all core needs of today's users, but actually amplify the upgrades part."*

### On quantification
- Analyst (Sachin Salgaonkar, BofA): Asked for proportion of differentiated NOV — directionally 10-20% or higher?
- CFO Rahul Bothra: *"It would be extremely hard for us... I think ultimately, the consumer proposition is very strong... over time, what really helps propositions like this is consumer retention and frequency increase."*
- Conclusion: Management is not yet ready to provide proportion data. Watch for Q1/Q2 FY27 data points.

### On Dineout
- Dineout crossed 52k average monthly active restaurant partners (9% QoQ)
- GOV +43% YoY to ₹1,245 Cr; EBITDA margin expanded to 0.8%
- GIRF (Great Indian Restaurant Festival) accentuated restaurant partner depth and advertising monetisation
- *"In-restaurant dining is a secular growth opportunity"* — category still significantly underpenetrated even in top-tier cities

### On Snacc shutdown
- Quick food (Snacc) shut down proactively — *"not convinced on steady state size"*
- Toing (affordable restaurant marketplace) still being tested
- Signals management's willingness to kill experiments that don't meet bar even under competitive pressure

## Signals

1. **Instamart differentiation = Noice as template.** The model: identify an underpenetrated premium segment in a mass category (eggs, bread), work with brands or create own-label, launch at a quality-price point that expands category participation. If successful across 10+ categories, Instamart builds a premium layer that is meaningfully differentiated from Blinkit and Zepto.

2. **No quantified guidance on differentiation NOV** — management is being appropriately cautious. The absence of data suggests they are still iterating; would be a red flag only if Q2/Q3 FY27 still lacks metrics.

3. **Dineout at 0.8% EBITDA = the proof of concept** for the out-of-home model. Food delivery was unprofitable for years before it worked. Dineout appears to be reaching the same inflection at a much earlier stage.

4. **Snacc shutdown = prioritisation discipline.** Indian consumer tech companies rarely shut down experiments publicly — Swiggy's willingness to do so (and discuss it explicitly on the earnings call) signals management confidence in the core strategy and maturity in portfolio management.

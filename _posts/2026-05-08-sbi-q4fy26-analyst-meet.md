## Signals

**SBI's Q4FY26 is deceptively soft — the real number is record FY26 PAT.** The MTM treasury loss of ₹4,520 cr in Q4 made Q4 PAT look worse than Q3 — but this is a bond yield mark-to-market swing, not a credit event. FY26 PAT of ₹80,032 cr (+12.88%) on a ₹76 lakh cr balance sheet at >1% ROA is extraordinary at scale. No global bank at this asset size consistently prints ROA >1%.

**NIM at 3.03% masks a structural tension: T-bill vs MCLR.** India's corporate bond market dried up partly due to West Asia uncertainty, pushing AAA/AA corporates back to banks at T-bill-linked rates. This temporarily widened credit but compressed yields. SBI's strategy — reprice T-bill loans to MCLR as renewals come due in FY27 — is the same NIM lever BoB is using. If the repo rate is stable (SBI house view: no cut in FY27), this repricing is low-risk and should restore domestic NIM above 3% sustainably.

**SBI's ECL buffer story is India's most watched banking event.** The ₹29,000–30,000 cr provisioning buffer (Chairman hinted, didn't confirm) vs a 4-year transition period from April 2027 means SBI's CET-1 is the most resilient ECL transition case in Indian banking. Management confidence on smooth transition — "it will not impact our ability to fund credit growth" — is material for the broader banking sector's re-rating case.

**India macro transmission:** SBI's 22%+ market share means its credit growth guidance (13–15% FY27) is a direct anchor for India's overall banking system credit. The shift to RAM (Retail, Agriculture, MSME) as the primary growth driver for FY27 is a bullish signal for domestic consumption credit and financial inclusion. The ECLGS 5 (₹2.5 lakh cr facility for MSMEs) using SBI's distribution is a direct demand support for the MSME credit cycle.

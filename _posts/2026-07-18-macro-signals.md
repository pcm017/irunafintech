## Signals

**Change detection:** The Jul 17 session is the first clean US/India equity decoupling of this cycle where India rallied *while* the US and Asia sold off on the same day, rather than India merely holding up better during a US pullback. The driver split cleanly: US/Asia weakness traces to the AI-hardware/semiconductor axis (Kimi K3 + the ongoing semis value-reallocation story) plus Iran, while India's rally is earnings-driven (bank/IT/auto), a domestically-sourced catalyst insulated from the global tech-sentiment shock.

**Root cause:** Moonshot AI's Kimi K3 model release is flagged by two independent sources (macro-signals and tech-ai-digest) as a contributing factor to the US AI-trade sell-off — a Chinese frontier-model release directly denting US AI-infrastructure valuations, a new and distinct channel from the chip-export-control or capex-guidance stories tracked so far.

**Sizing/impact:** Gold's rebound to $4,016.95 (+1.03%) after briefly breaking below $4,000 on Jul 16 shows the safe-haven bid remains reactive to day-to-day escalation cadence rather than settling into a new range. The fresh India G-Sec 10Y conflict (7.12% vs. 6.72%, ~40bps gap) is wide enough to materially change the India-US spread read (+258bps vs. +218bps) and should not be used for entity-page updates until reconciled against a primary source.

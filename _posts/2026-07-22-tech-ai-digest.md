# Tech & AI Digest — 2026-07-22

## TL;DR

- **Elon Musk announced "Terrafab," a new $20-25Bn wafer fab in Austin, TX** targeting 1 million wafer starts/month at the 2nm node to supply Tesla, SpaceX, and xAI — the first vertically-integrated fab commitment from a company outside the traditional foundry/IDM set.
- **TSMC guided $52-56Bn 2026 capex** after an eighth consecutive quarter of YoY growth, and separately committed a further $100Bn to Arizona (total announced US investment $265Bn); Q2 2026 revenue $40.20Bn (+33.7% YoY), N2 (2nm) running 70%+ yields vs. Intel's 18A stuck at 50-55%.
- **Samsung plans over ₩110 trillion (~$74Bn) in 2026 spend** to "secure leadership in the AI semiconductor era"; 2nm GAA now in mass production and its Taylor, TX fab secured its first HBM4 customer.
- **India: DLI-scheme chip-design startups raised $92M in the first five months of 2026** (14 companies, ~₹6.5Bn via the scheme) — a concrete funding figure for the design-startup layer, distinct from the ₹1.275 trillion Semicon 2.0 fab/ATMP envelope; ₹8,000 Cr FY27 ISM budget outlay reconfirmed.
- Gmail-sourced AI-model and big-tech stories (Claude math-proof coverage, Kimi K3 commentary, Benedict Evans newsletter) were snippet-only this cycle — thin on hard content, consistent with the recurring Gmail token-ceiling constraint.

## Key data / quotes

- TSMC: Q2 2026 revenue NT$1,270.38bn (US$40.20bn, +33.7% YoY, +12.0% QoQ); diluted EPS NT$27.25; 2026 capex forecast raised to $60bn (guidance range cited elsewhere as $52-56bn — treat as a range still firming, not a contradiction).
- Samsung: 2nm GAA in mass production; first Taylor, TX customer secured for HBM4 demand.
- Musk/Terrafab: $20-25bn, Austin TX, target 1M wafer starts/month at 2nm, backers Tesla/SpaceX/xAI.
- Qualcomm: $3.9bn Modular acquisition (CUDA-competing software platform); guided >$15bn annual AI data-center component sales by FY2029.
- India: 24 semiconductor design projects worth ₹9bn (~$94M) approved under Semicon 2.0's VC-matching scheme; separately, DLI-scheme design startups raised $92M / ~₹6.5bn across 14 companies in the first 5 months of 2026.
- Industry-wide: Semiconductor Intelligence estimates total 2025 industry capex at $166bn, rising to ~$200bn in 2026 (+20% YoY).

## Signals

Musk's Terrafab announcement is the most consequential new data point this cycle — a first-of-its-kind commitment from a non-chip-native company to build its own leading-edge (2nm) fab, extending the same hyperscaler/AI-compute-silicon-independence thread already tracked for Meta and Google, but at a scale ($20-25Bn, 1M wafer starts/month target) that would make Terrafab a meaningful new entrant within the ~$200Bn 2026 global semiconductor capex pool — with correspondingly high execution risk for a first-time fab builder. TSMC and Samsung's continued capex escalation ($60-64Bn and ~$74Bn respectively) reinforces the standing view that fab capacity, not chip design, remains the binding constraint on AI-hardware supply. On India, the $92M DLI-scheme design-startup funding figure is small in absolute terms but gives the wiki its first concrete number for the domestic chip-design layer specifically, distinct from the far larger capital being deployed at the Tata/Micron/Kaynes fab and ATMP scale — worth tracking as a leading indicator of whether India's "design, not just assembly" ambition is gaining real traction.

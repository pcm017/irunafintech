# Groww Q4 FY26 Shareholders' Letter

**Date:** 20 April 2026 | **Company:** Billionbrains Garage Ventures Limited (NSE: GROWW, BSE: 544603)  
**Raw file:** [raw/results/f68d64b3-d742-4274-9343-e21c055b8329.pdf](../../raw/results/f68d64b3-d742-4274-9343-e21c055b8329.pdf)

## TL;DR

- **Explosive growth, rapidly expanding margins:** Q4 FY26 Total Income ₹15,355 Mn (+81% YoY, +22% QoQ); EBITDA ₹9,387 Mn (+142% YoY, +30% QoQ); PAT ₹6,864 Mn (+122% YoY), PAT margin 44.7%.
- **Platform scale:** 21.6 Mn total transacting users (+25% YoY), ₹3.0 Tn total customer assets (+36% YoY), 16.7 Mn active users.
- **Market share gains across every product:** MF SIP market share 15.7% (vs. 12.1% a year ago); Equity Derivatives 10.6% (vs. 6.8%); MTF book ₹28,143 Mn (+22% QoQ, gaining share even as industry contracted -7% QoQ).
- **Operating leverage embedded:** Revenue and EBITDA growing far faster than costs (largely fixed). Platform EBITDA margin ~66% even while Fisdom (-₹102 Mn) and Growwmf (-₹214 Mn) are sub-scale losses dragging consolidated EBITDA lower.
- **Credit contributes 4.1% of consolidated PAT** in Q4; MTF book deployed ₹5,069 Mn of PAT during Q4, LAS/PL deployed ₹1,057 Mn — the company is deploying earnings into lending on-balance-sheet within broking and consumer credit.

## Signals

- **Groww is the most profitable fintech company in India by PAT margin.** 44.7% PAT margin in Q4 is extraordinary for a platform of this scale. The operating leverage story is intact: cost to serve has fallen from 18.5% to 10.7% of revenue in one year.
- **Market share gains are broad-based and accelerating.** MF SIP +3.6 pp, Derivatives +3.8 pp in a single year. The combination of tech-led user experience and low-cost acquisition is compounding network effects — more users → more liquidity → better fills → better retention.
- **Geopolitical volatility (Hormuz) is a short-term tailwind.** High volatility spikes derivatives activity and platform revenue. The medium-term risk is sustained FII selling dampening investor sentiment and new user acquisition.
- **Growwmf and Fisdom are the long-duration bets.** The core platform subsidises these losses. Growwmf needs 5-6x AUM to break even — this is a multi-year journey. Fisdom profitable by FY28.
- **On-balance-sheet lending is the next chapter.** By deploying PAT into MTF + LAS/PL, Groww is transitioning from a pure-play marketplace to a balance-sheet participant in financial products. This increases ROE but also adds credit risk — still early stages.
- **India equity penetration still in single digits** — management's 3-4x expansion claim over the next decade is credible given demographic tailwinds, Jan Dhan infrastructure, and continued formalization. Groww's platform position is among the strongest in capturing this wave.

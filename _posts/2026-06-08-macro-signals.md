## Signals

**Hormuz day 100 — escalation premium persists.** WTI +3.41% on Strait of Hormuz near-closure signals oil markets remain in "tail risk" pricing mode. OPEC+ adding supply (+188K bpd, July) is irrelevant if transit lanes are closed. India's oil import bill remains elevated; OMC losses continue.

**Gold at new 2026 low.** $4,328.87 has broken below the prior $4,342 floor. Rate-shock + reduced haven demand as US inflation trajectory looks higher-for-longer. COT gold positioning still elevated (+111K to +176K managed money net long across the past week) — if specs are forced to liquidate, gold has further downside toward $4,000–4,200. If geopolitical spike triggers short-covering, violent snap-back possible.

**Nikkei ▼4.07% — regional contagion deepening.** The June 8 Nikkei decline is steeper than the June 5 ▼1.31% figure previously captured — Asian markets are repricing for higher-for-longer US rates + sustained energy shock simultaneously.

**Bank credit slowdown ahead.** ICRA's FY27 estimate (11.0–11.7% YoY) vs. FY26 actual (16.2%) is a significant deceleration call. Driven by: (1) higher funding costs, (2) deposit repricing pressures, (3) rate transmission from RBI holds + Goldman's 2 hike call, (4) demand slowdown as consumers face fuel-driven input cost squeeze. NBFC AUM growth guidance (Bajaj Finance: 20–24%) will face headwind if this ICRA trajectory proves accurate.

**ATMs running out of cash — liquidity distribution failure.** India's ATM cash shortage signal is unprecedented in recent years. Possible drivers: fuel hike cash demand surge, rural demand for cash, or RBI currency management lag. Watch for RBI response (cash injection into ATM networks, banknote issuance).

**LPG ₹29/cylinder hike — fuel inflation compounding.** This is an additional household cost burden on top of prior petrol/diesel hikes. Rural households are disproportionately impacted (LPG is primary cooking fuel). Watch for: (1) demand substitution (biomass reversal); (2) political pressure on subsidy restoration; (3) rural consumption proxies (FMCG small-pack growth, 2W sales).

**Nuclear energy private sector sops in view.** India government signalling private sector nuclear energy participation — first significant shift from the government-only nuclear model. If implemented: long-term positive for Tata Power, Adani Green, L&T; signals India's energy transition has a nuclear dimension that wasn't explicit before.

**Indus Waters Treaty ruling deepens India-Pakistan rift.** Legal development tightening pressure on Pakistan's water rights. Post-Operation Sindoor, the Indus Waters Treaty dispute adds another diplomatic friction layer. India's strategic posture toward Pakistan: multiple-front pressure (military readiness, water treaty, trade).

## TL;DR

- PB Fintech has fully delivered on its post-IPO operating plan: insurance premium 4.9x, revenue 4.8x, PAT swung from -₹833 Cr (FY22) to +₹670 Cr (FY26) — with ₹1,000 Cr PAT targeted for FY27.
- Policybazaar is now India's 3rd largest retail health player (18-20% market share) and 4th largest life distribution player; renewal revenue at ₹935 Cr (~80% margins) is the primary compounding engine.
- Paisabazaar resumed core disbursal growth (₹9,986 Cr in Q4 FY26), now pivoting to full lifecycle credit — repeat lending, wealth/savings, and credit progression for NTC customers.
- AI is deeply operationalized across the business: 21 lac calls processed daily, ₹9,618 Cr sum assured risk averted via fraud detection in FY26, +20% conversion uplift from AI sales stack.
- FY30 vision: ₹1 Lac Cr total insurance premium — placing PB Fintech among India's largest insurance distribution franchises.

## Signals

- **Renewal compounding is the core moat.** ₹935 Cr renewal ARR at ~80% margins is near-pass-through to EBITDA — and hasn't decelerated (43% CAGR since FY19). This is structurally different from transactional fintechs.
- **Health insurance is the growth driver.** 40% of all new lives added to the industry in FY25 came through PB; loss ratio at 51% (down 3pp YoY) means PB is now a profit partner for insurers, not just a distribution channel.
- **Paisabazaar recovery is real but credit cards remain subdued.** Core disbursals at ₹9,986 Cr in Q4 — highest since Q4 FY24 — but cards issued have fallen from 152k/qtr peaks to ~18k in Q4 FY26. Credit quality tightening is intentional (8% pre-filter rejection).
- **PB Partners is the Bharat play.** 80% of business from T2/T3; POSP market consolidation (top-2 competitors merged) widens PB's lead.
- **AI-led underwriting edge:** Fraud controls averted ₹9,618 Cr in sum assured risk, claims within first 12 months fell from 3.6% to 3.2% of policies — directly improving loss ratios for insurer partners and justifying better commission rates.
- **UAE is a quietly compounding international bet** — 12x growth since FY22, shifting towards Health + Life (non-motor now ~50% of portfolio).

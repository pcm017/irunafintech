## Signals

**Change detection:** The standout new data this cycle is the fuller central-bank gold/forex reserves picture — India's gold share of forex reserves has risen from 13.9% to 16.7% over six months, a meaningful reallocation even as headline forex reserves ($675.16Bn) stay roughly flat WoW.

**Root cause:** The gold-share increase reflects both RBI's continued accumulation and gold's price appreciation (gold up sharply YoY) rather than a single large purchase — consistent with the broader global central-bank gold-buying trend (China's 20th straight month).

**Sizing / impact:** A rising gold share in India's reserves is a structural de-dollarisation/diversification signal that reduces reliance on US Treasuries within the reserve mix, though it also means reserve valuation becomes more sensitive to gold-price swings. Combined with confirmed 18.6% YoY bank credit growth and a positive July FII turn, India's domestic macro settings remain constructive heading into the August 4-6 MPC, even as the India G-Sec 10Y conflict (6.74-6.79%) continues to make the India-US spread read imprecise.

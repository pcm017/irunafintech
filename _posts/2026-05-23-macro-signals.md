## Signals

- **INR towards 100 — a real scenario:** The $28.4B April trade deficit, OMC losses of ₹1,000 Cr/day, and FII outflows point to sustained rupee pressure. The RBI USD 5B swap buys time but doesn't address the structural oil import problem. If Brent stays above $100, rupee at 100/USD is a plausible 6–12 month scenario.
- **Gold at 17.3% of reserves — illusory comfort:** Only 7% of the $72B appreciation came from purchases; 93% is price-driven. India's "usable" reserves for intervention are overstated by headline figures. The ~$40B drawdown since February is the real intervention signal.
- **FII vs. DII — domestic floor holding:** Daily FII selling (-₹4,440 Cr) is being absorbed by DIIs (+₹6,003 Cr), primarily through SIP flows. This has been the pattern for months. As long as domestic SIP inflows remain robust (~₹25,000+ Cr/month), the Nifty floor holds despite global headwinds.
- **US yields easing slightly:** 3rd consecutive session of US 10Y easing may signal a near-term bond rout pause. But structural drivers (US deficit, term premium repricing) haven't changed.
- **India spread widening as US yields ease:** India–US 10Y spread back to ~256bps as US yields ease — gives RBI some room on rates, and slightly improves FII debt flow incentive.
- **Tamil Nadu AI Act and RBI quiet signals:** State-level AI regulation emerging. RBI signaling through back-channel communications — watch MPC minutes and deputy governor speeches for signals on rate cycle and liquidity stance.

# Paytm Q3 FY26 Earnings Call Transcript

**Call date:** January 30, 2026, 8:00 AM IST  
**Filed with BSE/NSE:** February 4, 2026  
**Management:** Vijay Shekhar Sharma (CEO), Madhur Deora (CFO/President)

## TL;DR

- PIDF sunset is the elephant in the room — management expects 30-40% offset in Q4 via subscription repricing + targeted AI-led sales; remaining ~60% will flow to EBITDA as a short-term hit
- Long-term revenue growth and margin outlook "intact" — confident in 25%+ like-for-like revenue growth and 15-20% EBITDA margins over 2-3 years
- Online merchant acquisition restarted under VSS as CEO of PPSL; a "new line of business" effectively paused since 2021
- AI is central to operating model — AI-led sales planning, merchant underwriting, fraud detection, customer service; Paytm claims #1 on real-world enterprise AI benchmark (beats ByteDance, Alibaba, Snowflake, AT&T)
- Paytm Money targeting top-5 wealth player in India within 3 years; MTF book scaling, equity broking monetisation improving

## Key data / quotes

**On PIDF and contribution margin:**
> "Last quarter, this number was some Rs 80 crores. We should be able to offset 30-40% of it, but the remaining impact, call it 60%, will be down to EBITDA." — Madhur Deora

> "Our business model is not based on this [PIDF]. That's the key word. We are not sitting here to take grants as our profit and revenue." — Vijay Shekhar Sharma

**On long-term outlook (when asked about >30% growth, 15-20% margins guidance):**
> "I think the outlook is more or less intact. I think we are as excited about the opportunity in India today as we were two or three years ago." — Madhur Deora

**On payment processing margin (above 4 bps):**
> "We're not guiding to higher numbers right now. We want to see just market discipline continue and the trend of things like RuPay on UPI, et cetera, continue and positive mix. But we feel pretty confident that it will stay above four basis points for the next few quarters." — Madhur Deora

**On soundbox/device economics:**
> "Nearly all [offline GMV comes from device merchants]. QR only merchants are negligible." — Vijay Shekhar Sharma

**On online merchant expansion:**
> "I have become the CEO of the PPSL company. So online business will start generating more upside in payments and financial services... we are recruiting in that. You will see more recruitment of sales executives for our enterprise sales." — Vijay Shekhar Sharma

**On consumer monetisation targets:**
> "250 million customers is a good number for us to aim for." — Vijay Shekhar Sharma

**On merchant loan penetration ceiling:**
> "This number could get as high as 20% [penetration of device base]. Our whitelist base is 40 to 50%, typically." — Madhur Deora (current: ~5%)

**On Paytm Checkin (AI travel app):**
> "We have a funnel that converts 30 plus percent... 1% [conversion] is considered very good [for traditional OTAs]. So check-in is... I'm loving the product differentiation." — Vijay Shekhar Sharma

**On AI enterprise benchmark:**
> "Your favorite company tops this [real-world enterprise AI benchmark] on two days back. We are much ahead of ByteDance, Alibaba, and even AT&T, Snowflake, everybody." — Vijay Shekhar Sharma

**On conservative revenue recognition:**
> "[For inactive merchants], we have tightened [the recognition window]. As a result of that policy, we [now book] slightly lower revenue but also lower Provision for Doubtful Debt." — Madhur Deora

## Signals

- **PIDF drag is quantified**: ~₹80 Cr/quarter headwind in Q4. 30-40% offset executable fast; full offset is a multi-quarter journey. Watch Q4 FY26 EBITDA for execution evidence.
- **Online merchant pivot is real**: PPSL restructuring + VSS stepping in as CEO signals this is a strategic priority. First time since 2021 Paytm can properly acquire online merchants. Platform fees + EMI economics make online better-margin than pure offline.
- **Merchant loans have 3-4x runway**: Current penetration ~5%, whitelist 40-50%, ceiling guided 20%. Three drivers: device base growth (27 lakh YoY), penetration (+~1%/yr), ticket size (+15% CAGR). Repeat borrower behavior is the key moat.
- **Consumer credit still a drag** — personal loans and credit cards underperformed vs expectations. Postpaid scaling well. Recovery expected as macro credit cycle turns.
- **Subscription revenue per device guided slightly down** — low-to-mid single digit decline per year. Offset through efficiency gains, not by passing cost to merchants. The stack (subscription + MDR + credit) is the ARPU unit, not subscription alone.
- **Paytm Money is an option value** — currently low-to-mid single digit % of revenue. Top-5 target in 3 years. MTF (margin trade funding) actively scaling. More disclosures expected when it reaches "high single digits" of revenue.
- **International expansion**: Foundational work done in select markets; concrete announcements expected in 3-6 months. Merchant-stack only (no consumer, no remittances).
- **Real money gaming (RMG) headwind cleared** — RMG Act (Aug 2025) had a ~1% revenue impact; now fully lapped.

## Signals

- **India G-Sec 10Y easing to 6.95%** — the first time below 7.00% in weeks; partial relief as oil prices ease with Iran deal hopes; if Hormuz fully opens, India G-Sec could compress further → eases RBI's rate path considerations
- **India bank credit at 15.8% YoY** — strong, but driven by services/personal loans; industry credit more moderate; signals consumption credit still healthy despite macro stress
- **Brent −17% in May** is structurally significant for India: trade deficit, OMC losses, fuel inflation all ease if this level holds or improves further
- **China 18th consecutive monthly gold purchase** — sovereign gold accumulation as a de-dollarisation strategy; not stopping despite rally
- **FII −₹21,106 Cr vs DII +₹16,764 Cr** — gap is significant; DII is NOT fully offsetting FII outflows; net selling pressure on Indian markets from institutional side
- **Goldman's RBI hike call** is a major macro signal — if RBI hikes again while growth slows, it creates a stagflation-like scenario for India; watch for RBI's next MPC decision carefully
- **Nifty PCR 0.78** — options market skewing bearish; more call writers than put writers; institutions are not yet buying the dip in size

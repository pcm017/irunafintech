## Signals

- **DXY 101.6 + 3 Fed hikes priced — major regime shift:** The market's pivot from pricing 3 Fed cuts to pricing 3 Fed hikes is the most significant macro regime shift since the Iran deal was signed. Higher-for-longer US rates mean: (a) gold continues its selloff despite geopolitical noise, (b) EM carry trades unwind (India included), (c) RBI's space to cut rates in August narrows even though India CPI at 3.93% is below target. The India-US spread at ~230bps provides some cushion but compression continues
- **WTI below $71 — full supply normalisation underway:** WTI sub-$71 is a new conflict-era low and puts Indian crude import cost effectively back to pre-conflict levels. At $71, India's annualised import savings vs. the May $111 WTI peak exceed $150B. The RBI's rate cut window in August is now a function of (1) whether India CPI stays below 4% in June (data July) and (2) whether Fed hike repricing compresses India-US spread further
- **Gold $4,040 — 200-DMA breakdown extended:** Gold at $4,040 is a significant psychological level. The combination of DXY 101.6 (USD strength) + Fed hike expectations + US-Iran supply return (lower energy inflation thesis) = structural case for gold re-testing $3,800-3,900 range if US rate hike narrative hardens. India's RBI gold holdings (880.52t) face mark-to-market decline but this does not affect India's balance-of-payments position
- **PBoC Lujiazui speech:** Pan Gongsheng addressing China's financial modernisation at this forum is a policy signal moment — typically used to frame China's regulatory approach to fintech, digital payments, and capital market reform for the year ahead. For India: China's financial system modernisation (CIPS, digital yuan, cross-border CBDC pilots) creates competitive pressure on India's UPI/e-RUPI export ambitions in EM economies

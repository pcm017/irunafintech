## TL;DR

- **Trump declared the US-Iran ceasefire "over"; the US struck Iran for a second, stronger day (Jul 8)** — the direct trigger behind a steep Jul 8 India selloff (Sensex -1,677 pts, Nifty -2.12%) and a Brent spike to $78-79/bbl.
- By the Jul 9 session, Brent had partially moderated to $72.94 and India staged a partial rebound (Nifty +0.59%, Sensex +0.62%, Bank Nifty +0.89%).
- US 10Y hit 4.59% (highest since mid-May); India G-Sec 10Y paused at 6.69-6.70% after a 4-month low.
- India CPI confirmed 3.93% (May 2026, highest since Jan 2025); WPI 8.3% (April, provisional).
- SEBI shifted FPI registration fees from USD to INR denomination; RBI reiterated its crypto-prohibition stance, with the tax department flagging evasion risk.
- India forex reserves at $666.93Bn (-$5.66Bn WoW).

## Signals

**Change detection:** The dominant change is geopolitical, not economic — Trump's declaration that the Iran ceasefire is "over" ends a ~3-week calm since the June 19 Switzerland signing, reversing the Brent/INR/RBI-easing trajectory that had been building since mid-June.

**Root cause traversal:** The proximate trigger is a second, "significantly stronger" round of US strikes on Iran (per ZeroHedge), following Trump's public declaration. Markets read this as a genuine resumption of hostilities rather than posturing — Brent's intraday spike to $78-79/bbl on Jul 8 confirms this.

**Sizing / impact:** For India, the Jul 8 selloff (Sensex -1,677 pts) is one of the sharper single-session moves of 2026, and the rupee's intraday weakening to ~₹95.5/USD shows the INR-crude-price transmission channel remains fully live. If Brent holds above $78-80 into the August 3-5 RBI MPC, it directly threatens the rate-cut thesis built on CPI-below-target plus cheap crude.

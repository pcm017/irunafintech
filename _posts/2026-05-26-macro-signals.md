## Signals

1. **Iran deal is the biggest binary**: Brent -3% to -5% on a single day of deal progress signals how sensitive crude is to Iran resolution. If the deal closes and Strait of Hormuz reopens, Brent could fall to $75–85 range — resolving India's primary macro stress in one move.

2. **Nifty +1.13% on May 26**: India markets rallying on the Iran deal signal — energy import relief optimism transmitting directly to equity risk appetite. First session data since the Memorial Day weekend.

3. **WPI-CPI gap is dangerous**: WPI at 8.3% vs. CPI at 3.48% — the wholesale shock is not yet in retail prices. If Hormuz does NOT resolve, the CPI will follow WPI higher with a 1–2 month lag. This constrains RBI's ability to cut rates.

4. **RBI spent 2% forex to defend rupee**: $688.89B with $7.7B burned in a single week. If Hormuz persists and INR pressure continues, this rate of drawdown (~$30B/month) puts reserves under stress within 2–3 months.

5. **4th fuel hike — CPI pressure accumulating**: Cumulative fuel hikes since Iran war began are now feeding into India's inflation expectations even if the headline CPI hasn't moved dramatically yet.

6. **US equity 8th weekly gain**: S&P resilience at 7,473 with a new Dow record suggests US financial conditions remain accommodative despite elevated bond yields — a positive backdrop for global risk appetite.

7. **Copper +3.2% on Iran deal hopes**: Copper is serving double duty as both AI-infrastructure demand signal and Hormuz-supply-chain indicator. A de-escalation confirms both.

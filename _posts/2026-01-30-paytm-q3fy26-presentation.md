## TL;DR

- Revenue ₹2,194 Cr (+20% YoY); EBITDA ₹156 Cr (7% margin) — full YoY turnaround from ₹(223) Cr in Dec-24
- PAT ₹225 Cr vs ₹(208) Cr a year ago; 9M FY26 PAT positive ₹369 Cr vs ₹(118) Cr in 9M FY25
- PIDF incentive expired Dec 2025 (₹216 Cr over 9M FY26); management guiding 30-40% offset in Q4 via higher subscription revenue and targeted sales

- Cash and investable balance ₹12,882 Cr (adj.), essentially flat YoY

## Signals

- **Payments profitability has turned structurally** — payment processing margin now comfortably above 4 bps, driven by RuPay, EMI, and instrument mix. This is no longer a thin-margin pass-through.
- **PIDF headwind is manageable but real** — Q4 FY26 will take ~60% of the ₹~80 Cr/quarter PIDF impact to EBITDA before offsets. Full-year FY27 will be the first PIDF-free year, requiring clear offset execution.
- **Financial Services is the growth engine** — 34% YoY; merchant loans compounding steadily for 7-8 quarters; consumer credit (personal loans, credit cards) still recovering. Postpaid and MTF adding incremental growth.
- **Consumer UPI gaining share for 3 consecutive quarters** — regulatory clearance (Oct-25) enabling aggressive acquisition.
- **Marketing Services bottomed** — RMG headwind (~1% of revenue) now gone; travel stabilising.
- **AI deeply embedded** — not vanity; Paytm claims #1 rank on enterprise AI benchmark (real-world use-cases), beating ByteDance, Alibaba, Snowflake.

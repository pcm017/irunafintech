## Signals

**FCNR(B) rate cap removal — RBI's capital account lever working.** Removing rate caps on NRE/FCNR(B) deposits is the third leg of the June 5 MPC capital account package (alongside FII G-Sec exemption and LCR pricing reform). HDFC Bank as the primary beneficiary reflects its large NRI deposit base and global branch network. If $50B in incremental NRI deposits materialise, it provides meaningful INR support and liquidity in the banking system — partially offsetting the ₹2.08L Cr FII equity exit. This is RBI using the capital account toolkit rather than rate hikes to defend the currency.

**Payment system perpetual authorisation — compliance bar raised.** Moving from time-bound to perpetual authorisation sounds like regulatory simplification, but the actual effect is to raise the bar for ongoing governance: perpetual licences require continuous compliance demonstration rather than periodic renewal reviews. Small payment aggregators face the highest compliance cost burden. Large players (Razorpay, Cashfree, PayU, Juspay) benefit from the higher barrier to entry.

**SMFG India Credit 20% AUM / 60% secured target — NBFC cycle turn confirmed.** SMFG India Credit's explicit 20% AUM growth guidance and shift toward secured lending (60% target) is a sector-level signal that NBFCs are repositioning from the unsecured-growth era toward a secured-quality posture. The rural lending push is the incremental growth vector. For the India credit cycle broadly, this guidance — from a Temasek-backed NBFC with 20-year India vintage — confirms credit expansion is resuming but under tighter risk controls than the 2021-2023 cycle.

**FinTech 2.0 (Sahil Kini) — capability shift underway.** The RBIH CEO's framing — that FinTech 2.0 requires moving beyond EMI-led products toward AI-embedded operations — is the most authoritative India regulatory signal yet that the product bar is being raised. Companies still selling pure EMI products without an AI-decisioning layer are structurally disadvantaged against newer entrants. This is relevant for Paytm (declared AI-only for FY27), Bajaj Finance (27 agents in production), and emerging AI-native credit fintechs.

**FII ₹-2.08L Cr — structural drag on India credit conditions.** The FII equity exit is not just a market-level signal; it feeds into credit conditions via two channels: (1) INR weakness raises the cost of imported goods (crude, electronics, capital goods), which flows through to CPI and RBI rate-cut timing; (2) reduced domestic market liquidity tightens the secondary market for NBFC NCDs and bank CDs, raising funding costs for non-bank lenders. The macro relief from Brent at $79 partially offsets both channels.

## Signals

Brent's jump to $91.10 (+2.11%) is a genuine acceleration, not noise — it extends a four-session climb from $85.95 (Jul 17) that has now added roughly $5/bbl, or ~6%, in under a week. Sourced reporting attributes the move to Middle East geopolitical tensions and supply-disruption concerns, consistent with the Hormuz-related risk channel tracked since early July. A sustained move above $90 widens India's import bill and CAD pressure and reopens the inflation-vs-easing debate ahead of the August 3-5 RBI MPC — petrol/diesel at Delhi are already near record levels, reflecting prior pass-through.

The reported Asian equity moves (Nikkei +3.26%, KOSPI +3.56%, Shanghai +1.79%) are large enough to warrant a second-source check before being treated as confirmed — the collect run itself flags them as unusually large "AI-stock sell-off rebound" moves rather than adopting them outright.

Two India data points diverged sharply from established readings this cycle: bank credit growth at 9.5% YoY (vs. the established 18.6%) and forex reserves at $541.067Bn (vs. $675.16Bn carried since Jul 10). Both are flagged as likely stale, mis-scoped, or differently-dated reads rather than adopted as fresh deterioration — consistent with this wiki's now-persistent pattern of unresolved India data-series conflicts (joining G-Sec 10Y, gold/silver spot, and CMIE unemployment).

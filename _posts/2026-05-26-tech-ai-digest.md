## Signals

1. **India semiconductor race is a two-horse event** — Tata and Murugappa/CG Power represent India's two dominant bets. Tata's Rs 91,000 crore fab is the high-risk/high-reward play; Murugappa's fabless stack is lower-risk/lower-reward. Both strategies are necessary for India to build a complete semiconductor ecosystem.

2. **Tata chip packaging ambition closes the loop**: Tata is now pursuing both wafer fabrication (Dholera) and OSAT packaging. If successful, India will have a partially self-sufficient chip supply chain by 2028–2030.

3. **China open models = adoption arbitrage**: If China's open-weight models (Qwen, DeepSeek family) are winning on deployment while US frontier models win on benchmarks, the practical AI ecosystem outside the US is likely Chinese-influenced. This matters for India's enterprise AI tooling choices.

4. **Claude Cowork in mainstream product discourse**: Featuring in Lenny's "How I AI" signals Claude Cowork is now a reference product in product management/developer circles — validates Anthropic's Cowork category creation.

5. **50% desktop control success rate is a product reality check**: The desktop control narrative from Anthropic is real but nascent. 50% task completion means it's a productivity multiplier for tolerant users, not a seamless assistant yet.

6. **Local AI memory = data sovereignty product category emerging**: Product Hunt leading with on-device AI memory signals user anxiety about cloud AI data. Privacy-first AI products are becoming a distinct category — relevant for India's PDPB compliance context.

7. **AI in India hiring = trust crisis incoming**: Companies blocking AI from interviews is the canary in the coal mine for a broader credentialing trust crisis. If AI can simulate interview performance, the entire talent assessment stack needs redesigning.

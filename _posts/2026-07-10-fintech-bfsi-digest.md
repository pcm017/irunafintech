## TL;DR

- **A device-locking smartphone-lending enforcement tool is reportedly returning to India's EMI market with new conditions**, after RBI's outright 2024 ban — a notable regulatory reversal.
- **PhonePe/Google Pay's combined UPI market share reportedly loosened via competitive market moves (linked to Sachin Bansal)** more than via NPCI's market-share-cap regulation itself.
- **5.2 lakh mule accounts were flagged in March 2026 alone** (ETCISO) — a fresh, larger fraud-scale data point for UPI rails.
- **Zostel flagged Oyo's IPO disclosures to SEBI** — a capital-markets dispute ahead of a widely-anticipated 2026 IPO.
- Editorial framing (ETBFSI/ETCFO) poses whether India's growth engine is shifting from metro hubs to "the next 100 cities" — a tier-2/3 urbanization and credit-demand thesis relevant to branch expansion and NBFC underwriting.

## Signals

**Change detection:** The device-locking tool's return — reversing a 2024 outright ban — is the more consequential regulatory story: it signals RBI is willing to permit a previously-banned collections mechanism under new guardrails, a meaningful shift for unsecured consumer-durable lending risk management.

**Root cause traversal:** The PhonePe/Google Pay share-loosening via market competition (rather than NPCI's cap) suggests regulatory market-structure tools may lag market dynamics in this instance — worth watching whether NPCI revises its approach in response.

**Sizing / impact:** 5.2 lakh mule accounts in a single month is a large base for organized digital fraud on UPI rails, reinforcing the wiki's ongoing fraud/AI-governance thread (see prior digests on deepfake-KYC and agentic-fraud risk).

# Bajaj Finance Q4 FY26 Earnings Call Transcript

**Hosted by:** JM Financial (Ajit Kumar)
**Management:** Rajeev Jain (VC & MD), Sandeep Jain (COO & CFO), Anurag Chottani (AI/Tech)
**Raw file:** `raw/results/a448b6b0-edb5-4b99-a122-d602950310af.pdf`

---

## TL;DR

- Management opened with the ₹5 lakh crore AUM milestone and called out "transformation, not use case deployment" as the FinAI distinction — expects FY27 to be "probably the busiest year from a FinAI standpoint."
- Credit cost guidance for FY27: 145–160 bps (new metric); explicitly entered the year with tailwinds, not headwinds. MSME worst behind by June '26; 2-wheeler captive book winds to <₹1,500 Cr by Sep '26.
- Guidance conservative: explicitly predicated on "easing geopolitical tensions and macro stability." West Asia conflict risk acknowledged — if environment weakens, management believes entering with tailwinds gives room to navigate.
- Franchise model strength: 120M customers; 35% of all India new/used car finance buyers come from BFL's base; 45% of India personal loan takers are in their 120M franchise — cross-sell flywheel is working.
- AI ROI becoming tangible: AI call center agent = 1/3 the cost; peak Diwali loan processing at 600K/day (vs 100K pre-AI); 600+ autonomous agents in FY27 roadmap.

## Key Data / Quotes

> "We are doing transformation, and we are not just doing use case deployment." — Rajeev Jain

> "We are entering the year on credit costs with tailwinds. Headwind over headwind virtually would consume twice the fuel. Thankfully, we are entering the year with tailwinds." — Rajeev Jain

> "It's likely for the first time, we've never done 5 million loans in a month... very likely we'll cross 5 million-plus loans in the month." — Rajeev Jain (on April/May 2026 run rate)

> "AI call center agent is one-third of the cost, straight. We at a point in time had 5,000 outbound voice agents. That number itself, we have brought down... 30% of them are now AI voice agents." — Rajeev Jain

> "Last year, we processed — on peak of Diwali on a single day, we processed 600,000 loans, which without AI, we were like 100,000 loans... we had capacity to process only 100,000 loans on a single day." — Anurag Chottani

> "New car finance and used car finance, 35% of all new car finance and used car finance as per bureau data principally comes from this 120 million customers." — Rajeev Jain

**Credit cost detail (Q&A with Abhishek Murarka, HSBC):**
- Q4 FY26 credit cost (new metric): 165 bps. FY27 guidance: 145–160 bps.
- Old metric reconciliation: gross loan provisions ₹2,126 Cr minus ₹260 Cr recoveries = ₹1,866 Cr net = 165 bps. Full year FY26: 175 bps on old metric.
- Guidance reduction from "165–175 bps" (prior quarter) to "145–160 bps" is partly due to presentation change + genuinely improving outlook.

**MSME (Q&A with Piran Engineer, CLSA):**
- Portfolio pruned: from ₹1,800 Cr/month to ₹1,400 Cr/month disbursements; further cuts in May 2026 based on micro assessment.
- Recovery to double-digit growth expected Q2–Q3 FY27.
- "Our business is micro, not macro" — Rajeev Jain.

**Long-term ROA framework (Q&A with Shreya Shivani, Nomura):**
- 4 levers: growth, margin expansion, opex, credit cost — orchestrated to deliver 4.3–4.7% ROA and 20–22% ROE.
- 19-year track record invoked; NIM expansion not required to hit FY27 ROA guidance.

**Provision coverage (Q&A with Viral Shah, IIFL):**
- PCR at historical high outside COVID. Moving to quarterly ECL exercise (from annual). Philosophy: "resiliency of balance sheet."
- PCR expected to remain in current corridor.

## Signals

- **May 2026 run rate is exceptional.** Management's confidence that they may log 5M+ loans in a month — above the prior 4.6M festival peak — suggests April/May demand is strong despite West Asia uncertainty. This is a bullish read on India consumer and SME credit demand.
- **AI ROI is now quantifiable.** 1/3 cost on AI call centers, 6x peak loan processing, 28% software dev efficiency. These aren't pilots — they're in production at scale. FinAI is structurally changing BFL's operating model.
- **Management transparency on geopolitics is notable.** Rajeev Jain explicitly flagged Hormuz risk twice, adjusted guidance with caveats. This is atypical for an Indian NBFC earnings call — signals the disruption is on management's radar as a genuine risk to credit costs.
- **MSME micro-management.** Real-time portfolio cuts (₹400 Cr/month reduction in May 2026) based on micro-level location/underwriting assessment shows a level of operational precision that likely protects the book if the macro deteriorates.

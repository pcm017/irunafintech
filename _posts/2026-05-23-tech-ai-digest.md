## TL;DR

- **Data center veto problem** (Ben Thompson/Stratechery) — the defining constraint on AI buildout is now community opposition and permitting, not capital or compute; established hyperscalers benefit
- **Parag Agrawal's "Parallel"** — new company addressing agent economics: AI agents browsing/synthesizing web content without paying content creators; micropayment layer needed
- **Google I/O deeper analysis** — DeepMind's distinct AGI philosophy: "AGI as scientific discovery problem" (AlphaFold model); vs. OpenAI capability scaling vs. Anthropic safety-first
- **a16z Sohn: "Peak CS"** — rate of return on pure software engineering plateauing; domain expertise + AI > pure coding; small business boom thesis; token maxxing as workflow strategy
- **NVIDIA: inference scaling = training compute** — Blackwell for inference at scale; physical AI next wave; India strategic market
- **Intel closes Costa Rica A&T** — semiconductor geography lesson: back-end consolidating in SE Asia; India's OSAT ambitions = cautionary tale
- **Anti-AI legislation: bipartisan support in US** — Left (labor) + Right (culture) converging; content labeling, creator compensation, platform liability gaining traction; Tamil Nadu AI Act fits global pattern

## Key data / quotes

- Data centers: 100MW–1GW+ power requirement per facility; multi-year permitting delays for hyperscalers in coastal metros
- Intel Costa Rica: at peak (~2000), represented **~40% of Costa Rica's total exports**
- AI-generated phishing: UPI processes 21.63B txns/month; fraud detection window <1 second
- Sundar Pichai "flip phone moment": current AI products are the flip phone; smartphone moment still ahead
- Pichai on India: *"The scale of problems and the talent pool make India central to how AI develops globally"*
- NVIDIA: "inference scaling" — running AI for inference will require as much compute as training, maybe more
- India BPO/IT services sector: 3.5M+ employees at TCS, Infosys, Wipro, HCL exposed to AI automation

## Signals

- **Data center veto = structural moat for hyperscalers:** Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meta have existing land and permitting relationships. New entrants (even well-funded ones) face multi-year delays. This concentrates AI infrastructure power further in the Big 4 cloud providers. For India: Adani/Reliance Jio data center ambitions benefit from India's permitting environment being relatively less adversarial.
- **Agent economics problem is real and unsolved:** Parag Agrawal's Parallel addresses a structural tension: AI agents are destroying the web's economic model. Content creators and publishers will demand compensation. This will eventually create compliance/licensing costs for every company building AI agents — including Anthropic, OpenAI, Perplexity. India content companies lack lobbying power to participate in the early deals.
- **DeepMind AGI philosophy = differentiated product strategy:** Google's science-first AGI approach (AlphaFold, drug discovery, AlphaCode) produces different products than OpenAI's capability scaling. Google I/O was "not flashy but strategically significant" because it embedded AI into 330M+ business users' daily workflows. Long-term, this is a different and potentially more durable value capture than new product launches.
- **Peak CS → domain expertise premium:** The "peak CS" thesis suggests future value in tech accrues to people who combine deep domain expertise with AI — not pure coders. For India's IT services sector (TCS, Infosys, etc.), the offshore coding arbitrage model is under structural pressure. The pivot to AI-enabled consulting/domain expertise is existential.
- **Intel Costa Rica → India semiconductor caution:** India's initial semiconductor play (Micron Sanand OSAT, CG Power) is in exactly the back-end/OSAT space that Intel is abandoning to SE Asia. The value creation in chips is in advanced packaging and leading-edge fabs — not commodity assembly and test. India needs to move up the stack quickly or risk a "Costa Rica trap."
- **Anti-AI legislation as policy risk:** The bipartisan convergence on anti-AI legislation in the US is the clearest policy risk signal of 2026. If the US moves to mandatory AI content labeling, creator compensation, and platform liability, every major AI company's cost structure changes. India context: Tamil Nadu AI Act is the leading edge of state-level regulation here.
- **AI PR emergency → India's BPO/IT exposure:** India's 3.5M IT/BPO workforce is the most concentrated exposure to AI displacement globally. The PR emergency described by Kantrowitz will become a political emergency in India, driven by trade union pressure on Infosys/TCS. Watch for AI commitments in employment numbers in coming quarterly results.

## TL;DR

- Trump reinstated a naval blockade on Iranian ports/vessels transiting the Strait of Hormuz — Brent jumped to $86.35 (+3.66%, week gain >10%), the sharpest single read of the current Iran flare-up, though a stale Friday-settle source ($76.01) was also preserved as a conflict.
- Gold fell inversely on the oil shock (-1.4% to -2.9%, sources conflict, $4,002-4,064/oz); US equities fell a second straight session (S&P -0.79%, Nasdaq -1.55%, Dow -0.26%) with premarket futures soft ahead of US CPI.
- India June CPI confirmed at 4.38% (up from 3.93% May, food inflation 5.32%) via three independent sources — the sharpest MoM CPI jump logged in the wiki this cycle.
- India bank credit growth held at 17.7% YoY (fortnight to Jun 15) — a slight moderation from the 18.6% 2-year-high logged Jul 13; forex reserves rose to $674.19Bn (+$7.26Bn WoW).
- FII stayed net equity buyers for a second week in July (₹15,157 Cr MTD) after 4 months of selling; China's PBoC bought 14.93t of gold in June, a 20th straight month of buying.

## Signals

Trump's reinstated naval blockade on Iranian ports is the fourth distinct escalation of the Jul 8 ceasefire-breakdown cycle, and the Brent move to $86.35 (if the higher of the two conflicting reads holds) would be the sharpest single-day level since the May peak — directly threatening the August 3-5 RBI MPC's room to cut, even as June bank credit growth (17.7% YoY) shows the domestic credit cycle retains momentum independent of the external shock. The June CPI print (4.38%, up from 3.93%) is the largest single-month CPI jump logged in this wiki's tracking period, driven by food inflation (5.32%) — this compounds the crude-price risk into a genuine two-sided inflation problem for RBI rather than a one-off. FII's second consecutive week of net equity buying (₹15,157 Cr MTD) is a partially offsetting constructive signal, suggesting foreign capital is treating the Iran flare-up as noise rather than a reason to exit India risk.

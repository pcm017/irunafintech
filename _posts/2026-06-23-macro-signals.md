## Signals

- **US-Iran 60-day roadmap**: Reports emerged of a 60-day timeline toward a final Iran peace deal — adding a second phase to the June 19 Swiss signing. Markets are reading this as further normalisation, with oil risk premium continuing to compress. WTI at $74.30 represents pre-conflict pricing territory reached months ahead of formal Hormuz normalisation.
- **WTI sub-$75**: The most significant energy signal since the June 19 deal signing. At $74.30, India's annualised crude import savings vs. the May $111/bbl Brent peak approach $100B+. The trajectory toward $70 becomes a realistic 60-day scenario if the peace roadmap holds.
- **India oil imports rebounding on record Russian crude**: India replaced much of the Gulf supply disruption by sharply increasing discounted Russian crude imports — structural shift, not cyclical. ONGC rebranding as a "gas and oil firm" (not "oil and gas") signals India's energy policy explicitly pivoting to gas-first.
- **India forex >$700B**: Third consecutive weekly rise from the $681B May low — RBI's intervention capacity is restored at the same time as crude falls. The simultaneous Brent decline + reserves recovery is the most positive macro combination India has experienced in 2026.
- **India G-Sec at 3-month low (~6.80%)**: Foreign banks and overseas funds are active buyers at this level, consistent with the RBI capital inflow package (G-Sec tax exemption + FAR expansion). India-US spread ~230bps — narrowing from the 260bps May stress peak.
- **Nikkei +1.96%**: Asian equity resilience while US tech pulled back — the inter-market divergence continues; Japan is benefiting from the Iran deal risk-on and a structurally weak yen improving export earnings.

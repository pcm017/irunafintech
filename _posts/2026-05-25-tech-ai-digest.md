# Tech & AI Digest — 2026-05-25

**TL;DR**
- OpenAI: **$2M per YC P26/S26 startup in API tokens** — aggressive ecosystem lock-in via YC investment (paid in credits, not cash)
- OpenAI deliberately **avoided the telco-AI trap** — rejected deep distribution partnerships with Indian and global telcos; prefers direct consumer/enterprise relationships
- **Maharashtra walks back data centre incentives** — significant regulatory uncertainty for India AI infrastructure investment thesis
- "**The AI paradox**" (Dan Shipper/Lenny's Newsletter): more automation = more humans, more work; PM and designer roles become more valuable as agent-directors
- "**Cheap Competence**" (Every.to): AI commoditizes baseline professional competence; raises value of judgment, expertise, and trust; "volume work goes to AI, high-stakes work commands premium"
- Ryan Carson: **solo founder running a startup with AI agents** (OpenClaw, Codex, Devin) — handles meetings, ads, and product autonomously
- **AI at SMB scale**: local pizza shop using AI — adoption has crossed the enterprise/SMB boundary
- AI makes **great salespeople matter more**, not less — relationship-intensive enterprise selling becomes premium as SDR work is automated

**Key data / quotes**
- OpenAI: $2M investment per YC P26 and S26 company (in tokens); YC extended summer deadline to May 25
- Paul Graham: "Should founders move to Silicon Valley?" — ongoing founder location debate
- OpenAI deliberately evaluated and **rejected deep telecom distribution partnerships** in India and globally; prefers maintaining direct consumer and enterprise channels (The Ken)
- Maharashtra: state government **reversing data centre incentive commitments** to operators — Reliance/Jio/Adani hyperscaler plans affected; signals policy uncertainty
- Dan Shipper (Lenny's Newsletter): "Most work will happen inside Codex or Claude Code"; PMs and designers "wildly bullish" — human taste and judgment at orchestration layer become scarce
- Every.to "Cheap Competence": AI = cheap, abundant baseline competence; "New frontier: not replacing professionals but restructuring the economics of professional services"
- Ryan Carson (5x founder): running startup solo using OpenClaw + Codex (OpenAI) + Devin (Cognition) for meeting booking, running ads, shipping features
- "My Local Pizza Shop is Using AI" (Mostly Metrics): SMB operators now using LLM-based chatbots, scheduling, ordering optimization
- First Round Capital Review: "Why AI makes great salespeople matter even more" — AI automates SDR/prospecting, freeing top enterprise sales for complex deal orchestration

**Signals**
- OpenAI's telco rejection is a **strategic intelligence signal for India**: India's telcos (Jio, Airtel, BSNL) cannot be the primary AI distribution channel for frontier models. OpenAI and Anthropic will go direct. Telcos may still benefit as compute infrastructure hosts (GPUaaS — Tech Mahindra angle from May 24 digest)
- Maharashtra data centre reversal is a **setback for India's AI infrastructure ambitions**: Google Vizag ($15B) and Adani/Uber DC are central/state government-backed, but Maharashtra — which hosts most of India's enterprise tech footprint (Mumbai, Pune) — is now a question mark. Foreign investors will hesitate on state-level commitments
- The "AI paradox" thesis is the most important framework for India's knowledge worker economy: AI creates more work, not less, because it lowers the cost of trying things. PM, designer, and orchestration roles become more valuable; junior coding and analyst roles compress
- "Cheap Competence" has direct implications for India's professional services economy (TCS/Infosys at the top end, LegalTech, HealthTech): clients will benchmark against AI cost baselines; only differentiated expertise commands premium. India IT's offshore arbitrage is being replaced by expertise arbitrage
- The **solopreneur template** (Ryan Carson) is now commercially viable at meaningful scale. India's startup ecosystem — which is capital-constrained relative to Silicon Valley — should be watching this closely as it changes the capital efficiency bar for new ventures
- SMB AI adoption crossing the "local pizza shop" threshold means India's fintech companies targeting SMBs (Paytm for merchants, PhonePe, BharatPe) will soon be competing with AI-native tools embedded in their own customer workflows

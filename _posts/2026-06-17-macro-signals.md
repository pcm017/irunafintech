## Signals

**Change Detection:** Brent dropped from $83.82 (June 15) to $78.89 on June 17 — a further $5/bbl decline post Iran-deal signing announcement, confirming the supply-return trade is fully engaged. At $78.89, India's annualised crude import savings vs. May $111 peak exceed $85B/year.

**Root Cause:** Trump's June 16 explicit Hormuz reopening confirmation triggered accelerated crude longs liquidation. WTI at $75.85 is the first sub-$76 reading since before the conflict. Market divergence (Dow record vs. S&P/Nasdaq chip selloff) reflects rotation from AI/tech into cyclical/industrial beneficiaries of the peace deal.

**Sizing / India Impact:**
- Each $1/bbl Brent decline saves India ~$1.4B/year in import costs. At $78.89 vs. May peak $111, India is saving ~$45B/year annualised — the single largest macroeconomic relief since the conflict began.
- WPI 9.68% is a mechanical peak; with Brent now below $79 and falling, fuel & power component (+30.33%) will reverse over 60-90 days — setting up a sharp WPI deceleration by Aug-Sep 2026.
- US 10Y at 4.44% (declining on oil deflation + Iran deal) creates improved carry for FII India G-Sec flows; India-US spread should widen from ~238bps (June 16) back toward 250-260bps as US yields fall faster than India yields.
- PNB and Indian Bank guiding 12-14% credit growth is above system average — signals mid-tier PSU banks are still growing but below the 16.2% FY26 rate (ICRA FY27 projection 11-11.7%).

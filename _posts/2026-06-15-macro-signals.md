### Gmail Commentary — Citrini Research: "What Comes Out In The Warsh?"

**Source:** Citrini Research | Date: June 14, 2026

Key thesis: Medium-term bullish on US economy and equities. The inflation/rate concern is real but likely temporary — commodity disruption from Iran conflict has largely peaked; core CPI stable; labour market not as tight as headline payrolls suggest.

On Kevin Warsh as new Fed chair: "Nothing" — expected to hold rates, not hike, in response to current inflation readings. The recent tech/momentum selloff was an "overdue flush of extreme leverage in extended momentum names" — fundamentals support a melt-up into late summer, with 10–15% drawdowns off highs over the next 3–4 months.

Key escalation chain: Since the Iran war began (Feb 28), short-term US rate expectations jumped 100bps; SOFR futures briefly priced near-term rate hikes (first since early 2023); ECB hiked 25bps; US CPI printed highest headline since 2023; May jobs +172K vs 88K consensus (nearly double). But Citrini argues these factors are likely to fade: Iran commodity shock peaked; core inflation anchored; labour market internals diverge from headline.

"Momo vs. Macro" framing: Market oscillates between AI-driven equity momentum and brief macro-concern volatility windows. Last year: tariffs in spring + growth scare in winter. This year: Iran conflict correction → early ceasefire → ATH → Broadcom miss + jobs report correction → Iran deal resolution.

## Signals

**Iran deal pricing fully in:** Brent at $87.30 (vs. $111 May peak = -21%) with institutional longs at 4-month low means markets are pricing not just deescalation but near-certain Hormuz reopening. If formal deal materialises (Switzerland June 19 signing per Filter Coffee), Brent tests $75–80. India import bill savings: ~$6–8B/month vs. May peak.

**India G-Sec at 6.80% / India-US spread 231bps:** The RBI's coordinated capital inflow package (FCNR(B) + G-Sec tax exemption + LCR pricing) is working. G-Sec yield compressing while US 10Y holds at 4.49% = India gaining relative carry even as US rate hike fears persist. This is the narrowest India-US spread in the wiki's tracking period and represents a successful bond market liberalisation outcome.

**India CPI May 3.90%:** Below RBI's 4% mid-target for May. If June CPI continues below 4% (seasonal monsoon effects), August MPC becomes a live rate-cut meeting. The combination of below-target CPI + Brent at $87 and falling = stagflation risk easing, opening RBI's policy room.

**VIX at 14.72 (-5.7%):** Fear declining sharply. Combined with Nifty PCR 0.93 (mild sentiment normalization from extreme bearish PCR <0.80 of June 9), market internals are healing faster than the fundamental picture warrants. Citrini's 10–15% drawdown thesis (from highs, over next 3–4 months) remains relevant — elevated volatility around Warsh confirmation and AI capex data.

**China gold 19th consecutive month:** Persistent central bank gold buying from China (2,331.52t, +9.95t in May) continues regardless of price. At 2,331t, China has now accumulated ~74t in the last 19 months. This structural demand is the long-run floor under gold even as US rate expectations drive short-term selling.

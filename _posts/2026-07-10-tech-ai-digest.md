# Tech & AI Digest — 2026-07-10

## TL;DR

- **Meta confirmed its custom AI chip (designed with Broadcom, manufactured by TSMC) enters production in September**, aiming to double computing capacity and reduce Nvidia/AMD dependency.
- **Qualcomm's $3.9Bn Modular acquisition** builds a CUDA-competing software platform alongside its new Dragonfly data-center chip line; shares rose up to 15% on a $15Bn-by-FY29 AI-data-center sales forecast.
- **South Korea unveiled a $520Bn investment plan with Samsung and SK Hynix** to expand memory dominance (four new fabs plus HBM facilities).
- **India: L&T Semiconductor Technologies signed an MoU with Azuremoto** for AI-data-center Silicon Carbide (SiC) power semiconductors.
- **120,000 global tech layoffs in 2026 attributed to AI; ~35,000 India-specific cuts projected this fiscal year** — set against TCS adding >9,200 net employees in Q1 FY27.
- TSMC's Q2 2026 earnings (July 16) already guided to $39.0-40.2Bn revenue (+32% YoY).

## Key data / quotes

- ETCIO: Meta tailored a custom AI chip for its own needs, working with Broadcom on design and TSMC on manufacturing; production begins September as Meta looks to double computing capacity; aims to lower costs and reduce reliance on Nvidia/AMD.
- The Daily Upside/Fortune/TheStreet: Qualcomm's $3.9 billion Modular acquisition gives it a CUDA-competing software platform; shares rose as much as 15% on a $15Bn annual AI-data-center sales forecast by FY29.

- ETHRWorld: 120,000 global tech layoffs in 2026 attributed to AI-driven workforce reset; ~35,000 India-specific tech job cuts projected this fiscal year; TCS added >9,200 employees in Q1 FY27 — a hiring/layoffs divergence.
- SEC 6-K filing: ASML reported €8.8 billion Q1 2026 net sales, €2.8 billion net income; guided full-year 2026 net sales of €36-40 billion, gross margin 51-53%.
- Company/watch-list data: TSMC Q2 2026 earnings (July 16) guided to $39.0-40.2bn revenue (+32% YoY, 10% QoQ at midpoint), 65.5-67.5% gross margin.

## Signals

**Change detection:** Meta's confirmed September production date for its custom AI chip is a concrete milestone in the hyperscaler-silicon-independence trend already tracked (Amazon, Google custom silicon) — moving from "in talks" to a dated commitment.

**Root cause traversal:** The TCS-hiring-vs-India-layoffs divergence (9,200 net adds vs. a 35,000 India-wide cut estimate) suggests the AI-driven job disruption is concentrated at smaller/mid-tier IT firms rather than uniformly across the sector — consistent with the wiki's July 1 Ramp-study finding that heavy-AI-spend companies are hiring, not cutting.

**Sizing / impact:** L&T-Azuremoto's SiC power-semiconductor MoU adds a new India angle (power electronics for AI data centers) distinct from the logic/memory OSAT story already tracked — worth watching as a potential new category in India's semiconductor value chain. TSMC's July 16 earnings is the next major read on whether the memory/AI-hardware "mega-cycle" survives the July 7 sell-off.

## Signals

**Oil regime shift underway:** Brent at $89.23 and WTI at $85.94 represent the first sustained break below $90 since before the US-Iran conflict escalation. Trump's suspension of attack plans and deal signals remove ~$8–12/bbl of geopolitical risk premium. If a formal US-Iran deal closes, Brent could test $75–80 — India's single biggest near-term macro relief.

**US CPI 4.2% in May is the key rate signal:** Up from 3.4% in April; the largest monthly jump in 2026. Markets are now pricing a December Fed rate hike with ~70% probability. This extends gold's bear market (rate-driven safe haven destruction), keeps the US 10Y elevated near 4.54%, and constrains RBI's rate-cut path.

**India bond market diverging from US:** India G-Sec 10Y falling to 6.80% while US 10Y holds at 4.54% — the India-US spread narrows to +226bps, the tightest since tracking began. Driver is FII debt buying following the RBI's capital inflow package (tax exemption on G-Sec capital gains, expanded FPI access). This is the RBI's capital inflow strategy working — even in a rising US rate environment, India bonds are attracting flows.

**FCNR(B) rate hike signals dollar liquidity stress:** Banks aggressively raising FCNR(B) rates indicates they need NRI foreign currency deposits to manage the USD/INR liability mismatch from heavy INR depreciation. Historically, FCNR(B) rate spikes precede short-term INR stabilisation (2013 Rajan playbook). Watch for whether INR moves toward 94–95 if the Iran deal closes.

**RBI's 135 NBFC cancellations = regulatory cleanup:** Not a sign of sector distress per se — bulk of these will be dormant/shell entities. But signals RBI tightening sector oversight ahead of any credit cycle turn. Directionally consistent with ICRA's projection of bank credit slowing to 11–11.7% in FY27.

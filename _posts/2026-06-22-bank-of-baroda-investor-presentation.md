# Bank of Baroda Investor Presentation — June 2026 (Q4FY26 Results Data)

*Filed with BSE/NSE on 20.06.2026 ahead of a 22nd June 2026 Investor Engagement Meeting. Reports Q4FY26/FY26 data (as of 31 March 2026) in USD Mn, converted here to ₹ cr at the stated FEDAI rate (1 USD = ₹94.8350, as of 30 March 2026). Companion document to *2026 05 08 bank of baroda q4fy26 analyst meet* (analyst meet transcript), which was ingested as a Q&A dialogue without a full P&L table — that source page's Contradictions section explicitly noted "no investor presentation for detailed P&L line-items." This presentation closes that gap.*

## TL;DR

- Confirms transcript figures: FY26 PAT ₹20,022 cr (+2.2% YoY in USD terms; entity page's ~+11%+ framing was for operating metrics, not FY PAT — FY26 PAT growth is actually modest at the full-year level despite a strong Q4)
- **New, more precise number:** Cost-to-Income ratio fell sharply in Q4 to 44.90% (from 52.10% Q3FY26, -499 bps YoY vs Q4FY25's 49.89%) — but FY26 full-year Cost-to-Income actually rose 121 bps YoY to 49.15%, meaning the Q4 efficiency gain does not yet represent a full-year trend reversal
- **New:** Full interest income/expense bridge — Total Interest Income ₹1,26,995 cr FY26 (+3.8%), Total Interest Expenses ₹79,314 cr FY26 (+4.7%) — interest expense growing faster than interest income is the direct driver of the NII growth deceleration to +2.5% FY26 (well below the +8.7% Q4 YoY pace)
- **New:** Q3FY26 asset-quality data point now visible in trend — GNPA improved 2.04% (Q3) → 1.89% (Q4); PCR excl. technical write-offs also disclosed for the first time: 76.66% (Q4FY26) vs 72.21% (Q3FY26)
- Total Provisions Q4FY26 ₹3,148 cr (of which NPA/bad-debt provisions ₹2,570 cr) — this is the first quarter this component has been broken out; consistent with the transcript's flagged ₹1,500 cr voluntary ECL floating provision sitting inside "Provision for Standard Advances" (₹190 cr Q4 alone, but the ₹1,500 cr one-time item likely flows through a different sub-line or FY26 cumulative ₹1,204 cr standard-advance provision)

## Signals

**FY26 NII growth (+2.5%) is far weaker than the Q4FY26 YoY print (+8.7%) — full-year interest-expense growth (+4.7%) is outpacing interest-income growth (+3.8%).** This is the same T-bill-vs-MCLR and deposit-cost story flagged in the transcript, but the annual data shows the drag was heaviest in the first three quarters of FY26, with Q4 representing the inflection point where NII growth caught up. The FY27 guidance (NIM 2.75–2.95%) implicitly assumes this Q4 trajectory holds for a full year, not just one quarter.

**Cost-to-Income's Q4 improvement (44.90%, -499 bps YoY) does not yet show up at the full-year level (49.15%, +121 bps YoY) — treat the Q4 number as a single-quarter data point, not a trend confirmation.** Operating expenses fell 8.7% YoY in Q4 even as operating income rose — a genuine efficiency gain — but three quarters of FY26 ran cost-to-income above 50%, and the FY26 annual ratio is worse than FY25's 47.94%. BoB's own turnaround narrative (per the entity page thesis) depends on this Q4 print becoming the new run-rate, not a one-off.

**The provisions bridge confirms the one-time nature of Q4's credit-cost spike, but the composition matters.** Of the ₹3,148 cr in total Q4FY26 provisions, ₹2,570 cr was NPA/bad-debt provisioning (up 97.9% YoY) — a large absolute jump that dominates the "Provision for Standard Advances" line (only ₹190 cr in Q4, ₹1,204 cr for the full year) where the transcript's ₹1,500 cr voluntary ECL floating provision would most plausibly sit. This suggests the ECL-transition provision may be embedded across FY26 cumulative standard-advances provisioning rather than concentrated in Q4 alone — worth flagging as a reconciliation item for the next quarter's ingest, since the transcript described it as a discrete Q4 action.

**Fee mix ratio (11.4% FY26) is structurally the lowest among the three PSU/private banks now cross-referenced in this wiki (ICICI ~22%, SBI ~21%, BoB ~11%)** — consistent with BoB's PSU-bank profile (lower CASA, more commoditized product mix) and reinforcing the entity page's framing that BoB's re-rating case depends more on NIM/credit-cost discipline than fee-income diversification.

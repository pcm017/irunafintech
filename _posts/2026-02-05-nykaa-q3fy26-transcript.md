# Nykaa Q3 FY26 Earnings Conference Call Transcript

**Raw file:** [raw/results/132004f5-80ca-47e1-91ec-36190368640f.pdf](../../raw/results/132004f5-80ca-47e1-91ec-36190368640f.pdf)

## TL;DR

- Falguni Nayar (CEO/Chairperson) presented Q3 FY26 highlights: GMV ₹5,795 Cr (+28%), EBITDA ₹230 Cr (8.0%, highest ever), PAT ₹68 Cr (+156%).
- Beauty EBITDA hits 10.1% margin — driven by "all cylinders firing" (Beauty.com, retail stores, House of Nykaa brands all performing).
- Fashion EBITDA improved to −2.0% (from −5.4%) — H&M brand win, festive season, improved unit economics.
- Nykaa's 4 platforms: Nykaa Beauty, Nykaa Fashion, Nykaa Man, Nysaa (GCC); 52M total customers served across all time.
- L'Oreal partnership deepened — 3 major new exclusives this quarter (La Roche-Posay, NYX, Kiehl's).
- B2B2C partnerships: Nike (full D2C operations), Kiehl's (stores + ecomm + D2C), Foot Locker (growing).

## Key data / quotes

### Opening remarks (Falguni Nayar)
- *"It's been a good quarter... Nykaa has delivered for a long period, almost 13, 14 quarters of mid-20s growth on a sustainable basis."*
- *"The EBITDA is at about INR 230 crores for the quarter, which is 8.0% of net revenue and a 63% year-on-year growth. This is the highest EBITDA margin ever."*
- On Beauty: *"Pretty much all the cylinders have fired very nicely this quarter."*
- On Fashion: *"The fashion business EBITDA margin is minus 2.0%. It's come down from minus 5.4% a year ago."*
- On 52M customers: *"We now have 52 million customers who we have served till date through all our platforms."*

### Beauty business (Anchit Nayar, CEO Beauty)
- *"We have delivered the highest GMV in our history at INR 4,302 crores... 27% growth year-over-year. It has also resulted in a 10.1% EBITDA margin."*
- Customer acquisition: 18.7M annual unique transacting customers (+26% YoY); *"India is underpenetrated in terms of beauty consumption"*
- L'Oreal decade partnership: decade-long; *"La Roche-Posay — world's #1 dermatologist recommended skin care brand — exclusive launch with Nykaa."*
- NYX Makeup: exclusive distribution via Nykaa Global store.
- Kiehl's: *"taking over the Kiehl's operations in India... operating their D2C website, their freestanding stores as well as retailing the brand on Nykaa.com."*

### New store formats
- Nykaa Perfumery: fragrance-only stores (2 stores launched); *"average order values in these fragrance stores"* — transcript cut, but management bullish on premium fragrance as underpenetrated category.
- Kay Kafe: beauty + coffee + community concept — targeting Gen Z brand engagement.
- Nykaa Wanderlust card: low CapEx/fixed cost pop-up format for Bath & Body products.

### Fashion (implied from context)
- H&M win in Q3 — marquee brand addition driving fashion segment growth.
- NSV/GMV gap: Fashion GMV +31% but NSV +25% (9M FY26) — discount/returns impact.

## Signals

1. **Kiehl's B2B2C = the full-stack brand management play.** Nykaa is no longer just a retailer or marketplace — it is taking on full commercial operations for premium brands in India (stores, ecomm, D2C website, imports, distribution). Nike and Kiehl's are the proof-of-concept. If this scales to 10+ brands, it becomes a meaningful fee/margin stream independent of GMV.

2. **52M lifetime customers vs. 18.7M annual transacting** — only ~36% of ever-acquired customers are active annually. The re-activation and reactivation flywheel (loyalty programs, personalisation, new format stores) is an important growth lever that Nykaa hasn't fully exploited yet.

3. **Nykaa Man and Nysaa (GCC)** — mentioned but not quantified in this transcript. These are two growth vectors (male grooming, GCC market) that are currently small but could be significant within 3–5 years as male grooming penetration grows in India and Indian diaspora spending in GCC expands.

4. **Nykaa Perfumery = premium fragrance bet.** Fragrance is the highest-ASP, lowest-penetration beauty category in India. A dedicated store concept signals confidence that a meaningful Indian consumer segment is ready to spend ₹5,000–50,000 on fragrances with guidance and experience. Comparable to how speciality coffee/wine retail developed in India — small now, large category in 5 years.

5. **PAT at 2.4% of NR** — while the 156% growth is impressive, absolute PAT of ₹68 Cr on ₹2,873 Cr revenue means the P&L is still thin. One bad quarter on cost structure or gross margin could erase the PAT. The ₹78 Cr adjusted figure (excluding new labour code one-time) is more instructive — Nykaa's sustainable PAT margin is ~2.7%, not yet a high-conviction compounding margin business.

## TL;DR

- Q4 FY26: AUM ₹60,348 crore (+69.4% YoY); PAT ₹255 crore (+69.6% QoQ); ROA 1.81% — strongest quarterly profitability since company pivot.
- NIM restored to 9.05% (+43 bps QoQ) — management had guided 9%+ in 3–4 quarters; achieved in 3.
- Asset quality improving: GNPA 1.44% (-7 bps QoQ), credit cost declining to 2.51%.
- 6 new product lines (Prime PL, Gold Loan, Consumer Durables, CV, Education, Shopkeeper) now contribute 24% of disbursements.
- 400 gold loan branches operationalised as committed; QIP of ₹2,500 crore raised in April 2026.
- Management guiding **35–40% AUM CAGR** over next 2 years; "scale phase" beginning FY27.
- AAA rated (CRISIL + CARE). CAR 16.83% (simulated 20.74% post-QIP).

## Signals

- **Poonawalla inflection is real**: The combination of PAT +311% YoY, ROA recovery from 0.78% to 1.81%, NIM restoration to 9%+, and GNPA decline — all in a single quarter — validates the multi-product consumer NBFC strategy post the Adar Poonawalla pivot from legacy lending.
- **New product ramp-up de-risks AUM concentration**: Six new products now 24% of disbursements; historically heavy dependence on business loans and LAP reduces.
- **Gold loan network a potential long-tail moat**: 400 branches in Tier 2/3 locations, 90%+ in non-metro markets — Poonawalla is going where banks and fintechs don't go. Unit economics "very favorable" once at scale, per management.
- **Credit cost trajectory is the key watch**: GNPA at 1.44% and declining; 6MoB30+ delinquencies at new lows. If credit cost falls below 2% in FY27, ROA could cross 2%+.
- **Prefr listed as digital aggregator channel**: Confirms Poonawalla uses Prefr's DSA/digital stack for loan sourcing — relevant for Prefr's lender network context.

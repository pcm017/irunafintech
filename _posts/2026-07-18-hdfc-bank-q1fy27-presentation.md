# HDFC Bank Q1 FY27 Earnings Presentation (+ Key Parameters Sheet)

## TL;DR

- PAT ₹190.6 bn (+5.0% YoY, -0.8% QoQ); PBT +17.9% YoY as provisions normalised sharply (-78.8% YoY) against a high Q1 FY26 base.
- **NII +6.7% YoY / +1.4% QoQ to ₹335.3 bn**, but **net revenue fell -12.8% YoY** — Q1 FY26's non-interest income was inflated by a one-off ₹91.3 bn HDBFS transaction gain (OFS), so the YoY revenue decline is a base-effect artefact, not operating deterioration.
- **NIM compressed to 3.26%** (on total assets) from 3.4% a year ago and 3.4% last quarter — the first sub-3.3% print logged for this bank, even as deposit growth (14.7% YoY) continues to outpace advances (15.4% YoY EOP) and borrowings keep shrinking (11% of liabilities, vs. 21% at merger).
- Asset quality stable: GNPA 1.17% (ex-agri 0.91%), credit cost 40bps (up from 35bps Q4 FY26 but still well below the FY26 average); capital adequacy 19.6% (CET1 17.4%).
- Fee income (Fees & Commissions) ₹85 bn, down from ₹92 bn Q4 FY26 — fee mix ratio 18.3% of total revenue, still below the FY26 average.

## Signals

The headline -12.8% YoY revenue decline and -78.8% YoY provisions decline are both base-effect artefacts from Q1 FY26's one-off HDBFS OFS transaction gain and elevated provisioning — stripped of that, the underlying trend is NII growing a healthy 6.7% YoY on 14.7% deposit growth outpacing 15.4% advance growth, consistent with the bank's stated "deposit-first, ROA-over-NIM" optimisation-phase strategy tracked since Q4 FY26. The NIM compression to 3.26% — the first sub-3.3% print logged for this entity — is the one genuinely new negative data point this quarter, and sits in tension with management's prior guidance that TD repricing (40-50bps still to transmit) would support NIM recovery; this quarter shows further compression instead, reopening the open question already flagged on *hdfc bank* about whether NIM recovery materialises in FY27 or stays range-bound through the rate-cycle pause. Borrowings continuing to fall as a share of liabilities (11%, unchanged from Q4 FY26) confirms the multi-year deleveraging thesis remains on track even as it hasn't yet delivered the promised NIM tailwind.

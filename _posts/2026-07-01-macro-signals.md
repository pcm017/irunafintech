## Signals

**1. Dow crosses 52,000 for the first time; Q2 2026 was a blowout quarter across all three major US indices.** S&P +14%, Nasdaq +~20%, Dow +12% for the quarter — a decisive reversal from the AI-spending-skepticism selloffs tracked through mid-to-late June (June 23 "AI wake-up call," June 27 GPT-5.6-driven Nasdaq weekly -4.6%). The June 30 session alone (S&P +0.79%, Nasdaq +1.52%) confirms the AI/tech recovery narrative reasserted itself into quarter-end. This is a meaningful counter-signal to the ET CISO "AI investment boom may fuel financial risks" warning tracked in the same-day fintech digest — markets are pricing continuation of the AI capex cycle, not a pullback.

**2. Iran publicly refuses talks "at any level" even as a US delegation travels to Qatar — the ceasefire framework faces its first public friction point.** This directly contradicts the more optimistic June 24 "provisional ceasefire framework" and June 27-28 "peace dividend fully expressed" narrative that has driven WTI/Brent lower through late June. The market's initial reaction (Brent bouncing to $74.28, +1.99%, off the June 29 ~$72 low) suggests this is being read as a negotiating posture rather than a deal-collapse signal — but it is the most direct public friction point since the June 19 signing, and warrants close tracking given how central the Iran deal has been to India's 2026 macro relief thesis.

**3. Nifty PCR shifts from bullish (0.808, June 30) to near-neutral (1.02, July 1) in a single session.** Combined with Bank Nifty falling 449 points and India VIX ticking up slightly (13.73 from 13.43), India options positioning is normalising from the extreme bullish/complacent read of June 30 — consistent with profit-taking after the multi-week rally rather than a directional call. Max Pain at 24,100 (very close to spot) suggests near-term consolidation is the base case.

**4. DXY pulling back from its 15-month high (101.6 → ~101.1) even as it remains on track for a second consecutive monthly gain.** This is a genuinely two-sided signal for INR: the pullback from the peak is modestly INR-supportive, but the underlying monthly-gain trend confirms dollar strength is not simply a one-off spike — it is a persistent feature of Q2 2026 that India's capital-inflow package (FCNR(B), G-Sec exemption) has had to work against.

**5. India core CPI at 5.1% (up from 4.6%) is a notable divergence from the benign headline CPI (3.93-3.94%) narrative.** This is the first reading in the wiki's tracking to show core CPI running meaningfully *above* headline — a reversal of the "core CPI ex-gold/silver ~2.3%, giving RBI cover" framing used through most of June. If this core reading is confirmed (rather than a data/definitional discrepancy across sources), it would materially complicate the August RBI rate-cut thesis that has been building since mid-June. *Flagged for verification against the official MOSPI release — this may reflect a different core CPI methodology than the ex-gold/silver figure tracked previously.*

**Management:** Ashok Vaswani (MD & CEO), Devang Gheewalla (CFO), Jaideep Hansraj, Paritosh Kashyap, Anup Kumar Saha (Whole-time Directors)

## TL;DR

- Vaswani opened with macro context first: West Asia crisis + Russia-Ukraine + US tariffs = "enhanced volatility"; nil credit stress visible in bank book as of May 2, but watchful stance on lower-spectrum borrowers and monsoon
- Advances grew 16.2% avg YoY and 14.9% avg deposits YoY — stated target is 1.5x–2x nominal GDP growth; unsecured retail resumed gradual sequential growth (₹1,200 cr absolute in Q4 vs ₹517 cr in Q3)
- Credit cost trajectory shows clear improvement across FY26: Q1 93 bps → Q2 79 bps → Q3 63 bps → Q4 39 bps; management attributed to collection improvement in CV, MFI, credit cards
- Kotak811 (digital savings account) now 12.2% of total SA book, growing 32% YoY; Kotak is second-largest tractor financer (10.9% market share) but flagged below-normal IMD monsoon as a headwind

**Segment insights from transcript:**

- **SME and mortgage** grew >18% YoY — key drivers of Q4 advance growth
- **Unsecured portfolio** (PL, BL, credit card): unsecured mix stable at 8.9% of net advances; absolute unsecured grew ₹1,200 cr in Q4 (sequential resumption after slowdown)
- **Credit card**: portfolio flat sequentially; restacked with Solitaire/Air+/Cashback+ propositions
- **Tractor finance**: Kotak #2 with 10.9% market share; industry volume +35% YoY (GST cut + subsidies); collection efficiency improved; but IMD below-normal monsoon forecast for FY27 is a risk
- **Gold loan**: small but growing fast; plan to scale over next 18–24 months
- **Kotak811**: 250,000–300,000 new accounts/month; 12.2% of SA book; +32% YoY
- **Private Bank / HNI**: manages ~60% of India's top 100 families; combined relationship value ₹10.8 lakh cr (66,000 families)
- **Floating rate SA**: deliberately reduced by 30% YoY to lower cost of funds
- **Tech spend**: ~13% of total opex (FY26)
- **Infina Finance stake sale**: ₹1,094 cr standalone PAT (KMCC), ₹185 cr net at consolidated level

## Signals

**The credit cost improvement trajectory is a clean signal.** From 93 bps in Q1FY26 to 39 bps in Q4FY26 — this is a systemic normalization of the MFI/retail stress cycle, not a one-quarter event. The improvement in collection efficiency in CV, MFI, and credit cards suggests the NBFC sector stress (Sonata Finance merger into BSS Sonata Microcredit, now fully absorbed) has largely played through. Kotak's GNPA at 1.2% and net NPA at 0.25% are best-in-class for a large private bank.

**ROE in "high teens" remains aspirational — currently 10.8% standalone, 11.92% consolidated.** Vaswani's stated ambition. The path is: (a) NIM recovery as repo cut cycle ends, (b) operating leverage as cost-to-assets fell 27 bps to 2.75%, and (c) subsidiaries (insurance ROE 9.6%, AMC ROE 19.1%) adding ~2 points to group ROE. If Kotak compounds PAT at 12–15% with capital at 22% CET1, ROE expansion requires either accelerated loan growth or dividend/buyback discipline.

**West Asia war + below-normal monsoon = Kotak-specific risk vector.** Tractor finance (market #2, 10.9% share) and gold loan (scaling) are rural India plays. A below-normal El Niño monsoon in FY27 would pressure tractor collections and kisan segment. This is a H1FY27 watch point.

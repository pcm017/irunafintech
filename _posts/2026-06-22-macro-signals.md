## Signals

**Brent below $80 — India import bill math accelerates.** Brent at $79.20 is down ~$32/bbl from the $111 May peak. At current levels, India's annualised crude import savings vs. the conflict peak approach $90-100B. The supply trajectory toward $70-75 (the wiki's target range as Hormuz transit normalises) adds further relief. OMC profitability is restoring; August RBI rate cut probability elevated.

**Vance "skeptical" statement — caution flag, not reversal.** VP Vance's characterisation of Hormuz is a communications divergence from the June 19 deal, not a deal collapse signal. Pentagon's confirmation of "safe passage intact today" and the ~2.9M bbl/day transit rate confirm operational reality. The Vance statement is either (a) domestic messaging designed to claim US "deterrence" rather than Iranian concession, or (b) inaccurate. Watch MarineTraffic vessel transit data as ground truth over diplomatic language.

**India forex $700B — material upgrade in RBI optionality.** Crossing the $700B threshold is a structural positive: it restores the buffer from the $681B May trough and signals that the RBI's FCNR(B) capital inflow package is working. At $700B, India has ~15+ months of import cover — well above the 9-month threshold the IMF considers comfortable. RBI's ability to intervene to support INR without depleting reserves is meaningfully higher than it was six weeks ago.

**FII ₹-2.08L Cr YTD — structural drag, not panic.** The FII equity exit is now the single largest source of INR pressure. But the composition matters: the selling is concentrated in secondary markets (not primary issuance), suggesting it is portfolio rebalancing (EM underweight, US AI equity overweight) rather than India-specific fundamental concern. Once Hormuz reopening and rate cut trajectories are priced, FII re-entry into India equity could be a sharp reversal.

**US 10Y at 4.44% — Warsh's rate path credibility building.** The easing from 4.49% to 4.44% post-FOMC/Warsh statement is consistent with the Warsh "AI productivity bet" thesis: markets are beginning to price that a dovish new Fed chair can cut rates without reigniting inflation if Hormuz normalises and Brent declines. India-US spread at 236bps is above the 231bps June 15 trough — in the comfortable range for FII debt attraction.

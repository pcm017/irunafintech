# Tech & AI Digest — 2026-07-05

## TL;DR

- TSMC's 3nm lead times now exceed one year on AI-driven demand; Samsung Foundry is capturing overflow orders from Google, NVIDIA, Tesla, AMD, and BYD, while Apple is reportedly exploring Samsung (2nm, Texas) and Intel (18A-P) for its next-gen A21 SoC — the first real crack in TSMC's leading-edge near-monopoly.
- AMD and Qualcomm both unveiled new AI memory-packaging approaches (AMD's Memory-on-Package Versal Premium Gen 2; Qualcomm's "High Bandwidth Compute" AI250 platform) as chipmakers compete on packaging innovation rather than pure logic-node shrinks.
- India Semiconductor Mission 2.0 got a ₹1,000 Cr FY27 provision; the Union Cabinet approved two new projects (CRYSTAL MATRIX — Dholera compound-semiconductor fab + ATMP; SUCHI SEMICON — Surat OSAT), bringing the approved-project count context to 13 across seven states.
- A former NVIDIA silicon-photonics lead moved to ams OSRAM, raising the question of whether VCSEL-based optical designs could eventually challenge the InP-laser-dominated Co-Packaged Optics supply chain underpinning AI data-center scale-up.
- ByteByteGo's "Proof of Human" piece frames AI-agent identity verification (via World/Tools for Humanity's five-pillar framework: uniqueness, anonymity, recovery, verification, delegation) as an emerging infrastructure layer as automated agents increasingly compete with humans for scarce goods online.

## Key data / quotes

- AMD Versal Premium Gen 2: up to 32GB LPDDR5X per package, 288GB/s bandwidth; sampling end-2026, production H2 2027.
- Qualcomm AI250 rack-scale platform: commercial sampling targeted mid-2027.
- India Semiconductor Mission 2.0: ₹1,000 Cr FY27 provision; 13 projects approved across 7 states as of May 18, 2026 (one large logic/power fab, multiple OSAT/ATMP units, SiC/substrate projects) plus 2 newly Cabinet-approved (CRYSTAL MATRIX Dholera, SUCHI SEMICON Surat).
- Karnataka LEAP (Local Economy Accelerator Program): ₹1,000 Cr over five years, decentralising deep-tech/quantum innovation beyond Bengaluru.

## Signals

The Samsung/Intel foundry diversification story (Google, NVIDIA, Tesla, AMD, BYD reportedly hedging away from exclusive TSMC reliance, Apple exploring Samsung/Intel for A21) is the same crack-in-TSMC's-monopoly thread tracked in prior digests, now reinforced by named customers rather than just capacity-constraint framing. AMD and Qualcomm's simultaneous pivot to memory-packaging innovation (rather than node shrinks) signals that memory bandwidth, not raw compute density, is becoming the binding constraint for AI inference hardware — directly relevant to the Samsung AI-memory-shortage thesis tracked elsewhere in this wiki. India Semiconductor Mission 2.0's two new Cabinet approvals (Dholera, Surat) extend India's ATMP/OSAT-first strategy rather than competing at the advanced-node layer where the TSMC/Samsung/Intel battle is playing out.

# Tech & AI Digest — 2026-07-09

## TL;DR

- **Anthropic's autonomous, self-scheduling AI agents were reportedly replicated by AWS and Google within two weeks** — framed as the start of a race to own the "agent runtime" layer of AI infrastructure.
- **Michael Burry directly challenges Jensen Huang's "$1 trillion visibility" claim for Nvidia**, citing "94% unbuilt" data-center capacity and Jevons Paradox dynamics.
- **Samsung Foundry continues picking up Google/Nvidia/Tesla/AMD/BYD orders** as TSMC faces capacity strain; **Intel fell 21% in seven days** on a semiconductor-bubble warning plus 18A yield delays.
- **Micron, SanDisk, and Western Digital fell 7%** on July 7 after Samsung's earnings sparked a memory-sector sell-off, following a run of record quarters (Micron Q3 FY26 revenue $41.46B, +345.7% YoY).
- **Apple committing $30 billion to Broadcom chip purchases.**
- India Semiconductor Mission continues incremental progress: 13 approved projects (~₹1.64 lakh crore cumulative), Tata Dholera fab first silicon targeted December 2026, $92M raised by DLI-backed startups in 5 months.

## Signals

**Change detection:** The Anthropic-agent-copying story (AWS/Google within two weeks) signals the "agent runtime" layer is becoming commoditized far faster than the underlying model layer did — a compressed competitive-moat timeline worth tracking against Anthropic's broader enterprise positioning.

**Root cause traversal:** The memory-sector sell-off (Jul 7) is a valuation-correction after a run of blowout quarters (Micron +345.7% YoY revenue), not a demand-collapse signal — Burry's skepticism piece captures the broader "has AI capex outrun realizable demand" debate that's shaping this correction.

**Sizing / impact:** For India, the Samsung Foundry share gains and Intel's slide reinforce a foundry-market reshuffling that doesn't directly touch India's OSAT/ATMP-focused semiconductor strategy — India's mission remains on its steady, incremental delivery track (13 projects, ₹1.64 lakh crore) rather than being caught up in the leading-edge foundry competition.

# Paytm Q4 FY26 Earnings Release

**Released:** 6 May 2026 | **Earnings call:** 7 May 2026  
**Raw file:** [raw/results/Paytm_Earning-Release_Q4-FY2026-FY-2026.pdf](../../raw/results/Paytm_Earning-Release_Q4-FY2026-FY-2026.pdf)

---

## TL;DR

- **FY26 is Paytm's first full year of profit:** Revenue ₹8,437 Cr (+22% YoY), EBITDA ₹502 Cr (6% margin, a ₹2,008 Cr swing from -₹1,506 Cr in FY25), PAT ₹552 Cr (vs. -₹663 Cr in FY25).
- **Q4 FY26:** Revenue ₹2,264 Cr (+18% YoY reported, +26% comparable); EBITDA ₹132 Cr, EBITDA margin 6% — partially impacted by PIDF discontinuation; management achieved the guided 30-40% offset.
- **Merchant payments flywheel compounding:** Merchant GMV ₹6.5 Lakh Cr (+27% YoY), 1.51 Cr subscription merchants (+22% YoY), payment processing margin structurally above 4 bps.
- **Financial services distribution at scale:** FY26 revenue ₹2,594 Cr (+52% YoY); Q4 ₹750 Cr (+38% YoY); 7.5 Lakh key financial services customers (+36%).
- **Consumer UPI growing at 2.2x industry:** Consumer UPI GTV ₹5.5 Lakh Cr (+46% YoY), MTU 7.7 Cr (+50 Lakh YoY). Consistent market share gains every month for 12+ months.

## Signals

- **FY26 profitability inflection is genuine.** The ₹2,008 Cr EBITDA swing is not a one-quarter bump. Contribution margins at 58% FY26 vs 53% FY25, indirect expenses down 16% — the operating leverage story is playing out structurally, not just via one-time reductions.
- **Financial services distribution is the growth engine.** +52% YoY in FY26, now ₹2,594 Cr. Repeat borrowers >50% of merchant loan disbursements — this is a compounding annuity, not a lumpy origination business.
- **Payment margin expansion is structural.** Moving from "above 3 bps" (12 months ago) to "comfortably above 4 bps" is driven by credit-card-on-UPI and EMI instrument mix shift. Guided to continue expanding in FY27.
- **Asset-light model is a strategic choice, not a constraint.** Management explicitly closed the door on balance-sheet lending. This is a direct response to analysts pushing on NBFC route. Implication: Paytm's lending upside is bounded by lender appetite and partnership economics, not first-party balance sheet risk.
- **International expansion starting small.** PT Paytm Indonesia Teknologi incorporated April 10, 2026 with ₹8 Cr invested. Merchant payments stack being exported — low risk, optionality story.
- **PIDF wind-down absorbed.** ₹50 Cr in Q4 FY25 → ₹10 Cr in Q4 FY26. The 30-40% offset guidance was achieved. Marketing services continues to decline (payment aggregator guidelines impact) — this line is a drag.

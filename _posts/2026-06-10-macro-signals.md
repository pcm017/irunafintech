## Signals

- **Mag 7 derating continues; S&P 493 rotation is now a confirmed theme.** The broader market (S&P 493 ex-Mag 7) has been outperforming Big Tech YTD going into June 9. This represents a structural shift away from the concentrated AI-stock rally that drove 2024–2025 gains. India's IT sector should watch this carefully: a broad market rotation reduces the premium placed on AI-adjacent growth names.
- **India G-Sec 10Y at ~6.9% — below 7% for second time recently.** The compression to 6.9% signals bond market relief from lower crude expectations (Iran ceasefire signal + oil easing). India-US spread at ~233bps is tightening — lower carry reduces FII bond flow attractiveness, but the FII G-Sec tax exemption ordinance partially compensates. Watch whether the spread holds at 233bps or compresses further toward 220bps.
- **Brent below $94 — partial ceasefire signal is the dominant macro catalyst.** If Iran-Israel mutual halt becomes durable and Hormuz normalises, Brent at $75–85 is the India-relief scenario. Every $5/bbl decline in Brent = ~$3B/month reduction in India's import bill. India's oil stocks of 76–80 days provide a buffer — India has time to wait for the diplomatic resolution.
- **BHAVYA scheme (₹33,660Cr for 100 industrial parks) and Tejas Mk1A delivery** signal continued domestic infrastructure and defence spending despite macro stress — fiscal policy is expansionary even as RBI stays on hold.

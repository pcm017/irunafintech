## TL;DR

- Global equities sold off sharply on May 15: S&P −1.24%, Nasdaq −1.54%, DAX −2.07%, FTSE −1.71% — driven by rising bond yields (US 10Y 4.58%), tech weakness, and no US-China policy breakthrough.
- Oil surged again: Brent $109.26 (+3.35%), WTI $105.42 (+4.20%) — Strait of Hormuz closure compounded by stalled US-Iran peace negotiations.
- Metals diverge hard: Gold −2.63% ($4,561), Silver −9.12%, Copper −4.79% — energy risk premium UP, safe-haven metals DOWN, industrial metals DOWN. Unusual combination.
- India: Nifty 23,643 (▼0.19%), petrol Delhi hiked ₹3/L to ₹97.77 — energy cost pass-through now visible. INR in record-low zone (~₹89–91 per USD in this data series, consistent with prior ATL prints).

## Signals

1. **Hormuz + Iran = sustained oil elevation.** Brent $109+ with WTI above $105 — stalled US-Iran negotiations mean no near-term relief. Energy inflation is now flowing through to retail (Delhi petrol +₹3/L, CNG +₹2/kg). India's CPI 3.48% (April) hasn't yet captured this; May/June CPI will be the test.

2. **Equities and bonds both selling off together** — the classic "risk-off but not flight-to-safety" pattern, where rising yields drive both equity multiple compression AND bond losses. Unusual: typically equities and bonds anti-correlate in recessions. Current pattern (equity + bond selloff) = inflationary shock pricing, not growth shock.

3. **Metals divergence is a strong signal.** Energy risk premium ↑ + safe-haven and industrial metals ↓ = market pricing "Hormuz-driven supply disruption" specifically, not a broad commodity supercycle. This is consistent with COT data showing gold positioning hasn't confirmed the price move — smart money is not chasing gold at these levels.

4. **India fuel price hike = government signals Hormuz is structural.** The ₹3/L petrol hike and ₹2/kg CNG hike are the first domestic fuel cost pass-throughs. Combined with gold import duty 6%→15%, these are government signals that Hormuz disruption is being treated as a sustained rather than transient event.

5. **Nifty holding relatively well** (−0.19% May 18) vs. Europe (−2%) and US (−1.2%). India's domestic consumption story and contained CPI providing some insulation, but Bank Nifty (−0.77%) and FMCG (−1.81%) show sector-level stress.

# Tech & AI Digest — 2026-07-18

## TL;DR

- **Moonshot AI's Kimi K3 model** entered the frontier-model conversation and is cited as a contributing factor in the Jul 17 US/Asia semiconductor-led equity sell-off — a Chinese model release denting US AI-trade sentiment directly.
- **TSMC raised 2026 capex guidance from $52-56B to $60-64B**, disclosed additional detail on its $100Bn Arizona commitment (Fab 21 Phase 2 construction complete ahead of schedule, equipment move-in Q3 2026); leading-edge wafer demand now running at 110-120% of available capacity.
- **NVIDIA tightened export-compliance "whitelisting"** for Asian customers (Singapore, Malaysia, Japan) on Jul 14 — more than half of past customers reportedly failed recent evaluations; **Qualcomm is designing China-specific data-center chips** to comply with export curbs.
- **India Semicon 2.0 (₹1,27,500 Cr) and a new Mobile Phone Manufacturing Scheme (₹62,500 Cr)** were formally detailed by Union Cabinet — six-pillar structure (design, machinery/materials, new fabs, ATMP/OSAT, R&D, talent), largely corroborating the Jul 15/16 Cabinet approval already logged.

## Key data / quotes

- "Semis shed a trillion dollars in under two months while demand data kept breaking records." — The AI Corner, framing the ongoing semiconductor sell-off as a valuation/rotation story, not a demand collapse.
- TSMC: additional $100B Arizona investment (4+ new 2nm-and-below plants plus advanced packaging), total US investment now $265B; 2026 capex guidance raised to $60-64B (from $52-56B) citing surging AI chip demand; Fab 21 Phase 2 completed construction ahead of schedule, equipment move-in planned Q3 2026; 2nm yields reported >70% in volume production.
- Samsung Foundry reported in orders/negotiations with Google, NVIDIA, Tesla, AMD, and BYD for sub-5nm nodes; Samsung 4nm capacity filling on HBM4 demand. Intel 18A stuck at 50-55% yields; Intel 14A reportedly slipped beyond 2030.
- NVIDIA (Jul 14): new "whitelist" compliance system for Asian customers to prevent chip rerouting to China via third parties — more than half of past customers reportedly failed recent evaluations; scrutiny targets Singapore, Malaysia, Japan (contract validation + end-user interviews). Follows the Commerce Department's 2026-05-31 rule requiring export licenses for China/Macau-headquartered entities.
- Qualcomm: announced plans to design China-specific data-center chips engineered to comply with US export curbs.
- India: Union Cabinet approved Semicon 2.0 (₹1,27,500 Cr, six pillars: chip design, machinery/materials, new fabs, ATMP/OSAT, R&D, talent development) and a separate Mobile Phone Manufacturing Scheme (₹62,500 Cr). To date, 12 semiconductor manufacturing projects worth >₹1.64 lakh crore approved (1 silicon fab, 1 SiC fab, 1 GaN micro-LED fab, 9 ATMP/OSAT units); Micron, Kaynes, CG Semi already in commercial production. 24 MSME/startup chip-design projects funded; EDA tools extended to 105 startups/MSMEs.

## Signals

**Change detection:** Today's genuinely new granular detail is on the supply side of the AI-hardware buildout: TSMC's capex raise ($52-56B → $60-64B) and 110-120%-of-capacity wafer demand figure are the clearest quantified confirmation yet that leading-edge fab capacity, not chip design, is the binding constraint — distinct from (and a possible counter-narrative to) the "AI trade lost $1 trillion" valuation-rotation framing dominating the same day's equity commentary.

**Root cause:** The Jul 17 US/Asia equity sell-off traces to two compounding factors — Moonshot AI's Kimi K3 release denting confidence in US AI-lab moats, and continued Iran-driven macro risk-off — layering on top of, not replacing, the multi-week semiconductor valuation unwind already tracked (chip-selloff-jul7, kospi-chip-rout-jul16).

**Sizing/impact:** NVIDIA's tightened export whitelist (>50% of past Asian customers failing evaluation) is a meaningful tightening beyond the existing China-headquartered-entity rule — it extends enforcement scrutiny to transshipment risk via allied/neutral jurisdictions (Singapore, Malaysia, Japan), which could pressure Q3 revenue in those geographies. India's Semicon 2.0/MPMS combined ₹1.9 lakh crore electronics push (consistent with the Jul 17 collect note) remains the largest single India-industrial-policy commitment tracked in this wiki; today's cabinet-level detail is corroborating, not incremental to the underlying figure already logged to *modi*.

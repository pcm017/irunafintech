# Fintech & BFSI Digest — 2026-07-14

## TL;DR

- Top private banks collectively cut 7,700+ employees in FY26 — corroborates and extends the HDFC Bank (8,000+) and Kotak Mahindra Bank (1,269) workforce-reduction figures already logged Jul 13; PSU Indian Bank is separately hiring 20 AI experts, a private-vs-PSU hiring divergence worth tracking.
- SBI Mutual Fund (SBI Funds Management) is reportedly moving toward an IPO — first mention in the wiki.
- SEBI introduced stricter service rules and investment norms for its own employees, framed alongside a DPDPA/RBI/SEBI workforce-security compliance webinar.
- A Value Research analysis found flexi-cap funds "hugging large caps" despite unrestricted mandates; 11 international funds were closed to new SIPs this week, leaving only one (Baroda BNP Paribas Aqua) open.
- Circle obtained a US bank charter (corroborating the Circle/Klarna/Sony charter race logged Jul 13); major US banks are reportedly seeking access to Fiserv's payment rails; Equifax acquired a business/stake in Mexico.

## Key data / quotes

- "Top private banks reduce over 7,700 employees in FY26" — ETBFSI headline; corroborates HDFC Bank (8,000+) and Kotak Mahindra Bank (1,269) cuts logged 2026-07-13.
- Indian Bank (PSU) hiring 20 AI experts — same digest cycle as the private-bank cuts, a hiring-vs-firing divergence between private and PSU banks.
- HDFC Bank's highest-paid executives in FY26 disclosed (ETHRWorld) — follow-up to the bank's already-logged workforce-reduction story.
- "Flexi-cap funds have the most open mandate in the industry: go anywhere, hold anything, chase returns across the whole market... That freedom is exactly why investors have poured more money into this category than any other in the last three years" — Value Research/Fundline, investigating why average flexi-cap funds "hug large caps only" despite the unrestricted mandate.
- PGIM, Franklin, and Edelweiss reportedly closed 11 international funds to new SIPs this week; only Baroda BNP Paribas Aqua remains open.
- India June CPI (4.38%, up from 3.93% May) independently corroborated a second and third time via ETCFO and ETRetail newsletters (food and transport cited as drivers) — consistent with the same print sourced in today's macro-signals collect.

## Signals

The 7,700+ private-bank FY26 workforce cut figure, alongside Indian Bank's simultaneous AI-expert hiring push, sharpens a private-vs-PSU divergence theme this wiki has been tracking since HDFC Bank and Kotak Mahindra Bank's individual disclosures on Jul 13 — private banks are net-shedding headcount even as at least one PSU bank builds out AI capability, suggesting the AI-driven restructuring is landing unevenly across the sector rather than uniformly. SBI Mutual Fund's reported IPO move would add a third SBI-group listing thread to the wiki (alongside SBI Life and SBI Cards) if it materialises — worth a follow-up fetch. The flexi-cap "closet indexing" finding is a modest wealthtech-adjacent governance signal: if fund managers are systematically underusing unrestricted mandates, it raises questions about fee justification precisely as India's mutual fund AUM continues to compound via SIP flows.

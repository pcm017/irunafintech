## Key Signal Details

### Apple WWDC — Siri Delivers (Finally)
- Siri rebuilt with AI (confirmed working at WWDC June 2026)
- Tim Cook's final WWDC as CEO (CEO transition underway)
- Not state-of-the-art AI, but functional and integrated into iPhone's 2.5B device installed base
- Strategic read: Apple's moat is distribution + ecosystem + privacy-first positioning, not model quality. A working Siri = Apple stays relevant in AI era even without leading model capability.
- Separate: Apple evaluating Intel Foundry + Samsung Foundry for A21 chip production (TSMC supply constraint)

### Anthropic Fable 5 — Guardrails Controversy
- Public release of Anthropic's Mythos model branded as "Fable 5"
- Visible guardrails: cybersecurity and biology topics restricted
- Silent nerfing: LLM creation capabilities suppressed without announcement
- Community outcry → nerfing reversed Thursday (2 days after release)
- Ben Thompson analysis: This behaviour was predictable — Anthropic's mission-driven culture means it can act unilaterally when it believes it's right
- TCS partnership (50K employees on Claude) confirmed publicly on June 12 — same week as Fable 5 launch

### SpaceX IPO — Largest in History
- IPO date: June 12, 2026; Nasdaq ticker SPCX
- Opening price: $135/share; raised $75B; valuation $1.75T
- Surpasses Saudi Aramco 2019 ($29B raised) as largest IPO ever
- At $1.75T, SpaceX is ~7th largest US company by market cap (above Tesla ~$1.6T)
- Revenue streams: Starlink broadband, Falcon 9 commercial launches, Starship (dev), Google compute deal
- Nvidia Vera CPU: OpenAI, Anthropic, and SpaceX confirmed as early customers — the three companies are deeply linked at compute layer
- **India/Jio implication**: SpaceX IPO success renews investor appetite for large-cap tech listings → Jio Platforms (telecom + digital payments + streaming + cloud) IPO expected H2 2026 could pull forward

### TCS-Anthropic Partnership
- TCS equipping 50,000 employees with Claude
- Formal enterprise AI partnership announced
- Positioning: TCS = Anthropic's enterprise delivery channel for BFSI, healthcare, manufacturing
- Competitive angle: Microsoft + OpenAI vs. TCS + Anthropic for large regulated-industry clients
- Anthropic's safety-first positioning (vs. OpenAI's commercial-first pivot) suits large regulated-industry clients
- Note: TCS's Q4 FY26 earnings call (April) had hinted at an Anthropic partnership "to be announced in the near future" — this is the fulfilment

### TSMC Supply Bottleneck — Diversification Accelerating
- TSMC: 72% global foundry market share; 11x Samsung Foundry (6.5% share)
- AI chip demand has pushed TSMC capacity to critical constraint
- Google considering Samsung for 10th-gen TPU production (TSMC supply locked up by Nvidia, Apple, others)
- Apple considering Intel Foundry + Samsung Foundry for A21 chip (TSMC unable to accommodate)
- Samsung launched HBM4 memory this week (tightening AI accelerator supply grip)
- TSMC A16 (1.4nm) in production roadmap confirmed; 1.4nm and below timelines published by all three major foundries
- India implication: TSMC bottleneck = more urgency for alternative supply chains; India ATMP/OSAT positioning becomes more strategic

### NVIDIA — PC Silicon + Data Centre Dominance
- RTX Spark / N1X: Arm-based Windows PC chip debut (Computex June 1, 2026)
  - Compete with Qualcomm Snapdragon X Elite; Qualcomm stock fell on announcement
  - AMD reportedly developing Arm-based response
  - Available fall 2026 on Microsoft, Dell, HP, ASUS, Lenovo, MSI devices
- Vera CPU: full production; early customers confirmed — OpenAI, Anthropic, SpaceX
- Strategic read: Nvidia expanding from GPU/AI accelerator to full-stack silicon (CPU + GPU + NPU)

### India Semiconductor Mission 2.0
- Union Budget 2026–27: Rs 8,000 Cr allocation (largest single-year semiconductor outlay)
- 13 projects approved across 7 states (ISM + SPECS)
- Focus: semiconductor equipment + materials, full-stack Indian IP, supply chain resilience
- NITI Aayog released India Semiconductor Industry Roadmap (June 1, 2026)
- Investors Support Portal launched by MeitY (June 1, 2026)
- Key players (Tata Electronics, CG Power, Micron Sanand) — operational progress unchanged this week
- G7 France (June 16–17): chip export controls on agenda; any new ASML restrictions would be market-moving for India semiconductor strategy

### EU Chips Act — Declared a Failure
- Asianometry (Jon Yu) at ITF World 2026 declares EU Chips Act failed
- EU targeted 20% global chip production by 2030 — trajectory off-track
- Anchor tenants (TSMC Dresden, Intel Germany) focused on mature nodes, not advanced silicon
- ASML facing capacity + geopolitical export restriction constraints
- India's ATMP-first approach assessed as "more defensible" (noted in prior wiki context)

### India Industrial AI Data Center Supply Chain — $48B Uplift
- Indian industrial companies supplying data center components (transformers, cooling systems, cables) have gained ~$48B in market value
- AI infrastructure buildout (primarily outside India) drawing on Indian industrial supply chains
- Signal: India's capital goods sector has a structural AI infrastructure tailwind even as India itself lacks advanced fab capability

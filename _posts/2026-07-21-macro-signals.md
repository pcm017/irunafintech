## Signals

**Change detection:** The MGNREGA repeal is a genuine structural break — India's rural employment-guarantee backbone since 2005 has been replaced by a new law (VB-GRAM G) with no person-days data yet available under the new scheme, unlike the routine market-data noise that dominates the rest of this cycle.

**Root cause:** Not stated in the raw collect (subject-line/summary level only); the "Viksit Bharat" branding suggests this sits inside the government's broader rural-development rebranding push rather than a reaction to a specific fiscal or political trigger — needs a dedicated follow-up source to establish the substantive policy difference (eligibility, wage guarantee days, funding mechanism) versus the old MGNREGA framework.

**Sizing / impact:** MGNREGA is one of the largest rural welfare programmes globally by beneficiary count; a formal repeal/replacement — even if VB-GRAM G preserves similar guarantees under a new name — is a labor-market and rural-consumption data point worth tracking closely, since it removes MGNREGA person-days (a widely used informal rural-distress indicator) from future data series. Separately, the sharply conflicting CMIE unemployment reads (7.70% overall vs. the 5.5%/6.6% figures logged Jul 20) and the fresh 3-way India forex-reserves conflict are both unresolved — treated as data-quality flags, not fresh macro deterioration, until a primary source reconciles them.

## TL;DR

- PAT ₹137.02 bn (+8.5% YoY) for Q4; FY26 PAT ₹501.47 bn (+6.2% YoY) — steady compounding but below the 15–18% growth pace of FY24
- NIM held at 4.32% despite full-quarter impact of Dec repo cut, as deposit repricing and mix improvement offset the rate headwind; margin described as "range-bound" going forward
- Credit cost collapsed to ₹0.96 bn in Q4 (0.03% of avg advances), driven by lower retail unsecured slippages + higher corporate recoveries; FY26 credit cost 38 bps vs guidance of sub-50 bps
- Loan growth accelerated to 15.8% YoY / 6.0% QoQ with broad-based drivers: rural/gold loans +18% QoQ, business banking +24.4% YoY, mortgages resuming after a pause
- Management flagged West Asia conflict uncertainty but stated no visible credit stress yet; business activity normal

## Signals

**NIM held — but growth in repo-linked book is a sword of Damocles.** 56% of domestic loans are repo-linked; any further cuts flow through immediately. Management guided "range-bound" NIM — not expanding. The comfort comes from deposit repricing: term deposits locked at higher peak rates (~summer 2025) will reprice lower over the next 12 months.

**Credit quality surprise: net credit cost 3 bps in Q4.** Two drivers — unsecured retail slippage normalization (PL/credit card delinquency peak has passed) and corporate write-back recoveries. The FY26 credit cost of 38 bps is materially below the sub-50 bps guidance. ICICI is building a large provisioning buffer (₹227 bn total, ₹131 bn contingency) — leaving room to absorb West Asia tail risk without P&L impact.

**Gold and rural loan surge (+18% QoQ) warrants watching.** Sharp jump in rural book suggests demand from kisan/gold loan customers. This segment carries higher delinquency risk if monsoon underperforms (IMD already forecasting below-normal FY27 monsoon). ICICI continues to hold an RBI-directed additional provision of ₹12.83 bn on agri priority sector portfolio.

**Credit card contraction (-5.6% YoY) is an industry story, not just ICICI.** Revolvers declining system-wide as borrowers deleverage. ICICI said cards remain "very profitable" with multiple levers but acknowledged this area needs work on fees. Connects to the broader RBI-engineered slowdown in unsecured credit.

**Opex growing ahead of revenue (+12% vs +8% NII).** Management cited Labour Code, priority sector compliance costs, and March equity market MTM as one-offs. Guide is opex below top-line growth in FY27 — to be watched.

## Key Data / Quotes

### Claude Code / Anthropic
- **Claude Code $2.5B ARR in year one** — Anthropic's fastest-growing revenue product. Key design insights (Head of Design interview): transition from chat to agentic workflows, developer trust-building, designing for probabilistic AI outputs, the role of "taste" in AI product design.
- Anthropic IPO (context from prior source pages): confidential SEC filing at $965B valuation / $47B ARR.

### India Government AI / Tech Policy
- **MeitY AI deployment:** TCS, NEC India, Kyndryl, and 3 others selected to deploy AI across government ministries for public service delivery, data analytics, and government operations. Moves IndiaAI Mission from policy to execution.
- **₹3,000 crore lithium/nickel PLI:** PLI-style incentive for lithium and nickel processing to boost EV supply chain. Minimum annual capacity: 30,000 metric tonnes. Direct industrial policy response to China's dominance in critical mineral processing.
- **FTAs operational in 10 months:** FTAs with 9 nations (covering 38 developed economies) to be operational within 10 months; 4 more trade pacts in pipeline (Commerce Minister Piyush Goyal).
- **UP Cabinet:** Cleared 1,725 e-buses; amended semiconductor policy.
- **Karnataka:** State government positioning Bangalore as hub for deeptech startups, quantum computing research, and semiconductor design — design-led strategy aligned with India Semiconductor Mission.
- **Kuku FM IPO:** Kuku Technologies Ltd has filed confidential SEBI IPO papers. India's leading audio storytelling/podcast platform — signals continued India consumer internet listing pipeline.

### Modi / Geopolitics
- **Venezuela oil diplomacy:** PM Modi held talks with Venezuela's Acting President Delcy Rodriguez. India exploring increase in Venezuelan crude imports (discounted due to US sanctions). Deepening India-Venezuela energy ties.

### AI Models / Products
- **BYD humanoid robotics:** Chinese EV giant formally announced humanoid robots programme — tapping manufacturing scale, battery tech, and motor expertise. Represents major new entrant in physical AI. Sam Altman has previously undisclosed investment in a robotics startup.
- **Reve / Ideogram:** AI image generation entering new phase — prompt-to-layout generation (spatial arrangement, text placement, design structure following user wireframes). Relevant for advertising creative automation.
- **Emergent "invisible users":** AI companies where the primary "users" are other AI systems or automated workflows rather than humans. Revenue without proportionate human user growth — a new pattern in AI-era software economics.

### AI Strategy / Viewpoints
- **A16Z mission statement update:** "Help not just founders, but America and her allies" — explicitly aligned with US geopolitical and technological leadership. Signals A16Z's portfolio increasingly oriented toward US defence tech, AI policy advocacy, and anti-regulation-that-slows-US-competitiveness.
- **Scott Galloway (via The AI Corner):** "95% of enterprise AI spend connects to no return a CFO can name." Dot-com era parallel: FOMO and competitive necessity driving spend rather than ROI. Key nuance: unlike 2000, the infrastructure being built (compute, data, models) has real value — question is whether the applications layer justifies current valuations.
- **Age of Extraction Part 2 (Token Dispatch):** AI intensifies extraction — personalisation loops, synthetic relationship simulation, commodification of human attention at finer resolution.
- **AI Internet / Attention economy:** "Attention is no longer scarce" — AI agents consume and act on information autonomously, shifting the constraint from human attention to compute and inference capacity. Changes monetisation models and platform economics.

### Chinese Tech / Global Macro
- **Chinese internet valuation compression:** Combined market cap of Alibaba + Tencent + Baidu + JD + Meituan + PDD has compressed so dramatically that Micron Technology alone could theoretically buy the entire sector. Causes: CCP 2021 tech crackdown aftermath, geopolitical risk premium, US investor withdrawal, AI capex spending despite compressed valuations.

### Chips / Hardware
- **MicroLED + memory convergence:** All three major memory makers (Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron) monitoring MicroLED technology — convergence of display optics and memory architecture. Optical interconnects inside chips moving from research to near-term roadmap as AI inference pushes memory bandwidth beyond electrical path limits.
- **TextQL:** AI-native data intelligence layer for enterprise (natural language queries against Snowflake/Databricks without custom pipelines). Positioned between BI tools and AI coding assistants — relevant for BFSI data analysis use cases.

## Signals

1. **Claude Code $2.5B in year one** — reinforces Anthropic's ARR trajectory ($47B total). Agentic coding has crossed from developer experiment to core enterprise product. India's IT services sector (TCS, Infosys, TechM) must reorient delivery models around agent-assisted coding at scale.
2. **India's AI in government is now vendor-execution stage** — MeitY vendor selection marks transition from policy frameworks (IndiaAI Mission) to implementation. TCS wins further consolidate its position as the default Indian government tech integrator.
3. **₹3,000 crore lithium/nickel PLI** — India directly challenging China's critical minerals processing dominance. Combined with semiconductor mission and EV push, India is building an industrial policy stack for the electrification and AI hardware era.
4. **Venezuelan oil deepening** — India is diversifying crude sources toward sanctioned suppliers (Russia, Venezuela) for discount pricing. This carries a geopolitical cost (US disapproval) but is economically rational given India's current account pressure.
5. **BYD humanoid robots** — Largest EV manufacturer entering humanoid robotics. BYD's advantage: cost-optimised manufacturing + battery + motor expertise. This creates a Chinese hardware platform that could challenge Boston Dynamics / Figure / Unitree in mid-tier price segments.
6. **Chinese internet value destruction** — The scale of Chinese tech market cap compression is remarkable. For India: this creates a talent migration opportunity (Chinese engineers seeking non-China AI roles), and a template for what regulatory risk can do to tech valuations.
7. **Scott Galloway AI ROI warning** — Distinct from "AI is a bubble" argument: infrastructure has real value, but the applications layer may be overvalued. For Indian IT: companies that can demonstrate AI-driven productivity quantification (like TechM's Project Fortius EBIT expansion) will be rewarded; those that cannot face valuation pressure.

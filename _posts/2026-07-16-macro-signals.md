**Change detection:** Brent's 3rd consecutive up session to $85.44 confirms the Jul 14 naval-blockade escalation is holding, not fading — this is now a sustained multi-day trend rather than a single spike. WPI's jump to 9.87% (from 9.68%) is the first wholesale-inflation data point to move *against* the wiki's post-Iran-deal WPI-CPI convergence thesis; if this WPI/CPI combination (WPI 9.87%, CPI 4.38%) persists through the naval-blockade window, it materially narrows RBI's August 3-5 MPC room.

**Root cause traversal:** The driver is explicit and named — Trump's formal Congressional notification of Iran war resumption, following a third consecutive night of US strikes. This is a step up in formality from the "ceasefire breakdown" framing used since Jul 8: a war-resumption notification is a legal/procedural escalation, not just a market narrative.

**Sizing / impact:** At $85.44, Brent sits just below the Jul 14 close of $86.35 but above every other read since the Jul 8 breakdown began — the blockade is structurally holding oil in the mid-$80s. Combined with WPI at 9.87%, India's wholesale-to-consumer inflation pipeline is now the primary swing risk into the August MPC, offsetting the constructive bank-credit and FII-buying signals tracked since Jul 9. The bank-credit growth read (18.6% YoY, full-month June) needs reconciliation against the existing 17.7% fortnight figure before treating it as a fresh acceleration — flagged, not resolved.

## Signals

- **India-US divergence continues:** S&P at 9th weekly gain while Nifty is −10% YTD — structural gap driven by Hormuz oil shock hitting India disproportionately
- **Brent −17% in May** is the most significant monthly decline since 2020 — if the US-Iran deal confirms, India's macro picture reverses materially (trade deficit, INR, OMC losses all ease)
- **MSCI rebalancing** caused Nifty's final-hour crash — separate from macro; India was likely removed from some MSCI weightings amid sustained FII outflows
- **Consumption bifurcation:** Premium India booming (Apple, Blinkit, foreign travel) while base India faces rising rents, fuel, EMI burden; this is structurally relevant for credit quality — entry-level consumption stress ≠ aggregate consumption story
- **Goldman hiking calls:** If RBI hikes again, spreads compress further and INR benefits short-term but housing/consumer lending costs rise

## Signals

- **RBIH DPIP = next DPI layer after UPI/Aadhaar/DigiLocker**. If DPIP launches as a platform-layer above existing DPI rails, it could be the infrastructure that enables government-embedded finance, consent-based data sharing at institutional scale, and financial services for the unserved. Sahil Kini framing it signals this is in active development, not conceptual. Watch: what use cases DPIP enables that existing AA/UPI/e-RUPI cannot.
- **NSE IPO: size and timing signal**: With four major law firms engaged, NSE IPO is moving beyond concept. NSE's listing would be among India's largest-ever (NSE FY26 PAT ~₹10,000Cr+). The SEBI regulatory revamp (June 20) — streamlining capital markets rules — may have been partly in preparation for this listing. India's capital markets infrastructure itself going public is a structural market deepening event.
- **Stablecoin economy institutionalising**: BIS acknowledging stablecoin remuneration is a formal recognition that stablecoins are not temporary; they are competing with bank deposits for yield-seeking capital. For India: RBI's perpetual payment authorisation framework + potential DPIP integration = India's regulatory response to stablecoins needs to factor in yield economics, not just payment rails.
- **India services AI transformation**: Fintech Inside analysis ("quality and speed factory") — the structural argument that AI removes India's cost-only advantage and opens a capability advantage. For fintech/BFSI: India's talent pool (largest pool of engineers and finance professionals outside the US) could access global financial services workflows at AI-native speed. This is the bull case for India IT services that's been asserted; what's new is AI now makes quality and speed simultaneously achievable.
- **Insurance sector health**: ICICI Lombard and Niva Bupa leading premium growth = health insurance the structural winner in India's post-Covid insurance premium uptake. Consistent with IRDAI's push for expanded coverage.

## TL;DR

- Book (AUM) grew to ₹1,29,634 Cr (+27% YoY, +6% QoQ), tracking ahead of the "Lakshya 2031" 20%+ book-growth goal; disbursements ₹23,852 Cr (+36% YoY, -1% QoQ).
- PAT ₹902 Cr (+29% YoY, +12% QoQ); ROA 2.48% (+11bps YoY, +8bps QoQ) and ROE 12.71% (+185bps YoY, +100bps QoQ) — both still below the FY31 targets (3.0-3.2% ROA, 16-18% ROE) but converging.
- Credit cost improved to 2.54% (after macro-prudential utilization, down from 2.64% Q4FY26; before utilization also 2.54%, since no macro-prudential buffer was drawn this quarter, vs 3.43% pre-utilization a year ago).
- Retail asset quality stable: Stage 3 (GNPA-equivalent) 2.48% of retail book (down from 2.93% a year ago), Net Stage 3 0.77% (down from 0.83%), PCR on Stage 3 69.62%.
- Company describes itself as an "AI-native Retail Financial Services Institution" pursuing a "risk-first, tech-first" operating credo under its Lakshya 2031 strategic plan (unveiled April 2026).

## Signals

L&T Finance's book growth (27% YoY) running well ahead of its own 20%+ Lakshya 2031 target, alongside improving credit cost and stable-to-improving retail asset quality, signals the NBFC retail-lending cycle remains healthy even as the wiki has separately tracked signs of stress elsewhere in the NBFC space (the 600%+ interest-rate NBFC investigation logged 2026-07-13). The QoQ NIM dip (8.54% from 8.78%) alongside rising leverage (D/E 4.09x, up from 3.93x) is the one metric worth monitoring — if it continues, it would suggest LTF's growth is increasingly leverage-funded rather than purely margin-accretive, which would slow convergence toward its 16-18% FY31 ROE target rather than accelerate it.

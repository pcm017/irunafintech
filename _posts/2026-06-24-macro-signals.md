## Signals

- **Iran 60-day oil license → WTI at $73.15:** The US granting Iran a 60-day licence to sell oil marks an acceleration in the supply-return trade from the US-Iran peace deal signed June 19. At $73.15, India's annualised crude import savings vs. the May $111 WTI peak now exceed $130B. Every $1/bbl further decline saves India ~$1.5B/year on imports — the CAD improvement and INR strength trajectory is intact but FII outflows (-₹43K Cr MTD) continue to weigh on INR.
- **India decoupling:** Nifty +0.37% on a day when Nasdaq fell 2.21% and Nikkei fell 3.55% is a meaningful signal. India's crude-import relief and domestic macro resilience (CPI 3.93%, bank credit 17.1%) are partially offsetting global risk-off pressure.
- **Gold $4,123 (-1.89%):** The DXY + Iran supply return combination is compressing both energy and safe-haven commodities simultaneously — an unusual dual headwind for gold. With WTI now at $73, the inflation narrative that supported gold through the conflict period is structurally weakening.
- **FTSE rejig ~$600M passive inflow:** Programmatic passive inflows at rebalancing provide a mechanical support for Nifty and INR in late June — timing coincides with the period FII equity outflows have been heaviest.
- **Kunal Shah → WhatsApp:** Meta's $900M investment in CRED and Shah's transition to lead WhatsApp globally is India's most significant fintech-to-big-tech executive crossover. WhatsApp Pay has 500M+ users in India but has underperformed BHIM UPI in transaction share; Shah's CRED background (credit-led engagement, data monetisation) could structurally shift WhatsApp Pay's India strategy.

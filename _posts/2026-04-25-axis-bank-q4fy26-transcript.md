# Axis Bank Q4 FY26 Earnings Conference Call Transcript

**Raw file:** [raw/results/q4fy26-earnings-call.pdf](../../raw/results/q4fy26-earnings-call.pdf)

**Management on call:** Amitabh Chaudhry (MD & CEO), Puneet Sharma (CFO), Subrat Mohanty (ED), Munish Sharda (ED), Neeraj Gambhir (ED), Vijay Mulbagal (Wholesale Banking)

---

## TL;DR

- Management clarified two one-time Q4 items that net to zero on P&L: ₹2,193 Cr tax credit (Citi intangibles DTA) and ₹2,001 Cr voluntary standard asset provision for West Asia macro tail risk.
- NIM through-cycle target of 3.80% remains firm; expected 15–18 months from last rate cut transmission. Full 125 bps rate cut now fully in EBLR book (61% of loans are repo-linked). Residual TD repricing tailwind remains.
- Wholesale corporate growth (+38% YoY) is deliberate and RAROC-neutral — sectors: renewables, commercial real estate, data centres, NBFCs (PSL), manufacturing. No credit curve dilution; 91% of book rated A− and above.
- Technical slippages normalised to negligible levels — will discontinue separate disclosure from Q1 FY27. Management confident of no economic loss on technical NPA pool.
- AI roadmap (AXIOM): only ISO 42001-certified bank globally; "meaningful bottom-line impact" expected over next 18–24 months.

---

## Key Data / Quotes

**On NIM and rate cycle:**
> "We will get to through-cycle 3.80%, 15 to 18 months from transmission of last rate cut. That's a consistent comment we've offered." — Puneet Sharma, CFO

> "Yields on interest earning assets declined 5 bps QOQ, cost of funds were largely flat QOQ. The Bank maintains its through cycle stance of NIMs at 3.80%." — CFO opening remarks

**On the ₹2,001 Cr provision:**
> "The adverse stress scenario assumes average oil over US$150 for 12 months, inflation at 7.4% and currency depreciating by ~20% over current levels. Based on our current assessment, this provision is considered sufficient to absorb potential incremental provisioning charge to P&L even under the most adverse stress scenario modelled for FY27." — Puneet Sharma

> "Yes, we will draw down on these provisions in the event we see an impact on the P&L in FY27." — Puneet Sharma (confirming the provision is not floating but against an identified pool)

**On wholesale growth:**
> "We selectively grow and we are not chasing growth here. We invest in sectors with the strongest cycles and clear micro tailwinds. Incrementally, growth was seen in power (largely renewables), commercial real estate, data centres, NBFCs largely PSL driven, and manufacturing." — Vijay Mulbagal, Wholesale Banking

> "RAROCs that this business had in FY25 have held up through FY26. So growth has not come at the compromise of RAROC. The book composition at A- and above has stayed at 91%, so we've not seen growth come at the cost of asset quality." — Puneet Sharma

**On technical slippages:**
> "In the first quarter when we reported technical impact, gross slippages were ₹2,700 crores, they're down to ₹1,240 crores. Net slippages were ₹1,861 crores, they're down to ₹218 crores. In percentage terms, the net slippages are now 0.07%. We continue to believe that there should not be an economic loss on this portfolio." — Puneet Sharma

**On AI (AXIOM):**
> "Through AXIOM, our bespoke AI operating model, we are building an AI-led, customer centric Bank that's transforming the customer touchpoints, employee productivity, and core processes at enterprise scale. We are the only ISO 42001 certified BFSI organisation globally." — Amitabh Chaudhry

**On deposit market:**
> "In the retail deposit market, banks reduced pricing by approximately 10–15 bps in response to the 25 bps cut. I don't see any further cuts happening. On the bulk deposit market, we see some softening as we transition into the new year, but it's a wait and watch — really the question is what happens to crude oil prices." — Neeraj Gambhir, ED

**On book mix target:**
> "We expect 70-30, 70% is retail and SME kind of business and 30% is wholesale, plus-minus 3% or 4%." — Amitabh Chaudhry (vs. current ~67% retail+CBG / 33% corporate)

**On ROE aspiration:**
> "The aspirational ROE was 18% at the bank level." — Puneet Sharma

**On credit filter:**
> "The growth that we have delivered on disbursement is without loosening our risk filters as on date. We've clearly been prudent and we don't expect to be loosening our risk filters on a go-forward basis." — Puneet Sharma

## Signals

- **West Asia tail risk** is the key FY27 macro watch variable for Axis — management stressed it 4+ times. At oil >$110 sustained, impact on retail slippage pool materialises. Buffer is pre-positioned.
- **Deposit market is tight** — sector loan growth outpacing deposit growth. Bulk deposit rates saw Q4 year-end spike. Retail deposit pricing cuts incomplete at 10–15 bps vs. 25 bps repo cut. No further retail rate cuts expected.
- **Retail disbursement acceleration is real, not Q4 seasonal:** Disbursement +24% YoY, +19% QoQ in Q4. Home loans +28% YoY, vehicle +25%, retail agri +34%, personal loans +22%. Portfolio book growth lagging disbursements due to wholesale dilution — will normalise.
- **NTB quality improvement:** New-to-Bank average balances +53% YoY, NTB Product Per Customer +24% YoY — quality of customer acquisition improving materially.
- **Technology spend:** 14% YoY growth, constitutes ~10% of total opex — consistent with AI/digital transformation intent.
- **Capital position comfortable:** Bank explicitly stated "we do not need equity capital for either growth or protection." AT1/Tier-2 may be issued opportunistically.

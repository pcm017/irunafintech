# ICICI Bank Q4-2026: Performance Review (Investor Presentation) — April 18, 2026

*Companion document to *2026 04 18 icici bank q4fy26 transcript* (earnings call). This presentation supplies the full standalone P&L, capital adequacy, and NPA-movement tables the transcript-only ingest flagged as missing — including the Gross NPA ratio, which the transcript ingest could not confirm.*

## TL;DR

- Confirms transcript figures exactly: standalone PAT ₹137.02 bn Q4FY26 (+8.5% YoY), NIM 4.32%, Net NPA 0.33%
- **New data point:** Gross NPA ratio 1.40% at Mar-26 (down from 1.53% Q3FY26, 1.67% Q4FY25) — resolves the "no GNPA cross-check" gap flagged in the transcript source page and entity page Contradictions section
- Segment PBT split: Wholesale (₹244.89 bn FY26) now marginally exceeds Retail (₹232.44 bn FY26) for the first time in the tracked period — Treasury PBT fell to ₹172.30 bn FY26 (from ₹187.61 bn FY25)
- Standalone capital adequacy strong: CET1/Tier I 16.35%, Total CAR 17.18% at Mar-26 (up sharply from 15.59%/14.71% at Dec-25 — reflects FY26 profit accretion + proposed dividend adjustment)
- Total standalone advances ₹15,538.93 bn (+15.8% YoY, +6.0% QoQ)

## Signals

**Gross NPA ratio confirmed at 1.40% — closes a data gap.** The transcript-only ingest for this quarter could not independently verify GNPA and flagged it as a contradiction risk. This presentation confirms a clean, continuously improving trend (1.67% → 1.53% → 1.40% across the last three reported quarters), removing that flag from the ICICI entity page.

**Wholesale PBT overtaking Retail PBT for FY26 (₹244.89 bn vs ₹232.44 bn) is a mix shift worth watching.** Historically retail has been ICICI's growth engine; the wholesale segment's Q4FY26 PBT of ₹70.37 bn (vs Retail's ₹69.27 bn) reflects both business banking (+24.4% YoY) and domestic corporate (+9.3% YoY) strength — likely a beneficiary of the same T-bill-vs-MCLR corporate repricing dynamic flagged at SBI and Bank of Baroda, where AAA/AA corporates returning to bank credit lines add wholesale volume.

**Treasury PBT nearly halved YoY (₹172.30 bn FY26 vs ₹187.61 bn FY25)** — consistent with a rate-cut cycle compressing bond gains after FY25's larger G-sec rally capture; treasury income actually went negative in both Q3 and Q4FY26 (₹-1.57 bn, ₹-1.06 bn), a genuine drag that the transcript's "range-bound NIM" framing doesn't fully capture on its own.

**Capital position strengthened materially in the quarter** — CET1 jumped 164 bps QoQ (14.71% → 16.35%), consistent with full-quarter profit accretion and no major capital consumption event; this gives ICICI ample headroom (CET1 minus regulatory minimum of ~11.5% including buffers ≈ 480+ bps) to sustain the credit growth guidance without a capital raise.

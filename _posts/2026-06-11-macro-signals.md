## TL;DR

- **US-Iran conflict re-escalates sharply (June 10):** US carried out "self-defense strikes" after a US helicopter was downed by Iran; Iran retaliated by attacking Bahrain, Jordan, and Kuwait — the conflict spreads beyond Iran-Israel into multi-front Gulf war. S&P 500 fell to **7,266.99 (▼1.62%)**, Nasdaq to **25,169.50 (▼1.98%)**.
- **Gold collapsed to $4,152/oz** — another leg down from $4,285 (June 10 prior close). Rate-hike fear (70% Dec Fed hike probability) + geopolitical volatility without haven bid = continued breakdown below 200-DMA. Expected range June 11: $4,157–$4,255.
- **India resilient but macro tightening:** Nifty only ▼0.12% to 23,214.95; INR strengthened to 94.96. But India G-Sec 10Y firming back to ~7.00% on Middle East tension — spread with US widens to ~247bps from prior 233bps lows.
- **Fed inflation target narrative:** "4% is the new 2%" — commentary circulating that the Fed has effectively abandoned its 2% CPI target; new effective anchor closer to 4%. Direct implication: higher-for-longer US rates, tighter India-US spread, and reduced RBI easing space.
- **Cross-asset correlations rising** — equities, commodities, and safe havens moving together (macro dominance over micro); India fuel consumption fell 6.5% in May (war impact on demand).

## Signals

- **US-Iran conflict now a multi-front Gulf war** — Iran attacking Bahrain, Jordan, Kuwait is a qualitative escalation. Gulf Arab state stability at risk. Oil supply disruption scenarios broaden beyond Hormuz alone. Each Gulf state that becomes directly involved raises risk premium further. India: GCC remittances (~$36B/year), oil supply corridors, and FII EM risk-off sentiment all at risk.
- **Gold's 200-DMA breakdown deepening ($4,285 → $4,152)** — each leg down flushes more trend-following longs. At $4,000, the cycle-low scenario becomes realistic. Counterweight: COT short reduction + fresh longs building (contrarian signal). The catalyst for a gold snap-back remains a definitive Iran deal.
- **India G-Sec reversal (7.00%) signals bond market re-pricing** — the brief period at 6.9% (tightest India-US spread at 233bps) was disrupted by June 10's escalation. If G-Sec stays at 7.00%+ as US 10Y also rises (4.53%), the India-US spread stabilises at 240–250bps — still supportive of RBI's capital inflow framework but less compelling than the 233bps trough.
- **INR 94.96 (strengthened)** — counterintuitive strength despite escalation, possibly driven by RBI intervention and positive India macro data. If Brent stays below $92, INR can hold the 94–96 range. Hormuz full closure would push INR back toward 96–98.
- **Fed 4% inflation target** — if confirmed, meaningfully delays Indian rate cut expectations (Goldman's 2-hike call becomes baseline); raises borrowing cost floor for Indian NBFCs and banks funded by capital markets.

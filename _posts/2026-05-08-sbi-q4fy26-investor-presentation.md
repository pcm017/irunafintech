## Signals

**NIM compression is real and full-year — the ">3% guidance met" story is narrower than it sounds.** Whole-bank NIM fell 17 bps YoY to 2.91%; domestic NIM fell 18 bps to 3.03%. The driver is asset-side yield compression (-48 bps on domestic advances yield) outrunning liability-side relief (-7 bps on cost of deposits) as EBLR-linked loans reprice faster than term deposits roll off. The sequential quarterly data shows this stabilizing (domestic NIM roughly flat Dec-25 to Mar-26: 3.06% → 3.03%), which supports the T-bill-to-MCLR repricing thesis both SBI and Bank of Baroda have flagged as the FY27 NIM recovery lever.

**Fee income growth (+14.17%) outpacing NII growth (+4.08%) is the quieter revenue story.** Loan processing charges surged 28.17% YoY — a direct read-through of the 16.87% credit growth converting into upfront fee capture, and a sign SBI is monetizing relationship banking more aggressively (echoing the retail/corporate processing-fee surge management called out in the transcript). Recovery from AUCA (written-off) accounts of ₹10,054 cr in FY26 is a meaningful non-recurring contributor to non-interest income that should not be extrapolated into FY27 run-rate.

**ROE fell 130 bps YoY (19.87% → 18.57%) even as absolute PAT hit a record — capital is growing faster than earnings.** Capital Adequacy rose 115 bps to 15.40% and CET-1 climbed from 10.81% to 12.29%, meaning SBI retained more capital than needed to fund 16.87% credit growth. This capital build supports the FY27 SBI AMC listing and NSE stake value-unlock plans flagged in the transcript, but it also means near-term ROE is structurally capped until that capital gets deployed or returned.

**The ₹29,713 cr non-NPA provisioning buffer (~158% of Net NPA) is the single most important number for the ECL transition story.** Combined with PCR of 74.36% (91.97% including AUCA), SBI's total credit-loss cushion is unusually deep for a bank this size — directly supporting the transcript's claim of a "smooth" ECL transition from April 2027.

# Fintech & BFSI Digest — 2026-05-21

## TL;DR

- **RBI: banks cannot disable mobile phones of defaulting borrowers** — consumer protection ruling limits recovery enforcement methods; significant for digital lenders
- **BIS double alert**: (1) Basel Committee ICT risk management report; (2) cryptoasset targeted review progressing; (3) South African Reserve Bank deputy governor speech on AI regulation in financial sector
- **FPIs too costly to trade India?** — experts flag higher tax and compliance burden; context of INR at record lows and FII outflows
- **Bank of America fund manager survey at extreme bullish** — contrarian sell signal for global equities; relevant to FII risk appetite for India
- **Equitas SFB eyes 18.5% loan growth**; **Niva Bupa targeting sub-100 combined ratio by FY29**
- AI in finance may **structurally shrink junior roles** — CFOs confirm headcount reduction at analyst/associate levels

## Signals

- **RBI mobile phone ruling = borrower protection signal:** In a digital-first lending environment, restricting the ability to disable devices is a meaningful constraint on lenders. This signals RBI's continued lean toward consumer protection even as it manages credit quality concerns. Digital lenders (especially BNPL, fintech NBFCs) need to review recovery playbooks.
- **FPI cost friction compounding with macro headwinds:** If India becomes "too costly to trade" on top of currency losses (-12.35% YoY on INR), the FII exit dynamic could accelerate. The domestic SIP floor (₹25,000+ Cr/month) is the circuit breaker — but a prolonged FII exodus drains reserves and pressures the rupee.
- **BofA contrarian sell signal:** Extreme bullishness in fund manager surveys has preceded corrections historically. Combined with the global bond rout and India's macro headwinds, the risk-reward for Indian equities appears asymmetric near-term.
- **Basel ICT + crypto review:** Regulators moving to tighten bank cyber risk management standards and prudential treatment of crypto. Both will create compliance cost increases for Indian banks with digital/crypto exposure.
- **AI junior role compression:** Finance functions (AP, reconciliation, audit prep) are being automated. India's large CA/CPA/MBA finance talent pool is directly exposed. The near-term labor market impact in BFSI is becoming quantifiable.
- **Gambling-as-investing concern:** The blurring line between retail investing apps and prediction markets is a regulatory risk. SEBI's track record of protecting retail investors suggests action is plausible if consumer harm data builds up.

## Key Data / Quotes

### FY27 Hard Targets (Q&A confirmation)
- **EBIT target FY27: 15%** (confirmed in response to analyst; up from 12.6% FY26)
- **ROCE target FY27: 30%** (confirmed; current: 26.9% Q4 FY26)
- Management characterised these as "hard targets, not aspirational" — this is a departure from typical IT company hedged guidance

### Commercial Model: Token Pricing
- **T&M vs. fixed-price margin gap**: ~8 percentage points differential — fixed-price/token pricing has structurally higher margins
- **Service token model**: Each delivery engagement is decomposed into human and digital labor sub-processes; each sub-process is priced per token (not per hour)
- Implications: TechM is building toward a model where AI handles more sub-processes at lower cost; the token price remains constant while the cost per token falls — expanding gross margins
- This is referred to internally as moving "beyond the billable hour"

### AI-Specific Metrics (Q4 FY26)
- **+7% revenue per employee YoY**: Confirmed as AI-only contribution (isolated measurement)
- **95% of peak/prime accounts**: Have active GenAI deployments
- **80% workforce AI-ready** (trained); **76% AI-certified** (tested)
- **350+ service agents** deployed across client engagements
- **2 proprietary LLMs** in production
- BPS: **200+ Orion agents** and **20,000+ bots** running on Orion platform

### Orion Platform
- Orchestrates multi-vendor agents (not just TechM-built agents)
- Live on 3 hyperscalers (AWS, Azure, GCP)
- BPS (Business Process Services) is the lead deployment division: 200+ agents, 20,000+ bots
- Orion is the "agent marketplace" — TechM's IP for multi-agent orchestration

### Vector Squads
- New delivery unit model replacing traditional team structures
- Purpose: rapid delivery of AI-embedded projects using specialised micro-teams
- Paired with Orion orchestration for end-to-end AI project delivery

### Anthropic / Claude Reference
- Mohit Joshi specifically mentioned "Anthropic Mythos platform" in the context of rapid AI model capability releases
- An analyst asked whether the pace of model releases (e.g., new Claude versions) was causing clients to delay decisions pending clarity on which model to standardise
- Mohit's response: "minimal impact on decision-making; clients are taking a pragmatic view" — they proceed with available models and iterate
- Inference: TechM's Orion is designed to be model-agnostic to precisely avoid client lock-in to specific model vendors

### Industry Growth Estimate
- TechM management estimated industry revenue growth at **2–4% or 3–5%** (two bands given with uncertainty)
- This is meaningfully below pre-2024 IT growth norms — confirms secular growth deceleration in traditional IT services

### Analyst Concerns (Q&A topics surfaced)
- Will GCCs (Global Capability Centres) disintermediate TechM? Management: GCCs are partners not competitors for TechM's AI services
- Is the 15% EBIT target achievable on 2–4% revenue growth? Management: fixed-price shift + AI delivery efficiency is the mechanism
- Can T&M contracts be repriced to token model? Management: renewals are the primary opportunity; new contracts default to token pricing

## Signals

1. **Token pricing model is TechM's most defensible innovation**: No other India IT major has articulated a per-token commercial model at this level of specificity. If adopted at scale, this structurally changes IT services economics — from revenue-per-head to revenue-per-outcome.

2. **Anthropic's model release cadence is a real industry variable**: The fact that an analyst asked — and Mohit felt compelled to address — Claude/Anthropic model release frequency as a client uncertainty driver confirms that frontier AI model velocity is now a planning input for IT services buyers.

3. **Model-agnostic orchestration (Orion) is the right hedge**: TechM's investment in a multi-vendor agent orchestration layer is the correct response to rapid model evolution. Lock-in to any single LLM (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google) would be catastrophic as model capabilities shift rapidly.

4. **BPS is TechM's AI-first division**: 200+ Orion agents and 20,000+ bots in BPS confirms that back-office/process automation is further along than software development AI. This mirrors what we see at Bajaj Finance (AI in credit workflow automation before AI in software delivery).

5. **ROCE 30% target implies capital discipline is structural**: A 30% ROCE target from 26.9% (FY26) alongside 15% EBIT confirms TechM is not pursuing capex-heavy growth. AI delivery is capex-light by design — the productivity gains come from model fine-tuning and agent deployment, not hardware.

The Q&A does not resolve whether this implies equivalent headcount reduction or revenue stagnation. This is an unresolved contradiction — see *tech mahindra* Contradictions section.

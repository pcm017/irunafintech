## Signals

- **Aschenbrenner semiconductor short = major contrarian signal:** A credentialed AI insider flipping 62% short on the chip complex is the most significant bearish signal on the AI infrastructure trade since the cycle began. Potential thesis: AI compute demand may be peaking (or the near-term ordering cycle is maturing), and overinvestment risk is real. Watch Nvidia earnings for confirmation/denial.
- **Google I/O: distribution > capability:** Google's agent announcements are about embedding AI into products used by 330M+ businesses (Workspace). This is a fundamentally different strategy from OpenAI's new-product approach — slower but compounds via existing distribution. Implications for Anthropic: enterprise deals with Google customers become harder if Workspace AI is "good enough."
- **Anthropic $300M acquisition:** Acquiring a developer tools company suggests Anthropic is building out the developer experience layer — making Claude easier to integrate into workflows. This is MCP + API + tooling strategy, not just model development. Consistent with Karpathy joining for pre-training (capability) + tooling acquisition (distribution).
- **India electronics vulnerability:** China export curbs on semiconductors/components are a direct threat to India's "Make in India" electronics and mobile manufacturing ambitions. Companies like Dixon Technologies, Tata Electronics, and Foxconn India facilities are exposed to Chinese component supply chains.
- **AI-native search (Exa) as infrastructure layer:** As AI agents increasingly need to retrieve information from the web, a purpose-built AI search infrastructure becomes critical. Exa's a16z backing signals the VC community sees this as a necessary infrastructure layer, distinct from consumer search.
- **PhaaS and enterprise dark data:** AI-powered phishing-as-a-service lowers the cost of targeted attacks dramatically. For India's large BFSI sector (UPI, digital banking), this is an operational risk that scales with AI capability improvements.

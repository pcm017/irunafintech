## TL;DR

- Net profit ₹2,020 Cr (+26.84% YoY); operating profit ₹3,117 Cr (+21.29% YoY); NII +14.53% YoY — one of the strongest YoY profit growth rates among PSU banks logged in this wiki.
- Total business ₹6,50,457 Cr (+19.10% YoY) — well above system-average credit growth (17.7% YoY per the 2026-07-14 macro-signals snapshot); global advances ₹3,05,964 Cr (+26.90% YoY).
- Asset quality improved: GNPA 1.45% (down from 1.74% YoY), NNPA 0.13% (down from 0.18% YoY), PCR 98.55% (up from 98.36% YoY).
- RoA improved to 1.90% (from 1.80% YoY) — among the higher PSU bank RoA readings tracked in this wiki.
- CASA ratio declined to 49% (from 53% at Q4FY26, 50% a year ago) even as term deposits grew +16.62% YoY — a shift toward costlier funding mix worth watching against the NIM trend.

## Signals

Bank of Maharashtra's Q1 FY27 print is one of the stronger PSU bank results logged in this wiki: net profit growth of nearly 27% YoY and total business growth of 19.1% YoY both run meaningfully ahead of system-wide bank credit growth (17.7% YoY, per today's 2026-07-14 macro-signals snapshot), while asset quality continues to improve (GNPA 1.45%, PCR 98.55%). The CASA ratio decline (53% to 49% QoQ) alongside strong term-deposit growth (+16.62% YoY) is the one mix-shift worth tracking — a move toward costlier funding that could pressure NIM in coming quarters if it continues, even though the bank's RoA improved this quarter.

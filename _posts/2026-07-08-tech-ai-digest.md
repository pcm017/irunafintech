## TL;DR

- SemiAnalysis projects cumulative AI-infrastructure debt financing could exceed $7 trillion by 2029, with NVIDIA positioned as an implicit backstop across capital, GPU offtake agreements, and datacenter financing — a systemic-risk framing for the AI buildout, distinct from prior single-company debt stories (e.g. Apollo's $35B Anthropic compute financing).
- Microsoft is cutting close to 5,000 jobs, reported alongside a competing smart-glasses startup reaching unicorn status.
- Anthropic's internal interpretability research ("what Anthropic found hiding inside Claude's mind") is circulating in tech press — snippet-only, no technical detail captured this cycle.
- Qualcomm reaffirmed its $22B non-handset FY29 revenue target and said custom-ASIC shipments for hyperscale customers have been pulled forward to begin within calendar 2026 (earlier than previously guided), even as NVIDIA's Computex PC-chip entry pressures its stock.
- India-UK FTA (CETA) is reported taking effect July 15, 2026 (ETGovernment), with ETCFO separately framing it as an export-boost opportunity for Indian CFOs — two independent sources on the same near-term date, not yet logged as a Modi action.

## Signals

The SemiAnalysis $7T AI-debt-by-2029 projection is the standout new data point this cycle — it reframes the AI-infrastructure financing story from individual deals (Anthropic's $35B Apollo facility, Meta-Samsung's ~$7.2Bn chip order) into a systemic-risk thesis with NVIDIA as the implicit backstop across capital, offtake, and datacenter financing simultaneously. This is worth tracking as a macro tail-risk alongside the wiki's existing AI-capex-ROI-scrutiny thread (the Jun 23 Nasdaq selloff, Bloomberg's "AI wake-up call"). Microsoft's ~5,000 job cuts is a new big-tech-strategy data point, though not yet connected to a specific AI-capex-reallocation narrative in available reporting. The India-UK FTA effective date (July 15) is corroborated by two independent sources today and has a concrete near-term date — this is added to Modi's action log as a new Economic-category entry rather than left as an unlogged newsletter item, given the pattern of prior FTA entries (India-Oman CEPA, India-UAE CEPA) already tracked there. The Sanand inauguration date conflict (Jul 4 vs. the already-logged Jul 6) is noted but not acted on — the existing modi.md entry is left as-is pending a higher-confidence source, since one snippet-only newsletter mention is not sufficient grounds to overwrite a previously-logged date.

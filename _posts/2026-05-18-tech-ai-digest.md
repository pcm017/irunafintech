## Key data / quotes

### AI Models
- **Opus 4.7 winning people back** (Every.to, May 17): *"Opus 4.7 is winning people back"* — Claude regaining user preference. Signals Anthropic has addressed gap vs. competing models, likely GPT-4o/Gemini Ultra/Llama 4.
- **Inside Anthropic's Claude development** (Alex Albert interview, Product Compass): rare look at how Anthropic picks model capabilities, trains Claude's "character" (values, tone, personality), and ships better models. Primary-source material on Anthropic's model development philosophy.
- **Token Economy pt2 — Intelligence Companies** (Sy Taylor): *"Some companies are rebuilding themselves around AI. Everyone else is waiting for a lab, vendor, owner, or competitor to do it to them."* — The divergence between AI-native rebuilders vs. passive waiters is now the defining enterprise split.

### Big Tech / AI Infrastructure
- **Cerebras IPO at $95B** (Chamath, May 17): Cerebras makes wafer-scale AI inference/training chips; competes with NVIDIA. $95B IPO valuation signals continued premium for AI infrastructure at IPO stage. Sets benchmark for private AI infra company valuations (Groq, SambaNova, etc.).
- **Notion Workers** (Department of Product): Notion launches AI agents ("Workers") that can build Stripe dashboards and execute cross-app tasks. Notion shifting from note-taking to agentic workflow automation — the "productivity app → automation platform" transition.
- **"Claude Backlash brewing?"** (Department of Product): Possible user sentiment shift against Claude. Cause unclear — may relate to pricing, capability changes, or competitor feature parity.
- **Companies ditching AI features** (Department of Product): Counter-narrative piece — some companies walking back AI feature investments. Execution gap between AI feature launches and actual adoption/retention.

### AI Hardware
- **AI hardware boom: beginning** (Caitlin Kalinowski, Lenny's Podcast): *"We're at the beginning of the AI hardware boom."* Key insights: (1) Supply chain vulnerabilities threaten the robotics revolution — geopolitical chip dependency; (2) Consumer VR failed because of weight/comfort/price/content — same failure modes risk repeating for consumer AI hardware (glasses, headsets); (3) Building at Apple/Meta/OpenAI's scale requires 5–7 year hardware development cycles — not compatible with AI's 6-month iteration cycles.

### Viewpoints / Structural Shifts
- **"Infrastructure to imagination"** (Token Dispatch, May 17): *"The bottleneck in building has shifted from infrastructure to imagination."* AI infrastructure (compute, models, APIs) now abundant and cheap; the constraint is now domain insight, use-case clarity, and imagination. Commoditisation of AI infrastructure accelerating.
- **Agent-Led Growth replacing PLG** (Ramli John, May 17): PLG (product-led growth) relied on self-serve trials and usage-based conversion. Agent-led growth (ALG) replaces human-driven product exploration with AI agents that act on behalf of users — removing the "click tax." Five principles for ALG: (1) agents as users; (2) outcome-based activation; (3) value realization before payment; (4) trust as the conversion mechanic; (5) human escalation paths.
- **AI Anxiety at every layer of tech** (The VC Corner): AI disruption anxiety now permeates infrastructure, product, management, VC — not just developer roles. "AI second brain" growing as a category (cognitive extension/memory tools). Climate tech rebounding — data center energy demand creating co-investment opportunities.

### India Tech
- **Rapido $240M / $3B** — India urban mobility round; Prosus-led. AI/ML relevance: routing, pricing, driver allocation, demand forecasting.
- **India platform stress signals** (The Morning Context): Swiggy dark store pressures, Air India operational issues, Meesho "reality check," Paytm lending pressures — consumer tech platform headwinds in India.

## Signals

1. **Opus 4.7's comeback** signals the AI model competitive cycle is not over — users are sensitive to relative capability, and model choice is still fluid. For Indian enterprises building on AI APIs: model provider diversification vs. deep single-provider integration is an active strategic question.

2. **Notion Workers = vertical agent expansion.** Notion has ~35M users and is moving from a document tool to an agentic OS for knowledge work. Competitors: Salesforce Agentforce, Microsoft Copilot, Atlassian Rovo. If Notion can orchestrate cross-app actions (Stripe, Jira, Slack) from a single interface, it becomes the ambient enterprise agent layer — not just a notes app.

3. **Cerebras IPO at $95B** validates AI infrastructure premium in public markets. Implications: (a) AI chip companies have public market comparables above NVIDIA's revenue multiple; (b) India-equivalent HrdWyr ($13M Series A) is at a very early stage of what could be a large AI hardware market; (c) DRAM/NAND price inflation is rational given infrastructure demand.

4. **"Infrastructure to imagination" shift** has direct India fintech applications. The constraint in India fintech is no longer model access or compute — it's knowing what to build and for whom. Companies with deep domain data (Bajaj Finance on 93M customers, HDFC Bank on mortgage + banking cross-sell, Paytm on merchant GMV) have the imagination inputs that pure-AI companies lack.

5. **Agent-led growth is the PLG successor for SaaS.** For Indian B2B SaaS companies (Zoho, Freshworks, Chargebee, LeadSquared), the question is not "when do we add AI features?" but "when do we rebuild onboarding and activation as agentic experiences?" First movers in ALG will have structurally lower CAC and higher NRR.

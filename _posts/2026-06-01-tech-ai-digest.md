## Key data / quotes

- **Anthropic:** $65B raised at $965B valuation — surpasses OpenAI. Capital concentration at frontier AI accelerating.
- **Benedict Evans (via Lenny's):** "We are in the 1997 moment of AI — most of the economic impact hasn't happened yet." Value accrues to those combining AI with domain expertise and distribution, not necessarily model makers.
- **Taiwan DRAM (Asianometry):** Taiwan's strength is in logic semiconductors only. DRAM is dominated by Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron — different industry economics, different failure modes. India's semiconductor ambitions should distinguish between logic and memory pathways.
- **AI agent broker evolution:** "Your next broker is a chatbot" — AI agents moving from advisory to execution in financial services (Robinhood). Catena Labs pursuing OCC bank charter for AI agents.
- **Agentic coding workflow shift:** PMs now "review artifacts (AI coding outputs) not code" — Claude Code, Cursor, Devin enabling non-engineers to participate in software development cycles. Plan with /build, adversarial review, iterative fixing.
- **India VC lowest 2026:** VC inflow to India drops to lowest of year; AI infrastructure cost curve is higher than enterprise buyers expected.
- **AI slot machine psychology:** Ruben Hassid — intermittent reinforcement loops make AI tools addictive and hard to evaluate objectively. Contrarian to productivity optimism.
- **Grey Swan:** India "hosting the machine" — executed for others in 1990s IT wave; same inflection point now with AI — will India own the design logic or again execute for others?

---

## Signals

- **$1T AI company threshold** — Anthropic's $965B valuation signals the frontier AI era has entered a new capital intensity regime. IPO pipeline (Anthropic, OpenAI, SpaceX) will be defining market events in H2 2026.
- **"1997 internet moment" framing** — most economic disruption from AI is ahead, not behind us. For India, the question is positioning (design vs. execution layer) ahead of this disruption.
- **Taiwan DRAM lesson for India:** India Semiconductor Mission should calibrate ambitions clearly between logic (harder, TSMC-dominated) and memory (different economics); India's ATMP (packaging) strategy is more realistic near-term than full-scale fab.
- **AI-native PM/engineering workflow** is solidifying — companies that train product and engineering teams on agentic development will have structural velocity advantage.

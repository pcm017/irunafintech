## Signals

**Change detection:** Brent's single-session jump from ~$73 to $79.25 (+8.7%) is the sharpest commodity move of the week, driven by Iran's missile strikes on three Gulf Arab states rather than a market-positioning shift.

**Root cause traversal:** Iran's retaliation directly followed Trump's Jul 8 declaration that the ceasefire is "over," confirming a live military exchange rather than a diplomatic war of words — the same GCC-remittance-corridor risk (~$36B/year from Bahrain/Kuwait/Qatar-based Indian workers) that was active during May-June's multi-front escalation is back in play.

**Sizing / impact:** If Brent sustains above $78-80 into the August RBI MPC, it directly threatens the rate-cut thesis built on India CPI staying below the 4% target plus cheap crude. Bank credit growth at a 2-year high (17.65-17.7%) is a countervailing domestic-strength signal, but the conflicting forex-reserve reads (a >$150Bn discrepancy) undermine confidence in near-term RBI intervention-capacity assessments until reconciled.

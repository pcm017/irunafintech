- Net profit Q4 ₹5,616 cr (+11.2% YoY) and FY26 ₹20,021 cr — both highest ever; ROA >1% for 15 consecutive quarters; NIM improved sequentially to 2.89% (+10 bps QoQ)

## Signals

**PSU bank de-rating on credit cost is a head-fake in Q4.** The headline credit cost of 0.76% screams deterioration vs 0.44% last year — but the ₹1,500 cr floating provision is a voluntary ECL transition buffer, not slippage-driven. Underlying credit cost was 0.32%. SMA1+2 at 0.18% (from 0.33%) and collection efficiency at 98.9% tell the real story: BoB's asset quality is the best it's been in a decade.

**NIM defensibility is BoB's strategic tension.** Deposits are sticky at 4.78%, and management sees no downward repricing near-term (geopolitical-driven liquidity tightness). The NIM lever is now on the asset side: repricing T-bill-linked corporate loans back to MCLR as short-term rates normalize. NIM guidance 2.75–2.95% for FY27 is conservative and achievable — the lower end accounts for volatile IT refund income.

**Guidance upgrade from 11–13% to 12–14% loan growth** signals management confidence despite West Asia uncertainty. BoB is a large international franchise (24.4% international advance growth) — which also means crude/trade route exposure is higher than peers.

**ECL transition signal: ~18 bps CET1 impact** (management confirmed aligned to earlier estimate). BoB CET1 is 13.16% — comfortably above minimum but the gap to the 15–16% range of private peers is a persistent PSU bank discount. The capital raise plan (₹8,500 cr equity + ₹6,000 cr AT-1/Tier 2) is necessary to fund 12–14% loan growth without CET1 erosion post-ECL.

**India's PSU banks are delivering consistently — but at lower multiples than private peers.** BoB's ROE of 15.39% is respectable for a PSU, but ROE is capped by capital intensity and public ownership structure. The 93.94% PCR (including technical write-offs) means future slippage recovery potential is a positive optionality.

## TL;DR

- OnEMI (brand: **Kissht**) listed on NSE & BSE on May 8, 2026 — this is its first quarterly earnings as a public company. IPO raised ₹850 Cr fresh issue; listed at ₹191 vs ₹162–171 price band (+11.7% premium, 9.9x subscribed).
- AUM crossed ₹7,066 Cr (+73% YoY), PAT ₹281 Cr for FY26 (+75% YoY). ROA 5.0%, ROE 24% — strong return ratios for a digital NBFC at this scale.
- GNPA improved 77 bps YoY to 2.12% — one of the cleaner asset quality turnarounds in the digital lending cohort post FY25 overleverage stress.
- Half the AUM (49.7%) is off-book (co-lending/DA partners) — asset-light model reduces capital intensity. On-book cost of borrowings: 14.16% (A-/Stable rated).

## Signals

- **Digital lending survivors are emerging with clean books.** Kissht's GNPA improvement from 2.89% to 2.12% YoY, alongside rising Stage 1 share (93.6% → 95.5%), suggests that disciplined underwriting through the FY25 stress cycle creates durable differentiation. AI/ML models with 7,000+ variables appear to be delivering real alpha.
- **IPO premium and QIB oversubscription (25.9x) signals institutional confidence** in digital NBFC model at this scale. First-quarter results as a listed company will be scrutinised for PAT trajectory — Q4 FY26 PAT ₹82 Cr slightly above Q3's ₹77 Cr (+6.6%), but PPOP slightly lower (−0.6% QoQ) — marginal opex uptick worth watching.
- **Off-book shift (39.4% → 49.7%):** Increasingly asset-light model suggests capital efficiency optimisation. But reduces balance sheet growth per unit of AUM growth — PAT/AUM yield should be tracked.
- **LAP ramp (1.8% → 7.3% of AUM in one year):** Secured product diversification at lower risk — bullish on tenure extension and margin resilience. 98 LAP branches across 8 states.
- **Cost of borrowings (14.16%) is high** for an A-rated NBFC — room to compress as liability profile matures post-IPO. Management guidance notes: "As AUM scales, cost of borrowings and opex as % of AUM are expected to reduce."
- **Sachin Tendulkar as brand ambassador** — distinctive retail distribution signal for mass-market brand. Relevant given 81% of customers from top 100 cities, 73% under age 35.

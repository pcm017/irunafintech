## Signals

- **Brent at $83.82 is a structural macro shift for India.** From the May peak of ~$111, Brent has declined ~$27/bbl (-24%). At $83.82, India's monthly import bill savings vs. peak = ~$6B. If Hormuz fully reopens post-June 19 signing and Brent tests $75–80, savings widen to $8–10B/month. This is the most powerful India macro relief channel of 2026.
- **WPI-CPI divergence is a new risk.** WPI at 9.68% (fuel-led) vs. CPI at 3.9% signals that producer-side cost pressure is building even as consumer inflation stays muted. If crude stays elevated (deal delays), WPI → CPI transmission resumes through manufacturing and transport costs. With US-Iran deal confirmed, the base case is WPI easing over 60-90 days as fuel costs roll off.
- **Gold's COT divergence from crude positioning** (crude longs at 6-month high vs. gold longs at 11-month low) captures the regime shift: Iran deal = crude supply reopening trade (long crude for reopening) but **also** removes the geopolitical safe-haven bid for gold. Gold at $4,309 is recovering but faces structural headwinds (US rate expectations, systematic selling) without a new geopolitical shock.
- **India VIX at 17.02 (+7.85%)** is the counterintuitive signal of this snapshot. Equities rallied (+0.98% Nifty) but VIX spiked — suggesting options markets are pricing tail risk around the June 19 signing (treaty ratification uncertainty, nuclear verification clause, Hormuz reopening timeline slippage). This is normal "buy the rumour, hedge the event" behaviour ahead of a high-stakes signing ceremony.

# Xi Jinping — President of China / General Secretary of the CCP

**One-liner:** Xi Jinping is the President of the People's Republic of China and General Secretary of the Chinese Communist Party — the supreme decision-maker on China's foreign policy, military strategy, and major economic direction.

## Patterns

- **Dual-track diplomacy:** Simultaneously deepening economic engagement with the US (trade framework, Boeing deals) while maintaining hard lines on Taiwan and strategic autonomy.
- **Russia relationship as anchor:** The Putin-Xi summit immediately following the Trump-Xi summit signals Beijing uses the Moscow relationship as a baseline — the depth of Russia-China ties provides Xi leverage and a fallback regardless of how US-China relations evolve.
- **Pakistan alignment vs India:** Consistent CCP support for Pakistan at UNSC and diplomatically — a structural feature, not episodic.
- **Rare earth as leverage:** Xi using China's rare earth supply dominance as a negotiating chip in US deal-making — China controls >80% of global rare earth processing.
- **15th Five-Year Plan = stimulus cycle:** Economic priority is domestic consumption and technology self-reliance; expect continued targeted PBoC/fiscal support through 2026–2030.

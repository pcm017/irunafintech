# Narendra Modi — Prime Minister of India

**One-liner:** Narendra Modi is the Prime Minister of India (since 2014), currently serving his third term, managing the aftermath of Operation Sindoor while pursuing economic reform and broadening India's diplomatic footprint.

## Patterns

- **Military nationalism as political capital:** Operation Sindoor demonstrated willingness to use cross-border military force; BJP leveraged it domestically in West Bengal election cycle.
- **Bilateral-first diplomacy:** Consistently rejects multilateral mediation (US, UN) in disputes with Pakistan; prefers direct channels.
- **Reform in political windows:** Major reforms (energy pricing, trade liberalisation) timed to periods of political strength — West Bengal win provides next window.
- **Strategic autonomy under pressure:** India-US trade deal came with strings (stop Russian oil purchases); Modi navigating between US economic pull and Russia energy discount.
- **Narrative-led economic management:** During crises (Hormuz shock), uses Swadeshi framing and public austerity calls to shape corporate and consumer behaviour — a cost-free demand management tool that supplements RBI's forex interventions.
